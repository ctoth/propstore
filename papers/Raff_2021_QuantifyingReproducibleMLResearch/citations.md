## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Gundersen & Kjensmo 2018** — State of the art survey on AI reproducibility; provides context for the features chosen in this study and qualitative factors that this paper quantifies.
- **Pineau et al. 2020** — ML reproducibility checklist; complementary approach to improving reproducibility through standardized reporting rather than predictive modeling.
- **Henderson et al. 2018** — Deep RL reproducibility issues; provides domain-specific evidence that complements this paper's cross-domain analysis.
- **Bouthillier et al. 2019** — "Unreproducible Research is Reproducible" challenges definitions and may provide nuance to the binary outcome used here.
- **Lipton & Steinhardt 2019** — "Troubling Trends in ML Scholarship" discusses qualitative aspects of ML research quality that may interact with the quantitative features studied here.
