## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Open Science Collaboration 2015 (ref 1):** The RPP study providing the primary ground truth dataset of 100 manually replicated psychology studies. Essential for understanding the training data.
- **Camerer et al. 2018 (ref 12):** SSRP study replicating 21 social science experiments from Nature/Science. Provides Test II out-of-sample dataset and represents the gold standard for high-impact replication.
- **Dreber et al. 2015 (ref 8):** Prediction market approach to replication estimation. The primary comparator method showing markets achieve ~0.71 accuracy. Important for understanding the benchmark the ML model must beat.
- **Mikolov et al. 2013 (ref 31, 32):** Word2vec papers providing the core feature extraction method. Essential for understanding the narrative model's representation learning.
- **Altmejd et al. 2019 (ref 10):** Combines prediction markets and surveys for replication prediction. Another key comparator method and potential source of complementary features.
