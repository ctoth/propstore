This paper trains a machine learning model combining word2vec narrative features and reported statistics to predict whether published studies will replicate, achieving 0.65-0.78 accuracy across out-of-sample datasets from psychology, economics, and high-impact journals.
The key finding is that text-based narrative features outperform reported statistics for predicting replication, and n-gram word combinations are the discriminative signal rather than individual bias or novelty words.
The model's replication probability estimates could inform evidence quality assessment and argumentation preference orderings in systems that reason over empirical claims.
