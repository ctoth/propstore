Nosek and Errington propose that replication should be defined as any study for which any outcome would be diagnostic evidence about a prior claim, replacing the common but incorrect definition based on procedural repetition. They introduce a generalizability space framework (Units, Treatments, Outcomes, Settings) showing how replication and generalizability testing converge as theory matures. Relevant to propstore's modeling of inter-study evidence relationships and the conditions under which one study's findings bear on another's claims.
