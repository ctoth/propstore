# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The ASPIC+ framework is intermediate in abstraction between Dung's argumentation framework and concrete instantiating logics. This paper generalises ASPIC+ to accommodate classical logic instantiations, and adopts a new proposal for evaluating extensions: attacks are not defeats until deciding conflict-free sets, while defeats are exclusively used to determine the acceptability of arguments. Key properties and rationality postulates are then shown to hold for the new framework.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper isolates two different jobs that earlier preference-based ASPIC+ accounts had blurred together: representing logical incompatibility between arguments, and deciding which incompatibilities survive preference comparison. That distinction matters for propstore because it supports keeping the attack graph faithful to the underlying reasoning while applying preferences only when computing justified acceptance.
