## Key Citations for Follow-up

- Dung (1995): the paper's attack/defense semantics and complete-extension terminology are inherited directly from Dung's abstract framework.
- Prakken (2010): this is the immediate ASPIC+ predecessor whose defeat-based handling of conflict-freeness is being revised here.
- Amgoud and Vesic (2010): provides the semantics-level preference alternative that this paper argues against in the conclusion.
- Amgoud and Besnard (2009): motivates the need for an abstract framework that can still accommodate classical-logic-based argumentation.
- Modgil (2009): relevant for the unrestricted-rebut discussion and the paper's caution about asymmetric rebut attacks.
