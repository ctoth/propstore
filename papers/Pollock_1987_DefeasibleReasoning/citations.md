## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Doyle (1979)** - The original Truth Maintenance System that OSCAR contrasts with; Pollock notes Doyle's system stores all arguments (creating memory burden), while OSCAR stores only immediate bases. Already in collection.
- **Reiter (1980)** - Default logic, the main AI formalism for nonmonotonic reasoning; Pollock argues his approach from epistemology provides a richer structure than Reiter's defaults. Already in collection.
- **Pollock (1986)** - The full epistemological theory underlying this paper; contains detailed arguments for the rational architecture, the role of reasons vs. beliefs, and the relationship between warrant and justified belief.
- **Harman (1986)** - *Change in View* presents a competing philosophical account of belief revision; Pollock responds to Harman's observations about the interest-sensitivity of reasoning (we don't infer everything we can).
- **McCarthy (1980)** - Circumscription as another form of nonmonotonic reasoning; contrasts with Pollock's approach which is grounded in epistemological analysis of reasons rather than in model-theoretic minimization.
