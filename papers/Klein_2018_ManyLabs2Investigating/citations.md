## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Klein et al. (2014)** — Many Labs 1: the predecessor project with 13 effects, establishing the methodology used in ML2. Important for comparing replication rates across the two projects.
- **Open Science Collaboration (2015)** — The Reproducibility Project: Psychology. The other landmark replication project (100 studies), complementary to ML2 in using different methodology (single-site vs. multi-site). Important for triangulating base rates.
- **Camerer et al. (2016, 2018)** — Economics and social science replication projects. Extends the replication crisis literature beyond psychology.
- **Henrich, Heine, & Norenzayan (2010)** — "The weirdest people in the world." The foundational paper on WEIRD bias in psychological research, which ML2 directly tests by comparing WEIRD and non-WEIRD samples.
- **Van Bavel et al. (2016)** — Contextual sensitivity in reproducibility. Proposes that some effects are inherently more context-dependent than others, which ML2's heterogeneity analyses directly evaluate.
