## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[1] Dung 1995** — The foundational framework that this paper extends. Essential background for understanding all definitions and properties in the BAF paper.
- **[11] Amgoud, Cayrol, Lagasquie-Schiex 2004** — Prior/companion work by the same authors on bipolarity in argumentation frameworks, likely contains additional motivation and preliminary results.
- **[5] Karacapilidis & Papadias 2001** — Provides practical context for support relations in argumentation systems (Hermes system).
- **[7] Verheij 2003** — Addresses existence and multiplicity of extensions in dialectical argumentation, relevant to understanding extension computation.
- **[8] Prakken & Vreeswijk 2002** — Comprehensive survey of logics for defeasible argumentation, provides broader context for structured argumentation.
