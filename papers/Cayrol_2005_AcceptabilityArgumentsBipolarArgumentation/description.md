Extends Dung's abstract argumentation framework with an independent support relation alongside defeat, defining bipolar argumentation frameworks (BAFs) with new types of interaction (supported defeat, indirect defeat). Introduces three levels of admissibility semantics (d-admissible, s-admissible, c-admissible) that enforce internal and external coherence, generalizing stable and preferred extensions. Directly relevant for implementing argumentation systems that model both conflict and evidential support between claims.
