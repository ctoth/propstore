## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[306] Kleinberg 1999** — Authoritative sources in hyperlinked environments. The authority computation method used for identifying authoritative papers in the citation network.
- **[316] Bikhchandani et al. 1992** — Information cascade theory. Provides the theoretical framework explaining why amplification works — people rationally follow the prevailing view over private information.
- **[318] Tatsioni et al. 2007** — Persistence of contradicted claims. Complementary study showing that even after claims are contradicted, they persist in the literature. Directly relevant to propstore's claim lifecycle tracking.
- **[312] Gilbert 1977** — Referencing as persuasion. Early work on the social (non-scholarly) function of citation, foundational to the "social citation" concept in this paper.
