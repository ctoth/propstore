Compares the expressive power of Defeasible Logic and sceptical Logic Programming without Negation as Failure (LPwNF) under a uniform translation, providing the canonical proof-tag inference system (`±Δ` for definite, `±∂` for defeasible) that virtually all subsequent defeasible-logic literature reuses verbatim. Proves Theorem 4.1 (every sceptical LPwNF conclusion is a defeasible DL conclusion) and gives a strict-strength counterexample (Example 3, platypus) showing DL captures *teams of rules* with the same head while LPwNF cannot, and further shows DL captures Grosof's courteous logic programs but diverges from Wang/You/Yuan priority logic which propagates ambiguity. Directly grounds propstore's `propstore.defeasibility` machinery: the `±Δ / ±∂` tags, acyclic superiority relation, strict/defeasible/defeater trichotomy, and team-trumping clause are the formal heart of the CKR-style justifiable-exception layer that decides `ist(c, p)` applicability and injects exception-derived defeats at the ASPIC+ boundary.
