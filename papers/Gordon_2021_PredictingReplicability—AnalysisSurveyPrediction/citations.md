## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Camerer et al. 2018 (ref 11)** — SSRP: the largest and most prominent replication project for Nature/Science papers. Directly provides ground truth data this paper analyzes.
- **Benjamin et al. 2018 (ref 30)** — "Redefine statistical significance" proposes p < 0.005 threshold, which this paper validates empirically as a strong predictor of replicability.
- **Yang, Youyou & Uzzi 2020 (ref 33)** — Uses AI + human forecasting to estimate "deep replicability" — extends the forecasting approach with machine learning.
- **Altmejd et al. 2019 (ref 34)** — Directly related predecessor paper (includes Forsell as coauthor) on predicting replicability of social science experiments.
- **Pawel & Held 2020 (ref 40)** — Probabilistic forecasting of replication studies using Bayesian methods — complements the market/survey approach.
