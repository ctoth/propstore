This paper pools data from four large-scale replication forecasting projects (RPP, EERP, ML2, SSRP) covering 103 studies, finding that prediction markets correctly classify replication outcomes 73% of the time and surveys 71%. Original p-values strongly predict replicability, with studies having p < 0.005 replicating at 76% vs 18% for weaker evidence. Relevant to propstore's calibration and opinion layers as it provides empirical base rates for how reliably published findings replicate and how well crowd-sourced beliefs track ground truth.
