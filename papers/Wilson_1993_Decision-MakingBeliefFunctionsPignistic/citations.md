## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Smets 1989, 1990; Smets and Kennes 1989** — direct rival framework (pignistic + TBM); essential to fully understand the contrast Wilson is drawing and to ground propstore's TBM-style ATMS bundling decisions.
- **Dempster 1967** — origin of the envelope characterisation Bel(X)=inf{P(X) : P∈P} and the extremal-point construction Wilson reuses in Proposition 4.4.
- **Shafer 1976** — Mathematical Theory of Evidence; canonical Dempster-Shafer reference; already in the propstore collection.
- **Walley 1991** — Statistical Reasoning with Imprecise Probabilities; the behavioural lower-prevision foundation Wilson appeals to against Bayesianism; also already in the collection.
- **Wilson 1992a "How Much Do You Believe?", IJAR 6(3): 345-366** — companion paper explicitly arguing the [Bel, Pl] interval semantics; the closer match to the "envelope semantics" theme Q described in the dispatch (worth retrieving as a follow-up paper).
- **Wilson 1992b PhD thesis** — full proofs deferred to here.
