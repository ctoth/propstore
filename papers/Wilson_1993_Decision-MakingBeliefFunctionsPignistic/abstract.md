# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The paper discusses the different approaches to decision-making using Belief Functions. In particular, I describe Philippe Smets' method of decision-making, which transforms a Belief Function into a single probability function, the pignistic probability function. This transformation is sensitive to the choice of frame of discernment, which is often, to a large extent, arbitrary. It thus seems natural to consider all refinements of a frame of discernment and their associated pignistic probability functions and decisions. The main result of the paper is that this is equivalent to the standard approach.

---

## Our Interpretation

Wilson's central technical claim is that the two main rival decision procedures over Dempster-Shafer belief functions — the upper/lower probability envelope approach and Smets' pignistic-probability approach — coincide once frame refinements are admitted into the pignistic side. Defining P^S as the set of pignistic transforms induced by all refinements of the frame, he proves E_*[U] and E^*[U] over P^S equal the lower/upper expected utility over the full credal set P that dominates Bel. The paper supports propstore's choice to keep belief as an imprecise [Bel, Pl] interval at the source-of-truth layer rather than collapsing to a single probability prematurely, since any reasonable single-probability projection lives in the closure of P^S and hence inside P.
