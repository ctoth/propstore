## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Alchourron, Gardenfors & Makinson (1985)** - AGM postulates for belief revision; the paper notes that AGM cannot be directly applied to argumentation but the connection deserves formal development
- **Boella, Kaci & van der Torre (2009a,b)** - Most directly comparable work studying dynamics of AFs, but limited to single-extension semantics; understanding how their approach differs helps position the contribution
- **Rotstein et al. (2008a,b)** - Argument theory change and abstract framework for handling dynamics; complements this paper by working at a more structured (evidence-based) level rather than abstract
- **Cayrol, Dupin de Saint-Cyr & Lagasquie-Schiex (2008)** - Earlier version of this work (KR 2008) restricted to adding one argument with only one interaction; this JAIR paper generalizes to multiple interactions
- **Dung (1995)** - Foundational paper for abstract argumentation frameworks; all definitions and semantics in this paper build directly on Dung's framework
