Provides a formal taxonomy of how adding an argument with interactions to a Dung abstract argumentation framework changes the extension set, defining seven structural properties (decisive, restrictive, questioning, destructive, expansive, conservative, altering) and three status-based properties (monotony, priority to recency, partial monotony) with necessary and sufficient conditions under grounded and preferred semantics.
The key results show that under grounded semantics, adding an argument can never be restrictive or questioning, while under preferred semantics the presence of even-length cycles is the critical factor.
Directly relevant to predicting extension changes when new claims or arguments are added to an argumentation system, providing implementable conditions for classifying the type and impact of such additions.
