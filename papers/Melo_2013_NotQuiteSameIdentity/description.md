Provides formal criteria distinguishing strict identity from near-identity in Linked Data, proving that detecting and repairing erroneous owl:sameAs links is NP-hard (minimum multicut), and presents LP relaxation and Hungarian algorithm solutions that identify and remove hundreds of thousands of constraint violations at Web scale on the BTC 2011 dataset.
Directly relevant to concept identity management: the distinction between genuine identity and mere similarity mirrors the non-commitment principle, and the graph-based constraint framework offers a principled approach to detecting when concept merges violate distinctness assumptions.
