Proposes the Generative Lexicon theory, where word meaning is captured by four levels of representation (argument structure, event structure, qualia structure, inheritance structure) and generative devices (type coercion, cocompositionality) derive context-dependent senses without multiplying lexical entries.
The key contribution is Qualia Structure, inspired by Aristotle, which decomposes nominal semantics into constitutive, formal, telic, and agentive roles that interact compositionally with verbs and modifiers.
Relevant to propstore's concept representation and claim reconciliation, as it provides a principled theory of how the same term can carry different meanings in different argumentative contexts without requiring separate stored senses.
