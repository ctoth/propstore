Analyzes the systematic misuse of owl:sameAs in Linked Data, arguing it is too strong for most actual uses since it commits to full Leibnizian identity (substitutivity of all properties). Proposes a Similarity Ontology with eight graded identity/similarity properties (sameIndividual, claimsIdentical, almostSameAs, etc.) with formally specified reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity characteristics. An empirical Mechanical Turk experiment on 250 randomly sampled owl:sameAs statements from the LOD Cloud shows approximately half are not true identity, validating the need for weaker alternatives.
