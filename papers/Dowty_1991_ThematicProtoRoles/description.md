Proposes that thematic roles are not discrete categories but two graded cluster concepts (Proto-Agent and Proto-Patient), each defined by independent verbal entailments, with argument selection (subject vs. object) determined by counting which argument has more entailments of each type. Demonstrates that traditional role hierarchies, spray/load alternations, psychological predicate doublets, and the unaccusative/unergative distinction all follow naturally from the proto-role framework. Relevant to propstore as a foundational example of non-commitment to discrete categorization in semantic representation -- roles as graded entailment clusters rather than atomic labels.
