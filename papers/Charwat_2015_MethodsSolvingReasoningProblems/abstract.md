# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

Within the last decade abstract argumentation has emerged as a central field in Artificial Intelligence, Besides providing a core formalism for many advanced argumentation systems, abstract argumentation has also proved crucial in several application areas concerning logic and other AI methods. Also from a computational point of view, abstract argumentation has attracted significant attention, as several reasoning problems in this formalism exhibit high computational complexity. This led to a flourishing of different techniques in order to realize efficient and scalable solvers. We will provide a survey of different methods for solving reasoning problems in abstract argumentation and compare their particular features. Moreover, we highlight available state-of-the-art systems, both for research and practice.

---

## Our Interpretation

This paper surveys the landscape of computational methods for solving reasoning problems in Dung-style abstract argumentation frameworks. It systematically compares reduction-based approaches (encoding argumentation problems into SAT, CSP, or ASP) with direct algorithmic approaches (labelling algorithms, dialogue games, tree-decomposition-based dynamic programming), covering complexity results and nine implemented systems. The survey is essential reference material for anyone implementing argumentation solvers or choosing between computational approaches for reasoning about argument acceptability.
