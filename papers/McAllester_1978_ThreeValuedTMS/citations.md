## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Doyle 1978a** (TR-419) -- The primary comparison point: Doyle's TMS uses two-valued logic with non-monotonic dependencies and conditional proofs. McAllester's system is designed as a simpler alternative. Already in collection as Doyle_1979_TruthMaintenanceSystem (the published journal version).
- **Stallman and Sussman 1976** -- The ARS (Algebraic Reasoning System) that motivated truth maintenance work. Uses forward reasoning and dependency-directed backtracking for circuit analysis. The primary application domain for McAllester's TMS.
- **de Kleer, Doyle, Steele and Sussman 1977** -- The "Explicit Control of Reasoning" paper, describing AMORD which is another system in this family. Foundational for understanding the MIT AI Lab's approach to reason maintenance.
