Defines proof procedures for computing ideal sceptical semantics in both abstract argumentation and assumption-based argumentation. The paper introduces ideal dispute trees, AB-dispute derivations, IB-dispute derivations, and Fail-dispute derivations, proving soundness in general and completeness for p-acyclic finite ABA frameworks. It is directly relevant to propstore's conservative conflict-resolution layer because it gives proof-carrying procedures for sceptical acceptance that are less vacuous than grounded semantics.
