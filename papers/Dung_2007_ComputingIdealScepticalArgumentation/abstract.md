# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

We present two dialectic procedures for the sceptical ideal semantics for argumentation. The first procedure is defined in terms of dispute trees, for abstract argumentation frameworks. The second procedure is defined in dialectical terms, for assumption-based argumentation frameworks. The procedures are adapted from (variants of) corresponding procedures for computing the credulous admissible semantics for assumption-based argumentation, proposed in [P.M. Dung, R.A. Kowalski, F. Toni, Dialectic proof procedures for assumption-based, admissible argumentation, Artificial Intelligence 170 (2006) 114-159]. We prove that the first procedure is sound and complete, and the second procedure is sound in general and complete for a special but natural class of assumption-based argumentation frameworks, that we refer to as p-acyclic. We also prove that in the case of p-acyclic assumption-based argumentation (a variant of) the procedure of [P.M. Dung, R.A. Kowalski, F. Toni, Dialectic proof procedures for assumption-based, admissible argumentation, Artificial Intelligence 170 (2006) 114-159] for the admissible semantics is complete. Finally, we present a variant of the procedure of [P.M. Dung, R.A. Kowalski, F. Toni, Dialectic proof procedures for assumption-based, admissible argumentation, Artificial Intelligence 170 (2006) 114-159] that is sound for the sceptical grounded semantics.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper turns ideal semantics from a declarative definition into concrete proof procedures. Its main move is to refine admissibility procedures with extra filtering and failure checks so that sceptical ideal support can be computed in both Dung AFs and flat ABA frameworks. For propstore, this matters because it gives a formal recipe for conservative acceptance that is stronger than grounded semantics without collapsing into credulous preferred reasoning.
