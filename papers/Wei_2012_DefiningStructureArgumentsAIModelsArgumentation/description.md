Formalizes the standard informal taxonomy of argument structure inside ASPIC+ and argues that the usual labels are mixing up individual argument types with argument structures. The paper's main payoff is a clean distinction between unit I and unit II arguments, plus serial, convergent, divergent, linked, and mixed structures. It also shows that Vorobej-style hybrid arguments are unnecessary once deductive and defeasible inference are separated.
