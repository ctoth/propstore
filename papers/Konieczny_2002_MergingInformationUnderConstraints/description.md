Defines the IC merging framework with postulates IC0-IC8 for combining multiple belief bases under integrity constraints, proving representation theorems that link axiomatic operators to distance-based pre-orders on interpretations. Introduces three concrete operator families (Sigma for majority, Max for quasi-merging, GMax for arbitration) and shows that belief revision and Liberatore-Schaerf commutative revision are special cases. Foundational for any principled multi-source belief merging implementation, providing correctness criteria analogous to AGM postulates for revision.
