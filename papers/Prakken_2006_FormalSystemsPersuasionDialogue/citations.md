## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Walton & Krabbe (1995)** — *Commitment in Dialogue*. Foundational work defining dialogue types and commitment stores; directly relevant to propstore's stance tracking model.
- **Parsons, Wooldridge & Amgoud (2003)** — *Properties of formal inter-agent dialogues*. The Toulouse-Liverpool framework with formal properties for multi-type dialogues; relevant for extending propstore to handle information-seeking and inquiry alongside persuasion.
- **Prakken (2005)** — *Coherence and flexibility in dialogue games for argumentation*. The author's own extended framework with flexible turntaking and reply structure; closest to a complete formal specification.
- **Dung (1995)** — *On the acceptability of arguments*. Already in propstore collection; the argumentation semantics that underlie all dialogue systems reviewed here.
- **Gordon (1994)** — *The Pleadings Game*. Early formal dialogue system from legal AI; relevant for understanding how dialogue games model legal argumentation processes.
