# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

This article reviews formal systems that regulate persuasion dialogues. In such dialogues two or more participants aim to resolve a difference of opinion, each trying to persuade the other participants to adopt their point of view. Systems for persuasion dialogue have found applications in various fields of computer science, such as argumentation logic, artificial intelligence and law, multi-agent systems, intelligent tutoring and computer-supported collaborative argumentation. Taking a game-theoretic view on dialogue systems, this review proposes a formal specification of the main elements of dialogue systems for persuasion and then uses it to critically review some of the main formal systems for persuasion dialogue that have been proposed in the literature on computational argumentation. The focus of this review will be on regulating the interactions between agents rather than on the design and behaviour of individual agents within a dialogue.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper addresses the lack of a unified framework for comparing formal persuasion dialogue systems in computational argumentation. It proposes a specification template covering communication language, protocol rules, commitment stores, speech acts, turntaking, and termination, then applies this template to compare nine systems including Mackenzie (1979), Walton & Krabbe (1995), and Prakken's own frameworks. This is relevant to propstore because the commitment store model directly parallels stance tracking, and the speech act taxonomy provides formal grounding for the agent workflow layer's claim/challenge/concede operations.
