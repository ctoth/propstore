Introduces MultiLanguage (ML) systems — hierarchies of first-order languages linked by reflection-up/reflection-down bridge rules — and proves that the common normal modal logics (K, T, S4, S5) embed into corresponding ML systems via a syntactic `(□A)* = Th("A*")` mapping.
Presents MK (ascending-chain formalization of metatheoretic provability) and MBK (descending-chain formalization of belief/propositional attitudes), both equivalent to modal K but with locality properties modal logics cannot express — notably, in MK local inconsistency does not imply global inconsistency (Theorem 5.3, Corollary 5.5), and a finite set of assumptions cannot render all theories inconsistent (Theorem 5.4).
Provides the computational companion to McCarthy's philosophical `ist(c, p)` and is directly central to propstore's context/semantic layer: supplies formal grounding for "don't collapse disagreement in storage" (localization of inconsistency) and for bridge-rule primitives between context theories.
