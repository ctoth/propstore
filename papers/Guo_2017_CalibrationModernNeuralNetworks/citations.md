## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Naeini et al. 2015** — Bayesian Binning into Quantiles (BBQ): the strongest binning-based calibration competitor. Important for understanding when binning methods might outperform temperature scaling.
- **Platt 1999** — Original Platt scaling paper. Foundation for temperature scaling and all parametric calibration methods. Essential reading for understanding the logistic calibration framework.
- **Zadrozny & Elkan 2001, 2002** — Histogram binning and isotonic regression. The non-parametric calibration methods that predate deep learning era. Important baselines.
- **Niculescu-Mizil & Caruana 2005** — Comprehensive comparison of calibration methods on classical ML models. Provides historical context for why older models were better calibrated.
- **Lakshminarayanan et al. 2016/2017** — Deep ensembles for uncertainty estimation. Alternative approach to calibration that uses multiple models rather than post-hoc correction. Relevant if temperature scaling proves insufficient.
