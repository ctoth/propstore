# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

FrameNet is a three-year NSF-supported project in corpus-based computational lexicography, now in its second year (NSF IRI-9618838, "Tools for Lexicon Building"). The project's key features are (a) a commitment to corpus evidence for semantic and syntactic generalizations, and (b) the representation of the valences of its target words (mostly nouns, adjectives, and verbs) in which the semantic and syntactic properties of co-occurring complements and phrases, each accompanied by (c) a representative collection of associated corpus attestations, which jointly exemplify the observed linkages between "frame elements" and their syntactic realizations (e.g., grammatical function, phrase type, and other syntactic traits). This report will outline the project's goals and workflow, and information about the computational and lexicographic tools that have been adopted or created in-house for this work.

---

## Our Interpretation

FrameNet builds a lexical database documenting how English words combine with their arguments, grounded in Frame Semantics and backed by British National Corpus attestations. The paper describes the project's architecture (Frame Database, Lexical Database, annotated examples), its staged workflow from frame identification through human annotation, and the software tools supporting each stage. It is relevant as a large-scale precedent for organizing structured knowledge around semantic frames with corpus evidence and provenance tracking.
