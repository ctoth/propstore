## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[3] Alchourrón, Gärdenfors, Makinson 1985** — the foundational AGM postulates paper. Required reading for any of propstore's belief-revision work.
- **[2] Billington, Antoniou, Governatori, Maher 1999** — first paper on revising defeasible-logic belief sets and adapting AGM to non-monotonic settings; the direct precursor to this paper. Should be in the collection.
- **[4] Governatori & Rotolo 2010** — DL-based modelling of legal abrogation/annulment; cited heavily and shows AGM is unsuited to legal-reasoning theories. Strong candidate for retrieval.
- **[9] Governatori, Maher, Billington, Antoniou 2004** — argumentation semantics for DL; bridges DL and Dung-style argumentation. Useful for propstore's `aspic_bridge` and Dung-AF construction.
- **[26, 27] Moguillansky et al. 2008/2010** — DeLP argument-theory-change. Critically discussed here; offers an alternative non-monotonic revision style propstore could compare against.
- **[19] Levi 1977** + **[20] Gärdenfors 1988** — origins of Levi and Harper identities; relevant given that both fail in this DL setting and propstore must understand what classical AGM expected.
