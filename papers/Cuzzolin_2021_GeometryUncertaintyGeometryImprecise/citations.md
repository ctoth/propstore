# Citations

## Reference List

Cuzzolin's bibliography contains **2137 numbered references**, alphabetically sorted by first-author surname, with no separate author or subject index. Numbering scheme: numeric `[N]` keys cited inline throughout the book. The complete numbered list spans book pages 729-(end). For a curated working subset of ~80 highest-priority follow-ups organised by topic and author cluster, see [`chapters/chapter-10-future-references.md`](chapters/chapter-10-future-references.md).

The full 2137-entry list is not transcribed here in the interest of token economy; the chapter-10 file has the curated subset, and the original PDF is available at `paper.pdf` for any specific reference lookup. Below we list only the highest-priority follow-up clusters that propstore's future work would most likely consult.

## Key Citations for Follow-up

Reference numbers are Cuzzolin's bibliography keys.

### Founders of belief-function theory

- `[413]` Dempster, A.P. *Upper and lower probabilities induced by a multivalued mapping.* Annals of Mathematical Statistics 38 (1967). — The originating paper. Foundational for everything in propstore that touches Dempster-Shafer or random-set semantics.
- `[1583]` Shafer, G. *A Mathematical Theory of Evidence.* Princeton University Press (1976). — The canonical book-length treatment. Companion reading to Cuzzolin Ch.2-4.
- `[1703]` ff. Smets cluster. *The Transferable Belief Model.* Multiple papers (Smets, Kennes, etc.). — TBM is propstore's reference semantics for non-Bayesian belief reasoning.

### Imprecise probability and credal sets

- `[1871]`-`[1878]` Walley cluster. *Statistical Reasoning with Imprecise Probabilities.* Chapman & Hall (1991) and follow-on papers. — The lower/upper-prevision tradition. Compare-and-contrast reading for Ch.6 §6.1.
- de Cooman, Troffaes, Antonucci — multiple entries, see chapter-10 file. — Active modern imprecise-probability community, particularly relevant for credal-network practice.
- `[1141]`, `[1086]` — Levi *The Enterprise of Knowledge* and credal-set foundational entries.

### Random sets and statistical learning

- `[1849]`-`[1851]` Vapnik cluster. *Statistical Learning Theory.* — Fundamental for Cuzzolin's Theorem 117 PAC-bound generalisation in Ch.17.
- `[1300]`, `[1304]` Molchanov. *Theory of Random Sets.* — Cited by Cuzzolin as the right mathematical foundation for a future statistical theory of belief.

### Probability transforms

- `[245]`, `[291]`, `[293]`, `[295]` — Cobb-Shenoy plausibility transform and related work.
- `[1098]` ff. — Klir, Smets, Voorbraak on the pignistic transform (Smets BetP).
- `[1859]` Voorbraak. *A computationally efficient approximation of Dempster-Shafer theory.* Int. J. Man-Machine Studies 30 (1989).

### Conditioning rules

- `[531]` Fagin, Halpern. *A new approach to updating beliefs.* — Lower/upper conditional envelopes (Ch.4 §4.5.2).
- Suppes-Zanotti, Spies — see chapter-09 file for the full conditioning-rule citation cluster.

### Combination-rule landscape

- Yager `[1999]`-`[2011]` — Yager's combination rule (no normalisation, conflict to Θ).
- Dubois-Prade `[758]`, `[757]` — disjunctive and t-conorm combination.
- Dezert-Smarandache cluster — DSmT (free + hybrid models).
- Denœux — cautious and bold rules.

### Causality and probabilistic graphical models

- `[1398]`-`[1411]` Pearl cluster. *Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems* (1988); *Causality* (2000). — Background for any propstore work that touches causal inference, defeasibility, or graphical model semantics.

### Possibility theory and fuzzy

- Dubois, Prade — many entries. *Possibility Theory: An Approach to Computerized Processing of Uncertainty* (1988).
- Zadeh `[2089]` ff. — fuzzy sets, Zadeh's Generalised Theory of Uncertainty.

### Rough sets

- `[1393]`-`[1397]` Pawlak cluster. *Rough Sets: Theoretical Aspects of Reasoning about Data* (1991).

### Capacity theory

- `[283]` Choquet. *Theory of capacities.* Annales de l'Institut Fourier 5 (1953/1954). — The foundational paper on non-additive set functions. Background for Ch.6 §6.2.
- Grabisch cluster — modern capacity theory and Choquet integration.

### Belief revision (AGM and successors)

- `[26]` Alchourrón, Gärdenfors, Makinson. *On the logic of theory change.* J. Symbolic Logic (1985). — The AGM foundation paper. Companion reading for `propstore.belief_set`.
- Full lineage including Darwiche-Pearl iteration, Boutilier conditional revision, Gärdenfors-Makinson entrenchment is captured in the chapter-10 file.

### Game-theoretic probability

- `[1615]` ff. Shafer-Vovk. *Probability and Finance: It's Only a Game!* — Foundational for Ch.6 §6.13. Game-theoretic alternative to Kolmogorov; relevant to ASPIC+ rule-priority semantics.

### Subjective logic

- `[1877]` ff. Jøsang cluster. *Subjective Logic.* Springer (2016). — The opinion algebra grounding propstore's vacuity / honest-ignorance principle.

### Probabilistic argumentation systems

- `[758]`, `[1607]`, `[1685]` — Haenni, Lehmann; the ABEL system. Cited in Ch.6 §6.6.4 as the closest argumentation-theoretic neighbour to belief theory.

### Spohn epistemic states

- `[1717]`, `[1722]`, `[1730]` — Spohn cluster on ordinal conditional functions, ranking theory, α-conditionalisation. Cited in Ch.6 §6.9. Companion to propstore's defeasibility module.

### Information-theoretic decision making

- Smets pignistic transform / Generalised Insufficient Reason — see chapter-09 and chapter-07 files for the citation cluster.
- Choquet, Sugeno integrals — for non-additive utility maximisation.

### Continuous-domain belief functions

- Strat, Smets — belief functions on Borel intervals.
- Kramosil — measure-theoretic generalisations (`Bel_+, Bel^+, Bel_{++}, Bel^{++}`).
- See `chapters/chapter-03-reasoning.md` §4.9.
