## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Open Science Collaboration (2015)** [ref 19]: The RPP psychology replication project -- direct comparator for this economics replication study. Provides baseline replication rate (36%) for psychology.
- **Dreber et al. (2015)** [ref 25]: Prediction markets for the RPP psychology replications -- methodological precursor showing markets can predict replication outcomes.
- **Ioannidis (2005)** [ref 2]: Foundational paper on why most published research findings are false -- provides the theoretical framework for understanding replication failures.
- **Simonsohn (2015)** [ref 23]: "Small telescopes" approach to evaluating replication results -- alternative statistical framework for assessing replication success.
- **Cumming (2008)** [ref 21]: Replication and p intervals -- demonstrates that confidence intervals are better than p-values for predicting future replication outcomes.
