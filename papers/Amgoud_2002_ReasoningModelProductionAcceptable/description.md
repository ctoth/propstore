Introduces a preference-based extension of Dung's abstract argumentation framework in which an argument is acceptable if it either outranks its direct defeaters or is defended by other acceptable arguments. The paper defines the combined semantics as a least-fixpoint construction and proves an equivalent AND/OR dialogue-tree proof theory for deciding whether a given argument is acceptable. It is directly relevant to propstore because it is the original PAF design later criticized by Amgoud and Vesic 2011, and it offers both a semantics-level treatment of preferences and an explanation-oriented proof procedure.
