Defines and axiomatically evaluates two impact measures for quantitative argumentation frameworks: a revised removal-based measure (ImpS^rev) and a novel Shapley-value-based measure (ImpSh). The paper introduces nine desirable principles and shows that the counting semantics uniquely satisfies both Independence and Directionality across both measures. Directly relevant to propstore's gradual semantics layer, providing principled tools for explaining how arguments affect each other's computed scores.
