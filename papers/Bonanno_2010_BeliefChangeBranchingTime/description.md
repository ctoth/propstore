Proves that AGM-consistency of branching-time belief revision frames is equivalent to rationalizability by a total pre-order and to a directly-verifiable frame property (PLS), providing the formal bridge between temporal branching structures and the AGM postulates.
Introduces iterated belief revision functions B(h, K, phi) as ternary operations that take informational history into account, showing that AGM-consistency imposes only the weak REFweak constraint on iterated revision.
Directly relevant to formalizing propstore's multi-context reasoning as branching belief revision, with the PLS property providing a checkable well-behavedness criterion for branching knowledge structures.
