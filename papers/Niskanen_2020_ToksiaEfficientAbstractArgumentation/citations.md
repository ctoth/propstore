## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Cerutti, Giacomín, and Vallati 2019** — Algorithmic framework for planning-based argumentation solvers; provides context for the ITEGAR approach used in µ-toksia's dynamic AF support.
- **Charwat, Dvorák, Gaggl, Wallner, and Woltran 2015** — Comprehensive survey of methods for solving reasoning problems in abstract argumentation; essential background for understanding the landscape of approaches.
- **Dvorák, Järvisalo, Wallner, and Woltran 2014** — Complexity-sensitive decision procedures; theoretical foundation for why different algorithms are needed for different semantics.
- **Wallner, Weissenbacher, and Woltran 2013** — Advanced SAT techniques specifically for abstract argumentation; likely contains techniques adopted or refined by µ-toksia.
- **Egly, Gaggl, and Woltran 2010** — Aspartix ASP-based argumentation system; the main ASP-based competitor, useful for understanding the SAT vs ASP tradeoff.
