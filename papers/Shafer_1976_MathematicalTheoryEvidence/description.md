Foundational monograph establishing Dempster-Shafer theory of belief functions, a generalization of probability that replaces the additivity axiom with superadditivity, enabling explicit representation of ignorance and partial belief.
Introduces belief functions, Dempster's rule for combining independent evidence, simple and separable support functions, weights of evidence, compatible frames of discernment, and the weight-of-conflict conjecture.
Directly relevant to propstore's uncertainty representation layer: belief functions provide a principled framework for representing and combining uncertain claim support without forcing premature commitment to precise probabilities.
