## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[25] Mayer et al. (COMMA 2018)** - Predecessor work that created the original AbstRCT dataset and initial AM approach for clinical trials. Essential for understanding the dataset lineage and annotation scheme.
- **[7] Cabrio and Villata (IJCAI 2018)** - Survey of five years of argument mining, providing broad context for the field and its evolution.
- **[38] Stab and Gurevych (Comp. Linguist. 2017)** - Influential work on parsing argumentation in persuasive essays; provides the dominant framework for component+relation AM pipelines.
- **[11] Eger et al. (ACL 2017)** - Neural end-to-end AM learning; serves as one of the baselines in this paper. Important for understanding the Tree-LSTM approach to joint component and relation extraction.
- **[36] Reimers et al. (ACL 2019)** - Classification and clustering of arguments with contextualized embeddings; relevant for understanding how transformer representations capture argumentative semantics.
