# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

FrameNet is the best currently operational version of Chuck Fillmore's Frame Semantics. As FrameNet has evolved over the years, we have been building a set of increasingly ambitious prototype systems that exploit FrameNet as a machine resource. Results from this work point to frames as a natural representation for applications that require linking textual meaning to world knowledge.

---

## Our Interpretation

This paper surveys the evolution of FrameNet as a computational resource for bridging natural language text and structured knowledge representation. The key finding is that frame semantics provides a principled method for connecting surface linguistic forms to deeper conceptual structures needed for inference, with demonstrated applications in information extraction (95.9% precision), question answering, and cross-lingual metaphor analysis. It is relevant to propstore primarily in its treatment of multiple perspectives on events via frame-based representation, which parallels propstore's approach to holding rival interpretations without premature commitment.
