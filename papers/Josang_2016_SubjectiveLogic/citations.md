## Key Citations for Follow-up

For propstore's subjective-logic implementation, the following citations are highest-priority follow-ups. They establish the formal foundations referenced throughout this book and are likely candidates for further reading and ingestion into the propstore paper collection.

- **[42] Jøsang, "A logic for uncertain probabilities" (IJUFKBS 2001)** — The canonical subjective-logic paper. Read first if only one prior Jøsang paper is read; this book essentially expands and harmonises [42].
- **[33] Hankin, "A generalization of the Dirichlet distribution" (J. Stat. Software 2010)** — Provides the hyper-Dirichlet PDF used in Section 3.6.5 with no closed-form normalisation. Comes with an R package; relevant for any propstore numerical implementation of hyper-opinions.
- **[81] Pearl, "Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems" (1988)** — Foundational Bayesian network reference that subjective Bayesian networks (Ch. 17) generalise. Pearl's plausible-reasoning constraint (cited [82]) is enforced in multinomial deduction Eq. 9.69.
- **[60] Jøsang & Kaplan, "Principles of subjective networks" (FUSION 2016)** — The companion paper for Chapter 17. Read for the full subjective-network formalism beyond what the book chapter contains.
- **[90] Shafer, "A Mathematical Theory of Evidence" (1976)** — Dempster-Shafer baseline that subjective logic is repeatedly contrasted with. Required reading to understand the BCF/CBF distinction and Zadeh's [101] critique.
- **[98] Walley, "Inferences from Multinomial Data" (1996)** — Imprecise Dirichlet Model baseline. Subjective logic's bijection to Dirichlet PDFs is conceptually parallel but uses base rates rather than upper/lower probabilities.
- **[44] Jøsang, "Conditional reasoning with subjective logic" (JMVL Soft Comp 2008)** — Foundational paper for Ch. 9 (deduction) and Ch. 10 (abduction). Updated and harmonised in this book; the book corrects an earlier omission of MBR.
- **[59] Jøsang & Hankin, "Interpretation and fusion of hyper-opinions" (FUSION 2012)** — Foundational hyper-opinion formalisation, used throughout Sec. 3.6 and Ch. 12.
