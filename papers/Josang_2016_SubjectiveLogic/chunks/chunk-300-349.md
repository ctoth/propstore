## Definitions and Notation

### Trust Networks (Ch.15)

- **Definition 15.6 (Directed Series and Parallel Composition)** *(p.282)*: Directed series composition replaces edge `[A;C]` with `[A;B]` and `[B;C]` (B is a new node). Directed parallel composition replaces edge `[A;C]` with `[A;C]_1` and `[A;C]_2`.
- **Definition 15.7 (DSPG-Synthesis Criteria for Sub-Path Addition)** *(p.285)*: When adding a new sub-path:
  1. Target node must be reachable from source node in existing graph.
  2. Source and target must have equal nesting levels in existing graph.
  3. Nesting level of source/target must be ≤ nesting level of all intermediate nodes.

### Reputation (Ch.16)

- **Definition 16.1 (Community Base Rate)** *(p.296)*: Let `R_{M,τ}` be aggregate reputation vector for a community, and `S_{M,τ}` the corresponding multinomial probability reputation vector via Eq.(16.2). Community base rate: `a_{M,(τ+1)} = S_{M,τ}`.

### Bayesian Networks (Ch.17)

- **Notation conventions** *(p.309)*:
  - `x_{I,i}`: specific value with index `i` of specific variable `X_I`
  - `x_{I:}`: some value with non-specific index of specific variable `X_I` (colon as wildcard)
  - `x`: some value with non-specific index of variable `X`
  - `\hat{X} = {X_1, X_2, ..., X_K}`: set of joint variables
  - `\hat{Z} = {Z_1, Z_2, ..., Z_M}`
- **Markov property** *(p.310)*: BN represents all significant dependencies; no direct dependencies exist that are not shown via edges.
- **I-map / D-map / perfect map** *(p.311)*: I-map = Markov property holds (every absent edge → real independence). D-map = every present edge → direct dependence. Perfect map = both.
- **Definition 17.1 (Bayesian Conditional Independence)** *(p.321)*: X and Z are conditionally independent given Y iff knowing Y makes X uninformative about Z and vice versa.
- **Definition 17.2 (Subjective Conditional Independence)** *(p.322)*: Variable X and Z are conditionally independent given an opinion on Y iff, given an **absolute opinion on Y**, any opinion on X provides no information on the likelihood of values of Z, and any opinion on Z provides no information on the likelihood of values of X. Note the qualifier: **absolute** opinion on Y; relatively-uncertain opinions on Y do NOT produce independence.

### Bayesian Reputation (Ch.16)

$$
S = \frac{r + Wa}{r + s + W}
$$
*(p.291, Eq.16.1)* — Binomial reputation score; `r` positive ratings, `s` negative, `a` base rate (default 0.5), `W` non-informative prior weight (default 2). Score = probability the next experience is 'Good'.

$$
S(L_i) = E(p(L_i)\mid r,a) = \frac{r(L_i) + W a(L_i)}{W + \sum_{i=1}^k r(L_i)}
$$
*(p.292, Eq.16.2)* — Multinomial reputation score; `r(L_i)` rating count for level `L_i`; `a(L_i)` base rate over level.

$$
R_y = (R_y(L_i)\mid i=1,\ldots,k)
$$
*(p.292, Eq.16.3)* — Aggregate ratings vector (cumulative) for service object y.

$$
r_{y,\tau} = \sum_{A\in M_{y,\tau}} r_y^A
$$
*(p.293, Eq.16.4)* — Sum of all ratings during period τ.

$$
R_{y,(\tau+1)} = \lambda \cdot R_{y,\tau} + r_{y,(\tau+1)}, \quad 0 \le \lambda \le 1
$$
*(p.293, Eq.16.5)* — Recursive ageing with longevity factor λ. λ=0: full forgetting after one period; λ=1: never forgotten.

$$
R_{y,(\tau+n)} = \lambda^n \cdot R_{y,\tau} + r_{y,(\tau+n)}
$$
*(p.293, Eq.16.6)* — Multi-period update.

$$
\sum_{j=0}^{\infty} \lambda^j = \frac{1}{1-\lambda}, \quad -1 < \lambda < 1
$$
*(p.294, Eq.16.7)* — Geometric series for convergence analysis.

$$
R_{y,\infty} = \frac{e_y}{1-\lambda}
$$
*(p.294, Eq.16.8)* — Convergence value of accumulated rating vector with constant rating `e_y` per period.

$$
a_{y,\tau}(L_i) = \frac{Q_{y,\tau}(L_i) + W a(L_i)}{W + \sum_{i=1}^k Q_{y,\tau}(L_i)}
$$
*(p.294, Eq.16.9)* — Individual base rate computed from individual evidence vector `Q_{y,τ}` and community base rate `a`. Two-layered base-rate model.

$$
Q_{y,\tau} = \sum_{j=1}^{\tau} R_{y,j} \quad\text{(total history)}
$$
*(p.295, Eq.16.10)*

$$
Q_{y,\tau} = \sum_{j=u}^{\tau} R_{y,j}, \;\text{Window Size} = (\tau - u) \quad\text{(sliding window)}
$$
*(p.295, Eq.16.11)*

$$
Q_{y,\tau} = \lambda_H \cdot Q_{y,(\tau-1)} + r_{y,\tau}, \;\lambda < \lambda_H \le 1 \quad\text{(high-longevity)}
$$
*(p.295, Eq.16.12)*

$$
R_{M,\tau} = \sum_{y_j\in M} R_{y,\tau}
$$
*(p.296, Eq.16.13)* — Aggregate community reputation vector.

$$
a_{M,(\tau+1)} = S_{M,\tau}
$$
*(p.296, Eq.16.14)* — Community base rate as previous-period community score.

$$
S_y(L_i) = \frac{R_y^*(L_i) + W a(L_i)}{W + \sum_{j=1}^k R_y^*(L_j)}
$$
*(p.297, Eq.16.15)*

$$
\sum_{i=1}^{k} S(L_i) = 1
$$
*(p.297, Eq.16.16)* — Multinomial probability reputation score sums to 1.

$$
\sigma = \sum_{i=1}^{k} v(L_i) S(L_i), \quad v(L_i) = \frac{i-1}{k-1}
$$
*(p.298, Eq.16.18)* — Point estimate reputation score with evenly-spaced point values.

$$
\begin{cases} r = \sigma \sum_{i=1}^k R_y(L_i) \\ s = \sum_{i=1}^k R_y(L_i) - r \end{cases}
$$
*(p.299, Eq.16.19)* — Multinomial→binomial rating projection.

$$
[A,F] = [A;\text{RS}] : [\text{RS};B] : [B,F]

### Subjective Bayes' Theorem Validation *(p.315, Table 17.2)*

Three binary variables `X ⇒ Y ⇒ Z` with base rates `a_X = (0.700, 0.300)`, `a_Y = (0.732, 0.268)`, `a_Z = (0.717, 0.282)`.

Conditionals `ω_{Y|X}`:
- `ω_{Y|x_1}`: b(y_1)=0.950, b(y_2)=0.000, u=0.050
- `ω_{Y|x_2}`: b(y_1)=0.100, b(y_2)=0.850, u=0.050

Conditionals `ω_{Z|Y}`:
- `ω_{Z|y_1}`: b(z_1)=0.900, b(z_2)=0.050, u=0.050
- `ω_{Z|y_2}`: b(z_1)=0.050, b(z_2)=0.850, u=0.100

Method 1: `ω_{X̃|Z} = ω_{Ỹ|Z} ⊙ ω_{X̃|Y}` (Eq.17.15):
- `ω_{X̃|z_1}`: b=0.759, 0.036, u=0.205
- `ω_{X̃|z_2}`: b=0.075, 0.767, u=0.158

Method 2: `ω_{X̃|Z} = φ̃((ω_{Y|X} ⊙ ω_{Z|Y}), a_X)` (Eq.17.16):
- `ω_{X̃|z_1}`: b=0.774, 0.042, u=0.184
- `ω_{X̃|z_2}`: b=0.080, 0.769, u=0.151

Conclusion: projected probabilities equal, uncertainty masses (0.205 vs 0.184, 0.158 vs 0.151) approximately equal but not identical.

## Algorithms / Procedures

### DSPG synthesis flowchart *(p.283, Fig 15.10)*

(a) Prepare non-DSPG trust network for synthesis. Set threshold `p_T`. Create empty DSPG.
(b) Identify each trust path A→X. For each, compute the product of projected probabilities of referral trust edges (last functional belief edge to X excluded).
(c) Rank paths by product (highest = index 1). Initialize DSPG with path 1. Set index pointer to 1.
(d) Increment pointer; select next ranked path. Exit if no path left, or if path's product < p_T.
(e) Check if path can be integrated into DSPG (apply Definition 15.7).
(f) If yes, add new edges only (existing not duplicated).
(g) If no, drop path.
(h) Loop. Final DSPG analysed via algorithm of Section 15.3.

### Trust + Reputation combined derivation *(p.301)*

`A` derives trust in `F` via:
1. Reputation system RS publishes `R_B^RS = Beta^e(p_x, r_x, s_x, a_x)` → mapped to opinion `ω_B^RS` (Definition 3.3).
2. A trusts RS: `ω_RS^A`.
3. B trusts F: `ω_F^B`.
4. Trust path: `[A,F] = [A;RS] : [RS;B] : [B,F]`.
5. Result: `ω_F^A = ω_RS^A ⊗ ω_B^RS ⊗ ω_F^B`.

### Reputation→opinion→trust transitivity pipeline

Eq.(16.19) projects multinomial→binomial → Definition 3.3 maps Beta to binomial opinion → Chapter 14 trust transitivity applies.

## Implementation Notes

- **Base rate network (BRN)** *(p.316)*: Crucial for SBN — derived by setting vacuous parent-node opinions and propagating via conditionals. propstore implication: when implementing subjective BN, the base-rate distributions for non-root nodes are NOT free parameters but functions of the conditionals.
- **Two methods for inverted chained conditionals** *(p.313-315)*: Eq.(17.15) inverts each conditional then chains via inverse-deduction; Eq.(17.16) chains then inverts once. Eq.(17.16) is computationally cheaper. They produce equal projected probabilities but different (approximately equal) uncertainty masses. **Test cases for implementation: Table 17.2.**
- **Discounting `⊗` in trust+reputation**: Reputation score must be mapped through Definition 3.3 (Beta→opinion mapping) before trust transitivity applies.
- **W=2 default**: Standard non-informative prior weight; larger W means new evidence has less weight, which can encode domain expertise or bootstrap from another reputation system.
- **Longevity factor**: λ ∈ [0,1) needed for finite convergence of Eq.(16.7); λ=1 means infinite accumulation, no convergence.
- **Cold start problem**: Use `a` directly for unrated objects; can bias to `negative` for harder market entry or `positive` for easier.
- **Two-layered base rate**: Individual base rate `a_{y,τ}` is partially function of community base rate `a` via Eq.(16.9). Prevents new objects from being trapped at extremes.
- **Continuous→discrete via fuzzy triangular membership**: Sliding window with q-value at midpoint of each level gives r=1; off-center q distributes weight to adjacent levels (Fig 16.3).
- **Polarisation invisibility under point estimate**: σ from Eq.(16.18) cannot distinguish unimodal vs bimodal score distributions; use full multinomial vector for display.
- **Naïve Bayes assumption**: Critical when joint conditionals `p(D,X|C)` unavailable — replaces with product of single-variable conditionals. Eq.(17.7) → Eq.(17.8); subjective form Eq.(17.26) → Eq.(17.27).
- **Markov property = I-map**: Required for valid BN; guarantees no hidden dependencies. SBNs preserve this in Definition 17.1.
- **Independence topologies** *(Fig 17.5)*: Causal chain X→Y→Z and common cause Y←X→Z both yield `p(Z|X,Y)=p(Z|Y or X)`; common effect X→Z←Y yields `p(X|Y,Z) ≠ p(X|Z)` ("explaining away" / dependence).
- **Multinomial division in intercausal reasoning**: Two variants — selective division (Section 8.3.3) when evidence is absolute/product opinion; proportional division (Section 8.3.2) when evidence is uncertain/product opinion.
- **Chain irrelevance**: As K grows in Eq.(17.13), the conditional sub-space shrinks monotonically — infinite chains produce totally irrelevant serial conditional. Implication: long subjective chains accumulate uncertainty exponentially.
- **Subjective conditional independence requires absolute opinion** *(p.322, Definition 17.2)*: For SBNs, the standard Bayesian conditional-independence semantics only holds if the conditioning variable carries an absolute opinion (i.e., probability 1 on a specific value, equivalent to degenerate opinion). Implication for propstore: when implementing SBN reasoning over uncertain evidence, do NOT prune dependencies based on the underlying BN graph topology alone — the dependencies "reactivate" when the conditioning evidence is itself uncertain.
- **Aleatory vs epistemic + uncertainty-maximised** *(p.322)*: A relatively uncertain but non-uncertainty-maximised opinion is **aleatory**. An uncertainty-maximised opinion can be aleatory or epistemic. For an epistemic situation (variables with specific instances), input argument opinions should be epistemic and therefore uncertainty-maximised. Absolute epistemic opinion on Y → independence of X and Z. Non-absolute epistemic on Y → Z dependent on X to some degree.
- **Bob's umbrella worked example** *(p.322-323)*: X = {x, x̄} weather forecast for rain; Y = {y, ȳ} Bob carries umbrella; Z = {z, z̄} Bob forgets umbrella on train. Conditionals `ω_{Y|X} = {ω_{Y|x}, ω_{Y|x̄}}` and `ω_{Z|Y} = {ω_{Z|y}, ω_{Z|ȳ}}`. With absolute opinion on Y → Z is independent of X. With uncertain opinion on Y → Z depends on X (because evidence about X via the forecast can refine the opinion on Y).
- **Total irrelevance via conditionals** *(p.323)*: Even Eq.(17.34) topology can have total X⊥Z if the conditionals themselves are totally irrelevant — `ω_{Y|X}` makes X totally irrelevant to Y, OR `ω_{Z|Y}` makes Y totally irrelevant to Z. This is a structural condition on conditional-opinion content, separate from absolute-opinion conditioning.
- **Subjective Network = SBN ∪ STN** *(p.325, Fig 17.14)*: Two-layer architecture. Frame of sources (agents/sensors) modelled as STN; frame of variables (the world) modelled as SBN. Conditional and evidence opinions in the SBN are obtained via fusion+discounting over the STN.
- **Key SN derivation pattern** *(p.324-325, Eq.17.35-17.36)*:
  1. Apply trust transitivity `⊗` for each source opinion: `ω_X^{[A;B]} = ω_B^A ⊗ ω_X^B`.
  2. Apply trust fusion `⊕` (or product) when multiple sources contribute to the same variable: `ω_X^{[A;B]⋄[A;C]} = ω_X^{[A;B]} ⊕ ω_X^{[A;C]}`.
  3. Apply SBN deduction `⊙` along the conditional chain to derive the target opinion.
- **Belief fusion only applies to source fusion in STN** *(p.326)*: Critical clarification — fusion operators of Chapter 12 are applied to combine sources of evidence in the STN frame; they are NOT applied to "fuse variables" or "fuse Bayesian classifiers" in the SBN frame. Confusing the two layers leads to incorrect models.
- **Vacuous-opinion incrementalism** *(p.323)*: Missing input opinions for conditionals or evidence nodes → set to vacuous; analysis proceeds incrementally as evidence becomes available. This is the propstore "honest ignorance" pattern at the SBN level.
- **Joint conditionals vs independent conditionals** *(p.326)*: When the analyst lacks the joint conditional `ω_{Z|XY}` and only has `ω_{Z|X}` and `ω_{Z|Y}`, the analyst must construct (or learn) `ω_{Z|XY}` before applying subjective deduction; cannot simply combine the two via belief fusion.

## Criticisms of Prior Work

- *(p.303)*: Traditional BN limitation: "input arguments must be assigned precise probabilities... problematic in situations where probabilities can not be reliably elicited." → SBN remedy with conditional subjective opinions expressing uncertain probabilities.
- *(p.305)*: Bayesian fusion models (Pearl-style) ≠ belief fusion of Chapter 12. Using fusion operators on Fig.17.2.a would be "approximation at best but ... incorrect in general." Only joint conditionals correctly express the dependence structure.
