The canonical book-length specification of subjective logic — an algebra of opinions over Beta/Dirichlet distributions that generalises probabilistic logic by adding an explicit uncertainty mass `u` and a base-rate distribution `a`, providing closed-form operators for addition, subtraction, multiplication, comultiplication, division, deduction, abduction, Bayes inversion, joint, fusion (BCF/CBF/ABF/WBF/CCF), unfusion, fission, trust transitivity, and Bayesian network reasoning. Establishes that vacuous opinions `(b=0, u=1, a)` are the formal carrier of "I don't know," that `W=2` is the default non-informative prior weight, that the marginal base rate (MBR) is required for repeated Bayes inversion, that Dempster's rule is properly understood as constraint fusion (not cumulative), and that subjective networks combining a frame of sources (STN) with a frame of variables (SBN) cleanly separate trust transitivity from probabilistic deduction. Direct foundation for propstore's honest-ignorance principle, vacuous-status provenance, fusion-operator selection, and any reasoning surface that needs to express and propagate uncertainty separately from probability.
