# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim, from Preface, p.x)

The name 'subjective logic' is short and elegant, but what is the intention behind it? The adjective 'subjective' refers to the aspects: 1) that opinions are held by individuals, and in general do not represent collective beliefs, and 2) that opinions represent beliefs that can be affected by uncertainty. The noun 'logic' refers to the aspect that subjective logic operators generalise both binary logic operators and probabilistic logic operators.

The fundamental innovation of subjective logic is to generalise the aforementioned operators, by including second-order uncertainty in the form of uncertainty mass. Concepts that are already described in Bayesian theory and probabilistic logic must then be redefined in terms of subjective logic, by including the uncertainty dimension. Explicit representation of uncertainty also opens up the possibility of defining totally new formal reasoning concepts, such as trust fusion and transitivity.

The present book harmonises notations and formalisms of previously published papers and articles. This book also improves, and corrects whenever appropriate, descriptions of operators that have previously been published.

The advantages of using subjective logic are that real-world situations can be realistically modelled with regard to how those situations are perceived, and that conclusions more correctly reflect the ignorance and uncertainties that necessarily result from partially uncertain input arguments. It is my wish that researchers and practitioners will advance, improve and apply subjective logic to build powerful artificial reasoning models and tools for solving real-world problems.

— Audun Jøsang, Oslo, March 2016

## Our Interpretation

This book is the canonical specification of subjective logic — an algebra of opinions over Beta and Dirichlet distributions that generalises binary and probabilistic logic by adding an explicit second-order uncertainty mass alongside belief and a base-rate distribution. It defines closed-form operators for the standard logical and probabilistic operations (addition, multiplication, deduction, abduction, Bayes inversion), introduces fusion operators (BCF, CBF, ABF, WBF, CCF) selected by source-relationship type, develops trust networks with probability-sensitive discounting and conflict-aware revision, and integrates everything into a two-layer subjective network architecture (frame of sources + frame of variables) for distributed Bayesian reasoning. Directly relevant to propstore as the formal foundation for honest-ignorance provenance, vacuous-opinion construction, multi-source fusion, and any reasoning surface that must propagate uncertainty separately from probability.
