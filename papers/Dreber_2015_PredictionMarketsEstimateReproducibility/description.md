Applies prediction markets to forecast reproducibility of 44 psychology studies in the Reproducibility Project, finding 71% accuracy and well-calibrated probability estimates. Market prices outperform researcher surveys and provide crowd-aggregated measures of replication likelihood. Relevant to propstore's calibration pipeline and opinion algebra as an empirical demonstration that incentivized belief aggregation produces well-calibrated probability estimates suitable for evidence-based uncertainty representation.
