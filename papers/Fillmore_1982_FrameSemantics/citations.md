## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Minsky (1975)** — "A framework for representing knowledge": AI origin of the frame concept that Fillmore adapts for linguistics. Essential for understanding the computational lineage of frames.
- **Rosch (1975)** — "Cognitive representations of semantic categories": Prototype theory that Fillmore integrates into frame semantics. Explains why frame membership is graded rather than binary.
- **Fillmore (1977a)** — "The case for case reopened": Fillmore's own updated case grammar, bridging the gap between his earlier syntactic work and the frame semantics program.
- **Schank & Abelson (1977)** — "Scripts, Plans, Goals, and Understanding": Parallel knowledge representation framework in AI that provides computational counterpart to Fillmore's linguistic frames.
- **Goffman (1974)** — "Frame Analysis": Sociological frame concept that influenced Fillmore's thinking about how situations are structured and interpreted.
