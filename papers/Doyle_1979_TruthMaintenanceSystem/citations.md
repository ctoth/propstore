## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Stallman and Sussman [53]** (1977) "Forward reasoning and dependency-directed backtracking" -- The direct predecessor to the TMS; introduced dependency-directed backtracking in the context of circuit analysis. Doyle's TMS generalizes and extends this work into a domain-independent subsystem.
- **McDermott and Doyle [35]** (1978) "Non-monotonic logic I" -- Formalizes the logic underlying TMS-style non-monotonic reasoning. Essential for understanding the theoretical foundations.
- **McAllester [30,31]** (1978,1979) "Three-valued truth maintenance system" and "Use of equality in deduction" -- Alternative TMS designs with cleaner organization; McAllester's work influenced later TMS designs including de Kleer's ATMS.
- **De Kleer and Sussman [12]** (1978) "Propagation of constraints applied to circuit synthesis" -- Key application of dependency tracking to constraint propagation, which evolved into the ATMS constraint language.
- **De Kleer, Doyle, Steele, and Sussman [11]** (1977) "Explicit control of reasoning" -- The AMORD system implementing explicit control of reasoning via pattern-invoked procedures controlled by the TMS.
