Defines the rational closure of a conditional knowledge base — the unique smallest rational consequence relation extending K — together with representation theorems linking System-P-plus-Rational-Monotonicity to ranked preferential models, Adams probabilistic entailment, and non-standard probabilistic semantics.
The paper proves rational closure is computationally tractable (NP-relative-to-SAT, polynomial for Horn), exhibits cumulativity / reciprocity / loop properties, and demonstrates correct behavior on Nixon-diamond and Penguin-triangle examples while flagging the inheritance-failure limitation.
For propstore, this is the canonical reference for the rank-based defeasible-inference machinery, the chosen-extension policy at render time, and the principle that any reasonable nonmonotonic answer should be a rational superset of K̄.
