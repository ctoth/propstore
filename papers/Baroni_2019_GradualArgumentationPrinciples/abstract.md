The investigation of gradual evaluation methods in argumentation has received increasing attention in recent years, with studies devoted to various classes of frameworks and/or semantics. While it is commonplace, similar to the study of traditional (extension-based or labelling-based) semantics for abstract argumentation, to evaluate and compare gradual evaluation methods by making them pass considerations. First, we identify groups of conceptually related properties in the literature and, for the targeted family of quantitative bipolar argumentation frameworks, we propose a formulation of general principles, called groups of principles or GPs, in each group such that the instances of the GPs capture the instances of the relevant properties in that group. Then, we provide a simplifying and unifying perspective for these groups of properties by showing that all of the GPs are implied by two overarching principles (namely, balance (for strict) balance and monotonicity). Finally, we show that (instances of) these principles (and those for the various formulated and non-captured properties) can be used to classify gradual argumentation semantics, demonstrating the general validity and utility to support a compact, yet comprehensive, analysis of properties of gradual argumentation semantics.
