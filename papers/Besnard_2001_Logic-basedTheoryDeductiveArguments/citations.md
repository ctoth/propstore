## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[5] Dung 1995** — Abstract argumentation framework. Already in project. This paper's Section 9.4 shows the fundamental incompatibility between Dung's abstract attacks and logic-based attacks (Theorem 9.24). Critical context for understanding when to use abstract vs structured argumentation.
- **[22] Simari & Loui 1992** — Mathematical treatment of defeasible reasoning. Already in project. Provides the specificity-based preference ordering that complements Besnard & Hunter's framework.
- **[25] Vreeswijk 1997** — Abstract argumentation systems. Already in project. Provides an alternative abstraction of argumentation that Besnard & Hunter compare against.
- **[20] Prakken & Vreeswijk** — Logical systems for defeasible argumentation. Comprehensive handbook chapter surveying the landscape. Worth reading for the broader context of where Besnard & Hunter's approach fits.
- **[7] Chesnevar, Maguitman, Loui** — Logical models of argumentation (ACM Computing Surveys). Comprehensive survey of logical argumentation models including this one.
