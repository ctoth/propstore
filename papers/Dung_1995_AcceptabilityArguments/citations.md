## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[7] Bondarenko, Toni and Kowalski (1993)** --- Develops an assumption-based framework that unifies many nonmonotonic approaches; directly extends Dung's work and is the basis of ABA (Assumption-Based Argumentation).
- **[52] Reiter (1980)** --- Default logic; Dung proves that R-extensions correspond exactly to stable extensions of the corresponding argumentation framework. Already in collection as Reiter_1980_DefaultReasoning.
- **[47] Pollock (1994)** --- Justification and defeat; Dung shows Pollock's indefeasible arguments correspond to the grounded extension. Important for understanding the relationship between philosophical and formal accounts of defeasibility.
- **[22] Gelfond and Lifschitz (1988)** --- Stable model semantics for logic programs; the correspondence between stable models and stable extensions of argumentation frameworks is a key result of this paper.
- **[62] Von Neuman and Morgenstern** --- Theory of Games; Dung shows NM-solutions are stable extensions, providing a deep connection between game theory and argumentation.
