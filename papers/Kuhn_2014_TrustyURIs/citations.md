## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[4] Callahan et al. 2013 - Bio2RDF release 2**: Major application of trusty URIs to life sciences linked data with >1 billion triples. Directly demonstrates the practical value of content-addressed URIs at scale.
- **[7] Farrell et al. 2013 - RFC 6920 (ni-URIs)**: The Named Information URI standard that trusty URIs extend and improve upon. Important for understanding the standards landscape.
- **[6] Carroll 2003 - Signing RDF Graphs**: Earlier work on RDF graph signing/verification. Relevant background for understanding the design space.
- **[11] Kuhn et al. 2013 - Broadening the Scope of Nanopublications**: The nanobrowser application that uses trusty URIs in practice. Demonstrates the self-referencing and verification workflow.
- **[14] Pua and Wagner 2004 - Security considerations for incremental hash functions**: Relevant to understanding the security properties and limitations of the hash-based approach.
