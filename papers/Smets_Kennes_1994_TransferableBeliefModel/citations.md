## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[33] Shafer 1976** — *A Mathematical Theory of Evidence*. The foundational text the entire TBM is reinterpreting; mandatory reading for anyone implementing belief functions.
- **[39] Smets 1988** *Belief functions* — establishes open-world / unnormalised belief functions, the formal cousin of ATMS no-good handling.
- **[42] Smets 1990** *Constructing the pignistic probability function in a context of uncertainty* — alternative axiomatic justification for `BetP`; complementary to Theorem 3.1.
- **[48] Smets 1993** *Belief functions: the disjunctive rule of combination and the generalized Bayesian theorem* — provides combination/conditioning operators that the TBM paper defers to.
- **[52] Wilson 1993** *Decision making with belief functions and pignistic probabilities* — the `[bel, pl]` envelope result and lower-probability characterisation of `bel(A)` over betting frames.
- **[8] Dubois & Prade 1991** *Focusing versus updating in belief function theory* — the revision-vs-focusing distinction propstore needs at the AGM/render boundary.
- **[15] Halpern & Fagin 1992** *Two views of belief: belief as generalized probability and belief as evidence* — independent critical reading of belief-function semantics.
- **[24] Levi 1983** *Consonance, dissonance and evidentiary mechanisms* — the criticism TBM dissolves; useful for understanding what `P(t_i|θ)` claims TBM avoids.
- **[44] Smets 1991** *Varieties of ignorance* — supports the "uncertainty is polymorphous; no single model fits all" thesis directly relevant to propstore's multi-strategy render layer.
- **[40] Smets 1990** *The combination of evidence in the TBM* (IEEE TPAMI) — the companion paper that handles Dempster's rule of combination.
