Smets and Kennes present the Transferable Belief Model (TBM), a non-probabilistic two-level model of quantified beliefs in which a credal level uses belief functions and Dempster's rule of conditioning, and a pignistic level derives a probability `BetP(x;m) = Σ_{x⊆A} m(A)/|A|` only when a decision is forced.
The pignistic transformation is uniquely characterised by four axioms (linearity in mass, continuity, anonymity, impossible-atom irrelevance), Dutch books are blocked because the betting frame fixes the granularity of stakes, and four paradigms (Mr. Jones, guards-and-posts, translators, unreliable sensor) discriminate TBM from Bayesian, ULP, likelihood, and fiducial approaches.
This paper is the canonical foundation for propstore's no-fabricated-confidence discipline: vacuous belief is the unique honest representation of ignorance, the credal/pignistic split maps onto propstore's lazy-until-rendering architecture, and the mass-not-split principle directly motivates the rule that disagreement must not be collapsed in storage.
