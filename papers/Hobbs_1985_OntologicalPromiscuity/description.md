Hobbs proposes a flat, first-order, nonintensional logical notation for discourse interpretation in which every n-ary predicate `p` has a companion `p'` taking an extra "eventuality/condition" argument, with real-world existence expressed as a separate predicate `Exist` over a Platonic universe containing all nameable entities.
Working through opaque adverbials, de re/de dicto belief reports, and Frege's Evening-Star/Morning-Star puzzle, the paper shows that ontological promiscuity — admitting possible, impossible, abstract, and nonexistent entities — plus nominalization plus a restricted real-world identity relation `rw-identical` recovers enough expressiveness to support naïve compositional semantic translation without functions, nested quantifiers, or modal operators.
Directly underwrites propstore's concept/eventuality layer, honest-ignorance principle (existence as a first-class predicate separable from reference), and the preference to store rival co-referring normalizations under a real-world-identity relation rather than collapsing them by heuristic.
