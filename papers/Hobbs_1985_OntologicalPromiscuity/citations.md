## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[2] Davidson 1967** — *The Logical Form of Action Sentences*. Directly the paper Hobbs generalizes. Davidson reifies events for action sentences; Hobbs extends this to every predicate. Mandatory reading for anyone implementing the `p/p'` nominalization discipline.

- **[7] Hobbs 1983** — *An Improper Treatment of Quantification in Ordinary English*. Companion paper that handles the quantifier problem this paper defers (universal variables as typical elements, dependency functions for scope, indices on predicates). Needed to close the gap in Ontological Promiscuity.

- **[8] McCarthy 1977** — *Epistemological Problems of AI*. Hobbs credits McCarthy (footnote p.63) for a similar nominalization technique. McCarthy's later `ist(c, p)` context formalism is named in propstore's CLAUDE.md as a direct influence; this earlier paper is the precursor.

- **[11] Moore & Hendrix 1982** — *Computational Models of Belief and the Semantics of Belief Sentences*. Source of the canonical de re spy example; alternative framework for belief that Hobbs is positioning against.

- **[18] Zalta 1983** — *Abstract Objects: An Introduction to Axiomatic Metaphysics*. Substrate for Hobbs's third-solution / metonymic treatment of Frege's puzzle via abstract-object function `α`. Directly useful if propstore adopts sense/intension objects for co-referring-but-distinct concepts.

- **[14] Quine 1956** — *Quantifiers and Propositional Attitudes*. The scope-ambiguity position Hobbs argues against. Required reading to understand what Hobbs's ontological promiscuity replaces.

- **[4] Frege 1892** — *On Sense and Nominatum*. The sense/reference distinction that generates the Evening-Star puzzle Hobbs resolves with `rw-identical`.
