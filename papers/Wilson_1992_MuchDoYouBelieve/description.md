Wilson defends Dempster-Shafer theory against Pearl's criticisms by interpreting Bel as the infimum (lower probability) over a family of compatible probability functions, contrasting Dempster's "irrelevance"-default conditioning with Bayesian/FH conditioning, and arguing that Pearl's sandwich principle is incompatible with any adequate representation of ignorance.
The paper provides a hidden-condition semantics `(n ∧ a) → b` with `Pr(n)=α` for D-S if-then rules, recovering contraposition, chaining, and reasoning-by-cases under independence assumptions while exposing five problems with Pearl's conditional-probability reading of rules.
This is the canonical [Bel, Pl] envelope reference for propstore's honest-ignorance discipline and provides direct theoretical grounding for the defeasible-rule layer's hidden-context interpretation of rule strength.
