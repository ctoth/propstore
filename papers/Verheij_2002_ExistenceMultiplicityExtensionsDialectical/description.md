Characterizes the existence and multiplicity of stable-type extensions in dialectical argumentation using the notion of dialectical justification within the DEFLOG framework. The main result shows that extension existence and count can be determined by checking whether sentences are dialectically justifiable, defeatable, or ambiguous — a close cousin of Dung's admissibility. The results translate directly to Dung argumentation frameworks, default logic, and logic programming.
