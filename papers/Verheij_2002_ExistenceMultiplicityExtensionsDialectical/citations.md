## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Dung, P.M. (1995)** — "On the acceptability of arguments..." — The foundational AF paper; Verheij's results directly extend Dung's admissibility notion. Already in propstore corpus.

2. **Bondarenko et al. (1997)** — "An abstract, argumentation-theoretic approach to default reasoning" — Assumption-based argumentation frameworks; Verheij connects his stable extension results to these. Relevant to propstore's ASPIC+ layer.

3. **Verheij (2000a)** — "DefLog — a logic of dialectical justification and defeat" — The full DEFLOG system; needed to implement the dialectical justification checking that characterizes extension existence.

4. **Gelfond & Lifschitz (1988)** — "The stable model semantics for logic programming" — Origin of stable model semantics; Verheij shows formal connections between DEFLOG extensions and stable models.

5. **Prakken & Vreeswijk (2002)** — "Logics for Defeasible Argumentation" — Comprehensive overview of argumentation logics; provides broader context for DEFLOG's place among argumentation systems.
