## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Walton, D., Reed, C., & Macagno, F. (2008). Argumentation schemes. Cambridge University Press.** The foundational reference containing the full set of 60+ argumentation schemes that this paper classifies. Essential for any implementation of scheme-based argument analysis.
- **Kienpointner, M. (1992). Alltagslogik.** The major prior classification system that this paper extends and revises. Important for understanding the taxonomic decisions made.
- **Rahwan, I. et al. (2011). Representation of argument schemes in the Argument Interchange Format.** Describes the computational representation of schemes in AIF, directly relevant to encoding the classification in a knowledge base.
- **Palau, R. M. & Moens, M. F. (2009). Argumentation mining.** Foundational paper on argument mining from legal texts, directly relevant to automated extraction using scheme classification.
- **Macagno, F. & Walton, D. (2014). Argumentation schemes and topical relations.** Extends the classification with topical relations, relevant for understanding scheme interconnections.
