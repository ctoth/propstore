Presents a hierarchical classification system for argumentation schemes organized by source-dependency (source-based vs source-independent), reasoning type (epistemic vs practical), and inferential structure (rule-based vs case-based), with formal premise-conclusion representations and critical questions for each scheme.
The taxonomy integrates prior systems (Kienpointner 1992, Walton 1996, Walton et al. 2008) into a unified tree structure covering approximately 28 scheme types across four top-level categories, validated empirically on 388 arguments from political discourse.
Directly relevant for the propstore's claim representation and argument mining capabilities, providing a canonical ontology of argument types that can inform how claims, supports, and attacks are classified and structured in the knowledge base.
