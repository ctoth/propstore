This paper presents a labelling-oriented framework for probabilistic argumentation that unifies rule-based structured argumentation with (semi-)abstract accounts under diverse types of uncertainty. The framework introduces extended label sets — {ON, OFF} for structural uncertainty about argument inclusion and {IN, OUT, UN} for acceptance uncertainty — which can be combined into {IN, OUT, UN, OFF} labellings, then lifted to probabilistic labelling frames (PLFs) that assign probability distributions over labellings. The authors show that PLFs subsume both the "constellations" approach (Li et al. 2012) and the "epistemic" approach (Hunter & Thimm 2013) as special cases, while also enabling finer-grained probabilistic evaluation of both arguments and their conclusions through statement labellings.
