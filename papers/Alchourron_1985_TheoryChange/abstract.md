# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

This paper extends earlier work by its authors on formal aspects of the processes of contracting a theory to eliminate a proposition and revising a theory to introduce a proposition. In the course of the earlier work, Gardenfors developed general postulates of a more or less mandatory nature for such operations, and})})}) the present paper studies operations that satisfy all the basic Gardenfors postulates together. Principal results are: (1) Representation theorems, showing that a class of operations referred to as "partial meet contraction functions" is precisely the class of operations satisfying the Gardenfors basic postulates for contraction, and similarly for partial meet revision functions. (2) The supplementary Gardenfors postulates are studied and it is shown that they correspond to a special class of partial meet functions, the transitively relational ones. (3) Relations between revision and contraction functions are explored: the Levi identity defines revision in terms of contraction and expansion, while the Harper identity defines contraction in terms of revision.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper formalizes what it means for a rational agent to give up a belief (contraction) or to accept new possibly conflicting information (revision). The central result is a representation theorem: the abstract rationality postulates for contraction correspond exactly to a concrete construction (intersecting selected maximal consistent subsets). This provides the theoretical foundation for belief revision in any system that manages changing knowledge, including ATMS-based architectures like the propstore.
