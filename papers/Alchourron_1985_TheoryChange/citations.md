## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[5] Gardenfors (1988) "Knowledge in Flux"** -- The book-length treatment of AGM theory, expanding the postulates and introducing epistemic entrenchment as a constructive mechanism for contraction. Essential follow-up.
- **[6] Gardenfors and Makinson (1988)** -- Introduces the epistemic entrenchment ordering (EE1-EE5), which is the mechanism Dixon (1993) uses to bridge ATMS and AGM. Already referenced in our Dixon paper.
- **[1] Alchourron and Makinson (1982)** -- The predecessor paper, developing the contraction function framework that this paper extends. Useful for tracing the intellectual development.
- **[2] Alchourron and Makinson (1985)** -- Companion paper on "safe contraction," an alternative construction to partial meet contraction where beliefs are ordered by relative safety.
- **[7] Makinson (1985)** -- Survey by one of the AGM authors, providing broader context and connections to related work in formal epistemology.
