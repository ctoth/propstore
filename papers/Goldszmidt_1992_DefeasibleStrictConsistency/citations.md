## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Adams 1975** — *The Logic of Conditionals*: Foundational epsilon-semantics that Goldszmidt & Pearl build upon for their probabilistic interpretation of defaults. Essential background for understanding why P(b|a) > 0.5 captures "typically."

2. **Lehmann & Magidor 1992** — *Rational Logics and their Models*: Defines System Z and preferential semantics. The paper proves equivalence of p-entailment to System Z, making this the key bridge between probabilistic and model-theoretic approaches.

3. **Pearl 1988** — *Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems*: The broader probabilistic AI framework within which this consistency work sits. Already in our collection context.

4. **Reiter 1980** — *A Logic for Default Reasoning*: The classical default logic paper. Understanding how Goldszmidt & Pearl's consistency notion improves on Reiter's extensions is important for propstore's defeasible reasoning.

5. **Gärdenfors 1988** — *Knowledge in Flux*: AGM belief revision framework. The connection between consistency of defeasible databases and belief revision operations is relevant to propstore's revision layer.
