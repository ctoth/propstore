## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[28] Lehmann, D., Magidor, M. (1992) — "What does a conditional knowledge base entail?"** The original rational closure paper. Defines the desiderata (i)–(iv) the construction is required to satisfy, and the preferential closure P(B) / rational closure R(B) operators. Essential for verifying Proposition 1.
- **[19] Freund, M. (1998) — "Preferential reasoning in the perspective of Poole default logic."** Theorem 24 is the formal scaffolding: Casini–Straccia's construction is "an analogous of" it, extended to a non-empty background theory T using results from Bochman.
- **[6] Bochman, A. (2001) — "A logical theory of nonmonotonic inference and belief change."** Provides the *clash* construction (Cor. 7.5.2, Def. 7.5.2, Lemma 7.5.5, definition on p.175) used to justify Step 4.2's identification of ∞-rank sequents with the strict residue T̃.
- **[26] Kraus, Lehmann, Magidor (1990) — "Nonmonotonic reasoning, preferential models and cumulative logics."** KLM properties REF–OR (preferential consequence) without RM; foundational background for the rule schema on p.78.
- **[11, 23] Britz/Heidema/Meyer; Giordano et al.** The closest related DL work, explicitly contrasted in this paper. Their orderings encode *typicality among individuals*; this paper's order encodes only *priority of attention*.
- **[16] Donini, F.M., Massacci, F. (2000) — "Exptime tableaux for ALC."** Source of the EXPTIME-complete bound used in Corollary 2.
- **[2] Baader, Brandt, Lutz (2005) — "Pushing the EL envelope."** Justifies the polynomial-time claim for rational closure inheritance over EL.
