# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

This article extends Defeasible Logic to deal with the contextual deliberation process of cognitive agents. First, we introduce meta-rules to reason with rules. Meta-rules are rules that have as a consequent rules for motivational components, such as obligations, intentions and desires. In other words, they include nested rules. Second, we introduce explicit preferences among rules. They deal with complex structures where nested rules can be involved.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper extends Defeasible Logic with modal operators (BEL, OBL, INT, DES) and lifts the ⊗-preference connective from literals to whole rules so that *meta-rules* — rules whose consequents are rules — can describe context-dependent fallback chains over an agent's deliberation rules. Proof theory is given for both literals and rules with new tags (±Δ_C^{▷X}, ±∂_C^{▷X}), supported by definitions of sub-rules, modal-free rules, rule-supporting sets, maximal provable-rule-sets, two notions of incompatibility, and conversions c ⊆ MOD × MOD that allow one modality's rule to act as another's. Two worked examples (a Tolkienian "Frodo" agent and an office-assistant context-detector) illustrate how contextual information enables certain rules and changes priority between deliberations.
