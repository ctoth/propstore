## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Reiter 1980** (ref [11]): A logic for default reasoning — the companion NMR paper published in the same special issue of Artificial Intelligence. Reiter's default logic is the other major non-monotonic formalism alongside circumscription; essential for understanding the landscape of NMR approaches.
- **Davis 1980** (ref [3]): Notes on the mathematics of non-monotonic reasoning — formal mathematical analysis of non-monotonic reasoning, also in the same issue. Provides the mathematical foundations that McCarthy's model-theoretic section (Section 6) builds on.
- **McCarthy & Hayes 1969** (ref [7]): Some philosophical problems from the standpoint of AI — introduces the frame problem and situation calculus that circumscription is designed to address. Essential background for understanding the motivation.
- **McCarthy 1977** (ref [8]): Epistemological problems of AI — earlier formulation of the qualification problem that circumscription solves. Contains the original statement of the problem that this paper formalizes.
