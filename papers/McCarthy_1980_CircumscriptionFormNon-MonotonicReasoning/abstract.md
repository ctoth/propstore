# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

Humans and intelligent computer programs must often jump to the conclusion that the objects they can determine to have certain properties or relations are the only objects that do. Circumscription formalizes such conjectural reasoning.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper addresses the "qualification problem" — classical first-order logic is monotonic and cannot capture the common sense reasoning pattern of assuming only known entities and conditions are relevant. McCarthy introduces circumscription, a sentence schema that formalizes this minimization as a rule of conjecture (not inference), enabling non-monotonic reasoning within first-order logic. This is foundational for any system that must reason defeasibly about incomplete knowledge, directly relevant to argumentation frameworks where conclusions can be retracted when new evidence appears.
