## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Gardenfors (1984)** "Epistemic importance and minimal changes of belief" — The belief set approach that OCFs improve upon; directly relevant to understanding what OCFs add beyond AGM-style revision.
- **Harper (1976)** "Rational belief change, Popper functions and counterfactuals" — Connection between Popper measures and OCFs; explains why OCFs are the qualitative counterpart of probability.
- **Shackle (1969)** *Decision, Order, and Time in Human Affairs* — Functions of potential surprise that OCFs subsume; important for understanding the FPS-OCF correspondence.
- **Spohn (1988b)** "Stochastic independence, causal independence, and shieldability" — Companion paper with the independence theory that complements the conditionalization theory here.
- **Lewis (1973)** *Counterfactuals* — Similarity spheres / systems of spheres that provide an alternative representation of epistemic states; OCFs can be seen as a more tractable version.
