Presents a formal Bayesian framework computing the positive predictive value (PPV) of research findings as a function of pre-study odds, statistical power, significance threshold, and bias. Demonstrates through six corollaries and numerical examples that for most study designs and practical settings, claimed research findings are more likely false than true. Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer as a principled source of base-rate calibration for claim credibility and as a framework for generating structured attacks against claims from underpowered, biased, or exploratory research.
