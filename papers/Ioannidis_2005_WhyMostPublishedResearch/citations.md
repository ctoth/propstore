## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Wacholder et al. 2004 (ref 10):** "Assessing the probability that a positive report is false" — introduces the False Positive Report Probability (FPRP) approach, a complementary framework to Ioannidis's PPV for molecular epidemiology. Directly relevant to calibrating claim base rates.
- **Ioannidis 2005 (ref 36):** "Contradicted and initially stronger effects in highly cited clinical research" — empirical validation showing that highly cited clinical findings are frequently contradicted or found to have smaller effects, providing real-world evidence for the theoretical PPV framework.
- **Sterne & Davey Smith 2001 (ref 9):** "Sifting the evidence—What's wrong with significance tests" — foundational critique of p-value reasoning that complements the PPV framework.
- **Lindley 1957 (ref 32):** "A statistical paradox" — Lindley's paradox showing divergence between Bayesian and frequentist conclusions, theoretically underpinning the disconnect Ioannidis formalizes.
- **Yusuf et al. 1984 (ref 14):** "Why do we need some large, simple randomized trials?" — argues for the study designs that Ioannidis's framework shows have highest PPV.
