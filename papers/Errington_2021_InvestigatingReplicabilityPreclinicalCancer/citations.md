## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Open Science Collaboration, 2015** - The foundational psychology replication study; direct methodological comparison for cross-discipline replication rates
- **Begley and Ellis, 2012** - Prior estimate of 11% replication in preclinical cancer biology; important context for understanding the RPCB results
- **Ioannidis, 2005** - "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False" - theoretical framework explaining publication bias and inflated effect sizes
- **Nosek et al., 2021** - Cross-discipline review of replicability, robustness, and reproducibility; situates cancer biology findings in broader context
- **Mathur and VanderWeele, 2020b** - New statistical metrics for multisite replication projects; methodological foundation for some of the analyses used
