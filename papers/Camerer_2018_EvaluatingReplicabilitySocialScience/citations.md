## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Open Science Collaboration (2015)** [ref 7]: The RPP -- replicated 100 psychology experiments with 36% replication rate. Direct comparison project to this one; essential for understanding field-specific replication rates.
- **Camerer et al. (2016)** [ref 8]: EERP -- replicated 18 experimental economics studies with 61% rate. Companion study by same lead authors; provides economics-specific base rates.
- **Dreber et al. (2015)** [ref 46]: Used prediction markets to forecast replication outcomes in RPP. Establishes that prediction markets are viable for assessing scientific claim reliability.
- **Patil, Peng & Leek (2016)** [ref 29]: Prediction interval method for assessing replicability. Complementary statistical framework to p-value-based replication assessment.
- **Simonsohn (2015)** [ref 30]: Small Telescopes approach -- alternative criterion for replication that avoids dichotomous thinking about significance.
