## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Gordon & Karacapilidis [14]** — "The Zeno argumentation framework" — foundational predecessor to HERMES that established the argumentation model. Relevant because Zeno appears to have more formal argumentation semantics than HERMES.

2. **Farley & Freeman [7]** — "Burden of proof in legal argumentation" — source of the four proof standards (scintilla of evidence, preponderance, beyond reasonable doubt, dialectical validity) used in HERMES. Directly relevant to propstore's resolution strategies.

3. **Simari & Loui [23]** — "A mathematical treatment of defeasible reasoning" — formal treatment of defeasible reasoning that could bridge between HERMES' informal scoring and Dung-style formal argumentation.

4. **Kunz & Rittel [15]** — "Issues as elements of information systems" — the original IBIS paper that HERMES extends. Understanding the IBIS model helps contextualize what HERMES adds.

5. **Walton [25]** — "Argumentation Schemes for Presumptive Reasoning" — argumentation schemes could formalize the types of positions and constraints used in HERMES.
