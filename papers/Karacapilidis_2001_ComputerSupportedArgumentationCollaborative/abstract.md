## Abstract

Collaborative decision making problems can be addressed through argumentative discourse and collaboration among the ones involved. Consensus is achieved through the process of collaboratively considering alternative understandings of the problem, competing interests, priorities and constraints. The application of formal modelling and analysis tools to aid/s the related processes is impossible before the problem can be articulated in a concise and agreed upon manner. This paper describes HERMES, a system that addresses collaborative decision making by supporting argumentative discourse among decision makers. It is fully implemented in Java and runs on the Web, thus providing a wide variety of capabilities for accessing, managing, sharing, and allocating knowledge resources. We describe the formal model of argumentation elements, discourse and reasoning mechanisms involved in HERMES. We also describe the integration of advanced features in the system, those which serve to retrieve data stored in its remote databases in order to further warrant their arguments, and stimulate them to perform acts that best reflect their interests and intentions.

## Our Interpretation

HERMES represents an early (2001) attempt to bridge informal argumentation discourse and formal multi-criteria decision analysis within a single web-based system. Its proof standards (scintilla of evidence, preponderance, beyond reasonable doubt, dialectical validity) anticipate what propstore implements as resolution strategies at the render layer. The system's constraint consistency checking and weighted scoring mechanism, while less formal than Dung-style extension computation, demonstrate a practical approach to detecting and surfacing disagreement without premature commitment to a single resolution.
