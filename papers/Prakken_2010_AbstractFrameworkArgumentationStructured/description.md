Presents the ASPIC framework for argumentation with structured arguments, defining arguments as inference trees built from strict and defeasible rules with three attack types (undermining, rebutting, undercutting) that instantiate Dung's abstract argumentation frameworks.
The paper proves that rationality postulates (closure under strict rules, direct and indirect consistency) hold for well-formed argumentation theories under closure conditions, and shows that assumption-based argumentation is a special case.
This is the direct precursor to ASPIC+ (Modgil and Prakken 2014) and provides the formal foundations for structured argument construction and preference-based defeat resolution used throughout the argumentation literature.
