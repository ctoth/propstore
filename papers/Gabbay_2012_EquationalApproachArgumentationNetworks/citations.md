# Citations: Equational approach to argumentation networks

## Papers Cited (selected key references)

- **Dung (1995)** - Foundational abstract argumentation frameworks (S, R_A) with grounded/preferred/stable/complete extensions. The paper this work generalizes.
- **Caminada & Gabbay (2009)** - Logical account of formal argumentation; Caminada labelling (in/out/undecided) which Eq_max is proven equivalent to.
- **Barringer, Gabbay & Woods (2005, 2008)** - Earlier work on numerical argumentation networks with transmission factors, weighted nodes/arrows, higher level attacks, temporal dependence.
- **Brewka & Woltran (2010)** - Abstract Dialectical Frameworks (ADFs); Boolean conditions on nodes. Gabbay shows how Boolean networks relate to equational approach (Section 2.2).
- **Brewka, Dunne & Woltran (2011)** - Relating semantics of ADFs and standard AFs.
- **Boella, Gabbay, van der Torre & Villata (2010, 2011)** - Support in abstract argumentation; Boolean functions for attack/support.
- **Cayrol & Lagasquie-Schiex (2005, 2010)** - Bipolar argumentation frameworks with support and attack.
- **Dunne, Hunter, McBurney, Parsons & Wooldridge (2011)** - Weighted argument systems; compared with Gabbay's approximation approach in Section 5.
- **Dvorak & Woltran (2011)** - Intertranslatability of argumentation semantics; related to critical translations in Section 2.2.
- **Modgil (2007, 2009)** - Abstract theory accommodating defeasible reasoning about preferences; attacks on attacks.
- **Baroni, Giacomi & Guida (2005)** - SCC-recursiveness as a general schema for argumentation semantics.
- **Baroni, Cerutti, Giacomin & Guida (2009)** - Encompassing attacks to attacks in abstract argumentation.
- **Gabbay & d'Avila Garcez (2009)** - Logical modes of attack in argumentation networks.
- **Gabbay & Szalas (2009)** - Annotation theories over finite graphs.
- **D'Agostino & Gabbay (2011)** - Algorithmics for argumentation.
- **Gratie & Florea (2011)** - Fuzzy labelling for argumentation frameworks.
- **Hanh, Dung & Thang (2010)** - Inductive defence for sceptical semantics.
- **Boole (1847)** - Mathematical Analysis of Logic; historical root of equational approach.
- **Couturat (1914)** - Algebra of Logic; historical root.
- **Schroeder (1890-1904)** - Vorlesungen uber die Algebra die Logik; historical root.

## Citation Context

This paper sits at the intersection of abstract argumentation theory and numerical/algebraic methods. It bridges Dung-style set-theoretic semantics with continuous mathematics, opening connections to neural networks, Bayesian networks, ecological networks, and flow networks. The equational approach provides a unifying formalism that subsumes Caminada labelling, bipolar frameworks with support, higher-level attacks, weighted systems, and temporal argumentation.
