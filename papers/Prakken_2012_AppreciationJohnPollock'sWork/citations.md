## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Dung (1995)** "On the Acceptability of Arguments and its Fundamental Role in Nonmonotonic Reasoning" - The foundational paper for abstract argumentation frameworks; essential for understanding the field Pollock's work connects to.
- **Modgil and Prakken (2011)** "A General Account of Argumentation with Preferences" - The ASPIC+ framework paper, positioned as the modern integration of Pollock's ideas with structured argumentation.
- **Pollock (1995)** "Cognitive Carpentry" - Pollock's most comprehensive account of his system; primary source for anyone implementing Pollock-style defeasible reasoning.
- **Walton, Reed, and Macagno (2008)** "Argumentation Schemes" - Connects Pollock's defeasible inference rules to practical argumentation patterns with critical questions.
- **Pollock (2009b)** "A Recursive Semantics for Defeasible Reasoning" - Pollock's last major technical paper refining his semantics, included in the Rahwan & Simari handbook.
