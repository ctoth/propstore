A survey and critical appreciation of John Pollock's contributions to computational argumentation, covering his formalisms for defeasible reasoning including inference graphs, undercutting vs rebutting defeat, argument strength, and self-defeat handling. The paper contextualizes Pollock's work within modern structured argumentation (particularly ASPIC+) and identifies both its lasting influence and its limitations for current research. Highly relevant as foundational context for understanding the theoretical roots of computational argumentation systems and the design choices behind defeat semantics and argumentation schemes.
