## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Stab and Gurevych 2014a** (COLING): The precursor annotation study that established the annotation scheme used in this paper. Essential for understanding the annotation guidelines and corpus construction methodology.
- **Peldszus and Stede 2015**: Joint prediction in MST-style discourse parsing for argumentation mining. Provides an alternative joint modeling approach using minimum spanning trees that could complement the ILP approach.
- **Eger, Daxenberger, and Gurevych 2017**: Neural end-to-end learning for computational argumentation mining. The direct successor to this work using neural methods (LSTMs, CNNs) instead of feature engineering.
- **Habernal and Gurevych 2016**: Argumentation mining in user-generated web discourse. Tests similar argumentation mining approaches on noisy web data, showing generalization challenges.
- **Rinott et al. 2015**: Context-dependent evidence detection. Complementary approach focused on evidence rather than full argumentation structures, with relevance to claim-evidence linking.
