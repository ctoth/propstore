# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The paper does not contain a separate abstract section. The opening paragraph (p.361) serves as the de facto introduction: The paper argues that a central limitation of strict Bayesianism is the assumption that a decision maker's knowledge can be represented by a unique subjective probability measure. The authors propose a decision theory that uses a class of probability measures together with a measure of epistemic reliability, and show that this yields a decision criterion (MMEU) which generalizes both Bayesian expected utility maximization and classical maximin.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper addresses the problem that probability estimates differ in how well-supported they are by available evidence, and strict Bayesianism ignores this distinction by requiring a single probability measure. Gardenfors and Sahlin propose representing beliefs via a set of possible probability distributions plus a reliability measure (rho) that captures how well-backed each distribution is, then filtering by reliability before applying a maximin expected utility criterion. This is relevant to propstore because the two-component model (distribution set + reliability) provides a principled foundation for representing epistemic uncertainty that maps onto Subjective Logic's uncertainty mass and the project's non-commitment discipline.
