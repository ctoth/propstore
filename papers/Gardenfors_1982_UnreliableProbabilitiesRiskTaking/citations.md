## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[8] Ellsberg 1961** - Source of the Ellsberg paradox (ambiguity aversion); foundational for understanding how epistemic reliability affects choices under ambiguity
- **[25] Keynes 1921** - Original concept of 'weight of evidence' as distinct from probability; Gardenfors and Sahlin explicitly parallel their epistemic reliability with Keynes's weight
- **[26] Levi 1974** - Most developed alternative theory using convex sets of 'permissible' probability measures; the main comparison target in Section 5
- **[27] Levi 1980** - Full theory of E-admissibility, P-admissibility, and S-admissibility; Gardenfors and Sahlin argue their MMEU is more realistic
- **[5] Dempster 1967** - Upper and lower probabilities from multivalued mappings; one of the earliest formal treatments of imprecise probabilities, relevant to propstore's belief function layer
