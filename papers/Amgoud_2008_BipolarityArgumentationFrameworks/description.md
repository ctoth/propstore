A comprehensive survey of bipolarity in argumentation frameworks, showing that the coexistence of positive (support) and negative (defeat) interactions appears at every level of the argumentation process. The paper formalizes the abstract bipolar argumentation framework (BAF) as a triple (A, R_def, R_sup) extending Dung's AF with an explicit support relation, and defines gradual valuation functions and acceptability semantics (conflict-free, admissible, preferred extensions) that account for both defeat and support, including the novel concept of "supported defeat" where support chains create new attack paths.
