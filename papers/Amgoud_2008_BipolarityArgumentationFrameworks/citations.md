## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **[CLS06] Cayrol and Lagasquie-Schiex (2006)** — "Bipolarity in argumentation" — Technical report with full BAF semantics details including algorithms for computing extensions. Most directly relevant for implementation.

2. **[MCLS05] Mailli, Cayrol, Lagasquie-Schiex (2005)** — "Bipolarity in argumentation: acceptability and algorithms" — Contains the algorithmic details for computing acceptability in BAFs that this survey paper references but does not detail.

3. **[Dun95] Dung (1995)** — "On the acceptability of arguments" — The foundational AF paper that BAFs extend. Already in propstore's literature base.

4. **[Ver02] Verheij (2002)** — "On the existence and multiplicity of extension in dialectical argumentation" — Proposes an alternative conflict notion for bipolar settings that avoids the "two arguments attacking a third" problem.

5. **[DP06] Dubois and Prade (2006)** — "On the qualitative comparison of sets of positive and negative affects" — The formal bipolarity taxonomy (Type 1/2/3) used throughout this paper; relevant for classifying different kinds of bipolar relationships in propstore.
