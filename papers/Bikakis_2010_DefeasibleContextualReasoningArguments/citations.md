## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[13] Ghidini & Giunchiglia 2001** — Founding Local Models Semantics for MCS. *Already in collection.*
- **[16] Brewka, Roelofsen & Serafini 2007** — The MCS proposal this paper most directly extends and criticizes; central comparator for any propstore re-implementation.
- **[24] Governatori, Maher, Antoniou & Billington 2004** — Argumentation Semantics for (single-context) Defeasible Logic that the multi-context argumentation here generalizes.
- **[25] Dung 1995** — The abstract argumentation framework underlying the entire attack/defeat machinery.
- **[27] Bikakis & Antoniou 2008 (RuleML)** — Earlier RuleML version of the same model; precursor to this journal paper.
- **[26] Antoniou, Maher & Billington 2000** — *Already in collection (Antoniou_2000_DefeasibleLogicVersusLogic).*

## New Leads (not yet in collection)

- Brewka, Roelofsen & Serafini 2007 — "Contextual Default Reasoning" (IJCAI). The MCS predecessor this paper criticizes; high priority.
- Governatori, Maher, Antoniou & Billington 2004 — "Argumentation Semantics for Defeasible Logic"; the formal substrate this paper lifts to multi-context.
- Roelofsen & Serafini 2005 — "Minimal and Absent Information in Contexts" (IJCAI); the consistent-context MCS variant being relaxed.
- Bikakis, Patkos, Antoniou, Plexousakis 2008 — Survey of context-reasoning approaches for AmI; would help propstore decide what other formalisms to ingest.
- Adjiman et al. 2006 — Distributed reasoning in P2P semantic-web setting; comparator for the distributed algorithm.
