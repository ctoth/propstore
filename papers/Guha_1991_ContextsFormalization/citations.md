## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **McCarthy [jmc 59]** — The informal proposal that Guha formalizes. Essential for understanding why `ist(c, p)` is structured as it is. Guha credits McCarthy with first introducing contexts in Advice Taker *(p.9)*, and McCarthy was his advisor. Later formalization appears in McCarthy (1993) "Notes on Formalizing Context." propstore's CLAUDE.md cites McCarthy 1993 directly.
2. **Lenat & Guha — Building Large Knowledge-Based Systems (Cyc book)** — Companion substrate; many examples in this thesis are real Cyc microtheories. SubAbstraction formalism referenced as deferred to the Cyc book.
3. **deKleer ATMS** — The other major "context as assumption set" tradition. Guha explicitly distinguishes from it; propstore's argumentation layer references ATMS.
4. **Forbus & Falkenhainer** — The most substantive prior work Guha compares to. Their "consider" mechanism is the closest-in-spirit precursor. Worth reading alongside Guha to understand the assumption-vs-context distinction.
5. **Grosz & Sidner Discourse Structure Theory** — Guha positions his utterance/discourse context hierarchy as meta to DST. Necessary for understanding the NL understanding portion (Ch.4.2).
