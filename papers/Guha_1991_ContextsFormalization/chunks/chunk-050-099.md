## Key Findings

### 3.3.1 Lift and Solve (p.50-53)
- Two components: Access Module (indexing/accessing assertions) and Inferencing Module *(p.50)*
- Lifting module determines what to lift and handles the lifting *(p.50)*
- Two orthogonal factors for importing assertions: **Relevance** and **Appropriateness** *(p.51-52)*
- Relevance: is info in c1 relevant to goals in c2? Based on relative relevance information *(p.52)*
- moreRelevantMt(Ct, AutoMt, BotanyMt) - relevance statements *(p.52)*
- Appropriateness: is theory in c1 accurate enough for goals of c2? *(p.52)*
- Policy of "risky" lifting: system does only cursory checking for consistency before lifting; if inconsistency discovered later during problem solving, backtracks *(p.52)*
- **Ambiguity/conflicts in lifting**: sometimes both P and G can be lifted but not both simultaneously *(p.53)*
