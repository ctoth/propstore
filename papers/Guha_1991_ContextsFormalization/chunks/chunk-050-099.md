### 3.3.1 Lift and Solve (p.50-53)
- Two components: Access Module (indexing/accessing assertions) and Inferencing Module *(p.50)*

### 3.13 Example 8: Domain assumptions (p.81-89)

#### 3.13.1 Kinds of assumptions (p.81-82)
- Context-level assumptions: fundamental, violation means NO assertions apply to object *(p.81)*
- Object-level assumptions: some assertions assume facts about objects; violations may still allow other assertions to apply *(p.82)*
- Business theory assumes business tries to achieve goals *(p.82)*
- Assumptions often unstatable in the context's own vocabulary *(p.82)*

#### 3.13.2 The Scope of a theory (p.82)
- **Scope of context = set of objects over which predictions hold = domain of context = denotation of forall in that context** *(p.82)*
- "Outside scope" = not element of domain of context; formally ¬presentIn(c, o) *(p.82)*
- When lifting (forall x p(x)) from origin to target: if PSC makes same assumption, no change; else formula must change *(p.82)*
- Earlier examples focused on changes to p(x); now focus on changes to forall *(p.82)*

#### 3.13.3 Scope and Lifting (p.82-83)
- Example A46: (forall x primaryCar(x car) ^ children(x y) -> hasFeatures(car FourDoorBody)) in AutoMt *(p.83)*
- AutoMt's forall does not include Fred; lifted to CSPSC must add conjunct:
- A47: (forall x ¬(equal x Fred) ^ primaryCar(x car) ^ children(x y) -> hasFeatures(car FourDoorBody)) *(p.83)*
- **General rule**: (forall x R(x)) lifts to (forall x P(x) -> R_1(x)) where P is the strongest assumption of the origin context that the target doesn't make; R_1 is the target-context analog of R *(p.83)*

#### 3.13.4 Representing Scope Assumptions (p.83-84)
- A48: ist(c_i ¬A_1(x)) => ¬presentIn(c_j x) *(p.83)*
- System assumes presentIn(AutoMt G002) unless explicitly told otherwise (maximize scope) *(p.83)*
- A49: ist(c_i ¬A(x)) <=> ¬presentIn(c_j x) — A is necessary and sufficient condition for object presence *(p.84)*
- Lifting: replace (forall x R(x)) with (forall x A(x) => R_1(x)) to compensate for scope difference *(p.84)*

#### 3.13.5 Obtaining the strongest assumption (p.84)
- "A" = conjunction of simple necessary conditions *(p.84)*
- Equivalent to maximizing extent of presentIn, i.e., assuming maximal scope (a priori reasonable) *(p.84)*

#### 3.13.6 Assumptions behind CSPSC (p.84)
