Proposes a continuous dynamical systems approach (quadratic energy model) for computing argument strengths in weighted bipolar argumentation frameworks, with proven convergence for acyclic graphs and strong empirical convergence for cyclic graphs.
The quadratic energy model satisfies key axiomatic properties (anonymity, independence, directionality, neutrality) and outperforms DF-QuAD and Euler-based models on convergence.
Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer as an alternative or complement to DF-QuAD for gradual semantics computation, especially for cyclic argumentation graphs.
