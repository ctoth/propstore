## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Malinin & Gales (2019)** — Prior Networks / Reverse KL divergence training for Dirichlet output networks. Directly relevant to propstore's evidential deep learning pipeline. The loss function used by SLEs is essentially the Malinin & Gales loss.
- **Uma et al. (2021)** — "Learning from Disagreement: A Survey" — Comprehensive survey of the field. Would provide broader context on how SLEs fit into the landscape of disagreement-aware learning.
- **Sensoy et al. (2018)** — Already in propstore collection. SLEs extend this work by learning from annotation uncertainty rather than just modeling prediction uncertainty.
- **Jøsang (2016)** — The SL textbook. The operators (cumulative fusion, trust discounting) used in SLEs come from this source. propstore implements the 2001 paper; this 2016 book is the comprehensive reference.
- **Davani et al. (2022)** — Dealing with disagreements in computational linguistics. Relevant context for propstore's multi-source evidence handling.
