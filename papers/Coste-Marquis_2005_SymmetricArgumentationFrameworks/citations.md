## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Dung 1995 [5]**: The foundational paper for argumentation frameworks. Already in the collection.
- **Dunne & Bench-Capon 2002 [26]**: Coherence in finite argument systems -- provides the complexity baselines that this paper extends to symmetric AFs and set-of-arguments problems.
- **Baroni & Giacomin 2003-2004 [21, 22]**: Recursive approach to argumentation and odd-length cycle handling -- relevant because symmetric AFs with mutual attacks create even-length cycles, and understanding the odd-length case complements this paper.
- **Dimopoulos, Nebel & Toni 2002 [19]**: Computational complexity of assumption-based argumentation -- the single-argument complexity results that this paper extends to set-of-arguments.
- **Elvang-Goransson, Fox & Krause 1993 [10]**: Logic-based argumentation that naturally produces symmetric attack relations -- the practical motivation for studying symmetric AFs.
