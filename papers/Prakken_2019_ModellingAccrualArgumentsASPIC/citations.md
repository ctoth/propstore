# Citations

## Key Citations for Follow-up
- **Dung (1995)**, "On the acceptability of arguments and its fundamental role in nonmonotonic reasoning, logic programming, and n-person games." The basic abstract argumentation semantics used throughout the paper.
- **Prakken (2005)**, "A study of accrual of arguments, with applications to evidential reasoning." The older inference-based accrual model this paper replaces.
- **Gordon et al. (2007)**, "The Carneades model of argument and burden of proof." Background for the later Carneades-style accrual work.
- **Gordon (2018)**, "Defining argument weighing functions." The immediate target of the paper's monotonicity counterexample.
- **Gordon and Walton (2016)**, "Formalizing balancing arguments." Relevant because the new paper adapts ideas from this line of work.
- **Modgil and Prakken (2013)**, "A general account of argumentation with preferences." Core ASPIC+ preference machinery.
- **Modgil and Prakken (2014)**, "The ASPIC+ framework for structured argumentation: a tutorial." The main ASPIC+ reference used for the formal setup.
- **Modgil and Prakken (2018)**, "Abstract rule-based argumentation." Background for the structured argumentation formalism and rationality results.
- **Prakken (2013)**, "Relating ways to instantiate abstract argumentation frameworks." Background for the relation between instantiations and abstract semantics.
- **Prakken (2015)**, "Formalising debates about law-making proposals as practical reasoning." One of the legal-reasoning applications revisited in Section 6.
- **Al-Abdulkarim et al. (2016)**, "A methodology for designing to reason with legal cases using abstract dialectical frameworks." Relevant to the factor-hierarchy application.
- **Verheij (1996)**, "Rules, reasons, arguments: formal studies of argumentation and defeat." Early work on argument structure and defeat relations.
- **Verheij et al. (1998)**, "An integrated view on rules and principles." Relevant for the rules/principles/exclusionary-reasons discussion.
- **Raz (1975)**, "Practical Reason and Norms." Source for ordinary vs exclusionary reasons.
- **Dworkin (1977)**, "Is law a system of rules?" Background for rules/principles distinctions.
- **Walton and Gordon (2017)**, "Cumulative arguments in artificial intelligence and informal logic." Terminology for linked, convergent, and cumulative arguments.
