# Abstract

## Original Text
This paper proposes a new formal model of argument accrual as an ASPIC+ adaptation for structured argumentation. The model is designed to avoid the weaknesses of earlier proposals, especially exponential argument blow-up, awkward labelling of conclusions with source premises, and problems with strict arguments. It defines accrual sets and a labelling-relative defeat relation, proves that the resulting fixpoint semantics behaves well with standard Dung-style semantics, and shows that the approach applies to legal reasoning, legislative balancing, factor-based reasoning, and the distinction between linked, convergent, cumulative, and accruing arguments.

## Our Interpretation
The paper is best read as a semantic cleanup of accrual. Rather than forcing accrual into a statement-level weighting scheme, it makes accrual a property of argument sets inside ASPIC+ and then proves that the usual argumentation semantics still work. The result is more compact than the 2005 model, more stable than the 2018 weighting approach, and easier to apply to real legal examples.
