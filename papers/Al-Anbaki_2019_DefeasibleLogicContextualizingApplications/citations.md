## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[38] Antoniou, Maher & Billington (2000)** — provides the canonical `±Δ / ±δ` derivation conditions this paper reuses verbatim. The single most important upstream cite for understanding the proof system.
- **[16] Bikakis & Antoniou (2010)** and **[22] Bikakis & Antoniou (2008)** — the closest competitor work on distributed defeasible contextual reasoning. The paper under review specifically positions itself against these as offering an *applicable* framework rather than a characterisation.
- **[24] Governatori et al. (2012)** — directly addresses the static-priority limitation acknowledged in this paper's future work; the natural follow-up if propstore wants dynamic priority revision.
- **[34] Dastani, Governatori, Rotolo, Song, Van Der Torre (2007)** — agent-side companion: "Contextual agent deliberation in defeasible logic." Bridges this paper's framework to multi-agent settings.
- **[40] García & Simari (2014)** — DeLP-server contextual queries; directly relevant to propstore's `query_claim()` / `build_arguments_for()` backward-chaining surface.
- **[19] McCarthy (1993)** — "Notes on formalizing context"; the canonical `ist(c, p)` source already central to propstore.
- **[27] Dey et al. (2001)** — context toolkit; useful as a contrast point for propstore's "uniform reasoning surface above the application" pitch.
