Proposes a four-tuple defeasible-logic theory L = ⟨G, β, D, λ⟩ — Triggers, Concerns, Domain rules, Priorities — for contextualizing already-deployed applications without re-architecting them, generalizing the classical DL ⟨F, R, >⟩ triple to support multiple administrative authorities and a "spectrum of context" with observational, undertaken, and hidden layers (Cᵒ / Cᵘ / Cʰ). Re-uses the standard Antoniou-Maher-Billington `±Δ / ±δ` proof-tag system to certify justifiable decisions in the face of conflicting concerns, and works the formalisation through a phone-call management scenario in which anti-disturbance and per-user personalisation authorities resolve cross-authority conflicts via the global priority λ. Highly relevant to propstore's contextualisation lane: directly parallels propstore's `ist(c, p)` contexts, perspective/concern decoupling, per-source rule bases, and ASPIC+/CKR priority machinery, and supplies a literature-grade phrasing for the observed-vs-rule-governed-vs-embedded provenance spectrum.
