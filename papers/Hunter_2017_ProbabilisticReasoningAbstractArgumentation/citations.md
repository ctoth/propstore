## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Dung (1995)** — "On the acceptability of arguments..." — The foundational paper for abstract argumentation frameworks. Already in propstore's literature grounding.

2. **Thimm (2013)** — "Inconsistency measures for probabilistic logics" — Provides the inconsistency measure framework (d_KL, d_p distances) that Hunter & Thimm build upon for their inconsistency measures.

3. **Hunter & Thimm (2014a)** — "Probabilistic argument graphs for argumentation lotteries" — Extends this epistemic approach to structured (non-abstract) argumentation with argument graphs, potentially more directly applicable to ASPIC+ integration.

4. **Fazzinga, Flesca, & Parisi (2015)** — "On the complexity of probabilistic abstract argumentation frameworks" — Detailed complexity analysis for computing extensions under probabilistic AFs, essential for understanding computational limits.

5. **Katsuno & Mendelzon (1991)** — "On the difference between updating a knowledge base and revising it" — The AGM revision/update distinction that grounds the two consolidation operators (soft vs hard repair).
