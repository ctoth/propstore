This paper presents an extension-based approach to belief revision for Dung's abstract argumentation frameworks (AFs). The core problem addressed is that previous attempts to apply AGM-style belief revision to AFs could not guarantee that the result of revising an AF would itself be representable as an AF. The authors solve this by working at the level of extensions (sets of acceptable arguments) rather than at the model-theoretic level. They define two types of revision operators — one for revising an AF by a propositional formula, and one for revising an AF by another AF — and provide complete representation theorems for both. The results apply to proper 1-maximal argumentation semantics including stable, semi-stable, stage, and sub-stable semantics. The paper establishes a formal correspondence between revision postulates (analogous to AGM postulates) and faithful rankings over sets of extensions, showing that any operator satisfying the postulates can be characterized via such rankings, and vice versa.
