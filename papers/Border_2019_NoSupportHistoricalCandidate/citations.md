## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Caspi et al. 2003 (ref 3):** The original 5-HTTLPR × stressful life events finding — the single most influential candidate gene finding in psychiatry, which this paper directly refutes.
- **Wray et al. 2018 (ref 11):** The PGC3 GWAS that identified 44 real depression risk variants via genome-wide methods — represents the paradigm that supersedes candidate gene approaches.
- **Duncan & Keller 2011 (ref 8):** Critical review of the first decade of GxE research arguing many findings are false positives — provides the theoretical framework for this paper's empirical test.
- **Ioannidis 2005 (ref 18):** "Why most published research findings are false" — the statistical framework explaining how small-sample studies with flexible analyses produce false positives at high rates.
- **Keller 2014 (ref 19):** Identifies the specific methodological flaw (failure to control for confounders in GxE models) that likely drove many false positive candidate gene interaction findings.
