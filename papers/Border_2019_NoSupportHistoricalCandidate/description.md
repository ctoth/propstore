A large-scale preregistered study testing 18 historically prominent candidate genes for depression across samples of up to 443,264 individuals, finding no support for any candidate gene associations or gene-by-environment interactions. The authors demonstrate that depression candidate genes are no more associated with depression than non-candidate genes, concluding that the prior candidate gene literature likely consists of false positives. Relevant to propstore as a canonical example of how large-sample evidence can systematically defeat hundreds of small-sample findings, with implications for evidence weighting and argumentation about replication failures.
