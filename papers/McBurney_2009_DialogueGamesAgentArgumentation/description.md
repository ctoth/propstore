Surveys formal dialogue games for structured argumentation between autonomous agents, providing a generic framework for protocol specification organized by syntax (locutions, commitment stores), semantics (denotational and operational approaches), and pragmatics (strategic considerations). Establishes the Walton & Krabbe dialogue typology as foundational for categorizing agent interactions and proposes design criteria for argumentation protocols. Relevant to propstore as a theoretical basis for how structured multi-agent argumentation could govern claim resolution and stance adjudication.
