# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

In this paper we take a step towards using Argumentation in Social Networks and introduce Social Abstract Argumentation Frameworks, an extension of Dung's Abstract Argumentation Frameworks that incorporates social voting. We propose a class of semantics for these new Social Abstract Argumentation Frameworks and prove some important non-trivial properties which are crucial for their applicability in Social Networks.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper asks how a web debate system can keep the formal attack structure of abstract argumentation while also incorporating lightweight crowd judgments such as upvotes and downvotes. Its answer is to assign each argument explicit positive and negative vote counts and compute graded social values with an algebraic fixpoint semantics. For propstore, this is useful as an early formal template for combining explicit argumentative conflict with ranked or socially weighted acceptance.
