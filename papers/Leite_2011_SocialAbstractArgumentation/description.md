Introduces Social Abstract Argumentation Frameworks, extending Dung-style abstract argumentation with per-argument positive and negative votes from social participants. It defines algebraic semantics that turn vote counts and attack structure into graded argument valuations, and proves existence, uniqueness conditions, and vote-monotonicity properties for the resulting social models. The paper is directly relevant to propstore as a clean formal bridge from binary abstract argumentation to socially informed graded acceptance over competing claims.
