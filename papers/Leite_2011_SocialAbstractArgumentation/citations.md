## Key Citations for Follow-up

- Dung (1995): The immediate formal base class being extended; essential for comparing binary extension semantics against the paper's graded social models.
- Cayrol and Lagasquie-Schiex (2005): Important nearby work on graduality in argumentation, useful for mapping SAFs against other graded semantics.
- Matt and Toni (2008): Another quantitative valuation approach, cited by the authors as a close relative for measuring argument strength.
- Dunne et al. (2011): The main contrast class for weighted attacks, useful because this paper deliberately chooses socially weighted arguments instead.
- Rahwan et al. (2007): Background on the argument web vision that motivates the paper's social-web deployment story.
