Presents epistemic graphs, a formalism that combines labelled argument graphs with probabilistic epistemic constraints over degrees of belief and disbelief. Defines the epistemic language, restricted finite-value reasoning, proof rules, coverage, relation typing, graph-coherence checks, epistemic semantics, and comparisons with ADFs, constrained argumentation, CSPs, and Bayesian networks. Relevant to propstore's argumentation and source-local belief layers because it gives a principled way to keep graph structure, relation labels, belief constraints, and solver-backed consequence separate.
