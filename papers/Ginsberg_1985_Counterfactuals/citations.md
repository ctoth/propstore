## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[2] Genescreth 1984** -- The automated diagnosis framework that Ginsberg applies counterfactual reasoning to in Section 6. Essential context for understanding the diagnosis application and how counterfactuals relate to design descriptions.

- **[3] Ginsberg 1984 (Tech Report)** -- The full technical report version of this paper with more complete treatment of the formal framework. May contain additional proofs and examples not in the conference paper.

- **[4] Glymour & Thomason 1984** -- Connects default reasoning to theory perturbation, which Ginsberg notes shares territory with counterfactual investigation. Relevant to understanding the relationship between non-monotonic reasoning and counterfactuals.

- **[5] Lewis 1973** -- The foundational philosophical work on counterfactuals and possible worlds semantics. Ginsberg proves his construction is formally identical to Lewis's interpretation (Theorem 4).

- **[9] Stalnaker 1968** -- Alternative possible worlds semantics for conditionals. Important for understanding the philosophical context and the non-uniqueness of "most similar possible world" that motivates Ginsberg's approach.
