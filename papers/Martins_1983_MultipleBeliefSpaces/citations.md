## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[2] Doyle 1979 — "A Truth Maintenance System"**: The original TMS that MBR improves upon. Already in the collection as `Doyle_1979_TruthMaintenanceSystem`. MBR's approach to contradiction handling via restriction sets directly contrasts with Doyle's NOGOOD list.

- **[3] Martins 1983 — Ph.D. Dissertation**: The full dissertation contains the complete MBR system description, including extended origin sets (T=ext) and full formal proofs not covered in this 4-page conference paper. Would provide the complete specification needed for implementation.

- **[4] McAllester 1980 — "An Outlook on Truth Maintenance"**: Already in the collection as `McAllester_1978_ThreeValuedTMS`. Provides a three-valued TMS design that is referenced as a prior approach to contradiction handling.

- **[5] McDermott 1982 — "Contexts and Data Dependencies"**: Presents an alternative approach to contextual reasoning that MBR's context/belief-space mechanism can be compared against. Relevant for understanding the design space of context-based reasoning systems.

- **[7] Shapiro & Wand 1976 — "The Relevance of Relevance"**: Together with [1], provides the theoretical foundation for the SWM logic underlying MBR. The relevance-logic basis distinguishes MBR from classical-logic approaches.
