## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[1] Antoniou, Billington, Governatori, Maher 2001 — Representation Results for Defeasible Logic** — supplies the `Basic` transformations Maher relies on for Theorem 4 (linear blow-up to basic DL). Critical for any propstore DL evaluator that wants to inherit linearity.
- **[5] Antoniou, Maher, Billington 2000 — Defeasible Logic versus Logic Programming without Negation as Failure** — Antoniou_2000 already in collection; representation/expressiveness counterpart to the complexity result here.
- **[6] Billington 1993 — Defeasible Logic is Stable** — coherence proof; propstore relies on this when it asserts the DL substrate cannot produce both `+∂p` and `−∂p`.
- **[9] Cadoli, Schaerf 1993 — A Survey of Complexity Results for Nonmonotonic Logics** — anchors the contrast with Π₂ᵖ-hard / co-NP-hard NMR. Useful for justifying propstore's choice of DL.
- **[11] Dowling, Gallier 1984 — Linear-Time Algorithms for Testing Satisfiability of Propositional Horn Formulae** — methodological ancestor; Maher's algorithm is a body-tracking variant.
- **[22] Maher, Governatori 1999 — A Semantic Decomposition of Defeasible Logics** — shows first-order DL is r.e. and frames the well-founded variant Maher predicts is quadratic.
- **[24] Miller 2000 — DELORES User's Manual** — implementation reference for empirical benchmarking.
- **[28] Nute 1994 — Defeasible Logic (Handbook chapter)** — primary semantic ancestor.
