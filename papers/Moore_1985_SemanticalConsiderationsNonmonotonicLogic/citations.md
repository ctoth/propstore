4. Stalnaker, R., A note on non-monotonic modal logic, Department of Philosophy, Cornell University, Ithaca, NY, unpublished manuscript.

## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **McDermott and Doyle 1980 [1]**: The original nonmonotonic logic paper that Moore's work directly critiques and improves upon. Essential for understanding the fixed-point approach to nonmonotonic reasoning and why it fails.
- **McDermott 1982 [2]**: The follow-up that tried to strengthen the original logic using modal logics T, S4, S5. Contains the S5 collapse result that Moore explains via autoepistemic analysis.
- **Stalnaker (unpublished) [4]**: Credited with the key observation that autoepistemic reasoning is nonmonotonic because autoepistemic statements are context-sensitive/indexical. Also credited with suggesting the term "autoepistemic reasoning" and the stability conditions.
