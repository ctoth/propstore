Introduces autoepistemic logic as a formally grounded model of an ideally rational agent reasoning about its own beliefs, providing a possible-worlds semantics that is both sound and complete. The paper distinguishes default reasoning from autoepistemic reasoning and shows that McDermott and Doyle's nonmonotonic logics conflate the two, explaining pathologies such as the collapse of nonmonotonic S5 to monotonic S5. Foundational for understanding the epistemic dimension of argumentation and belief maintenance systems, particularly the relationship between multiple stable expansions and multiple acceptable belief sets in argumentation frameworks.
