## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[29] Guha 1991 "Contexts: A Formalization and Some Applications"** — McCarthy/Buvač explicitly adopt Guha's notation because it was already built into Cyc. Direct ancestor of `ist(c, p)` syntax as used in this paper. Guha's thesis is the primary next read for anyone wanting to connect McCarthy-style context logic to a working KR system (Cyc microtheories).

- **[42] McCarthy 1993 "Notes on formalizing context"** — the paper this expanded note revises and supersedes. Useful for seeing the 1993 vs 1998 delta (new §7 planning contexts and §13 conclusion; updated bibliography).

- **[12] Buvač, Buvač & Mason 1995 "Metamathematics of contexts"** — provides the proof that propositional context logic bijectively reduces to multi-modal logic (cited in §12 remark 4). Needed to understand the formal underpinnings and when the McCarthy/Buvač approach is equivalent to standard modal logic.

- **[9] Buvač 1996 "Quantificational logic of context"** — companion result showing the above reduction does **not** carry over to the quantified case (p.33). Crucial for anyone trying to decide whether propstore can get away with propositional context modalities or needs a genuinely new quantificational context logic.

- **[39] McCarthy 1987 "Generality in artificial intelligence"** — the Turing Award paper stating the "generality problem" that is the entire motivation for this line of work (cited throughout, [39] is reference [39] deliberately). Foundational reading.
