# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

We propose an abstract argumentation theory whose dynamics is captured by the application of belief revision concepts. The theory is deemed abstract because both the underlying logic for arguments and argumentation semantics remain unspecified. Regarding our approach to argument theory change, we define some basic change operations along with their necessary collateral elements towards the definition of a warrant-prioritized revision operation. This kind of revision operator may be an argument and then applies a contraction ensuring that the added argument can be believed afterwards.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper imports AGM-style change ideas into a structured argumentation setting where arguments have internal subargument structure and active/inactive status. Its main result is a revision operator that first adds a target argument and then deactivates a carefully chosen set of attackers so the target becomes warranted while preserving as much of the theory as possible.
