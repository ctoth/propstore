Defines prudent semantics for Dung argumentation frameworks by strengthening admissibility so that no two co-accepted arguments are connected by an odd-length indirect attack path. Establishes preferred, stable, grounded, and complete prudent extensions, compares their cautiousness against Dung inference relations, and shows the decision-problem complexity classes do not increase relative to the corresponding standard semantics. Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer as a coherence policy for preventing indirectly conflicting claims from appearing together in an accepted extension.
