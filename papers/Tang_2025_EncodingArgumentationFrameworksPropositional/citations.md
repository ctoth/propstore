## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[1] Dung 1995** - Foundational AF paper defining all the classical semantics that this paper encodes; already in collection.
- **[12] Gabbay 2012** - The equational approach to argumentation networks that this paper shows corresponds to specific fuzzy logic encodings; essential for understanding the Eq^R_max and Eq^R_inverse systems.
- **[29] Besnard & Doutre 2004** - The original model checking approach that encodes AFs as PL2 formulas; the starting point that this paper generalizes.
- **[44] Baroni, Caminada & Giacomin 2011** - Comprehensive survey of argumentation semantics including labelling-based approaches used throughout this paper.
- **[16] Wu, Li, Oren et al. 2016** - Godel fuzzy argumentation frameworks; closely related work on fuzzy AFs using Godel t-norm that this paper subsumes via its encoding methodology.
