This paper demonstrates that nanopublications with trusty URIs (cryptographic content-hash identifiers) can provide granular, verifiable, and persistent references to evolving Linked Data datasets through incremental versioning and subset-based retrieval. The overhead from auxiliary provenance triples disappears when accounting for version history, and typical researcher subsets can be retrieved efficiently from a decentralized server network. Directly relevant to propstore's content-hash-addressed storage, git-backed versioning, and the design principle that data should carry its own provenance at the individual claim level.
