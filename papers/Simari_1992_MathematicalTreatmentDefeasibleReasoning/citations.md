## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Poole 1988 [19]** — "A Logical Framework for Default Reasoning" — Defines the specificity comparison that Simari & Loui formalize and extend. Essential for understanding the theoretical basis of the specificity ordering.

- **Pollock 1987 [18]** — "Defeasible Reasoning" — Provides the warrant/justification theory that Simari & Loui reformulate as the dialectical S-argument/I-argument process. The other half of this paper's foundation.

- **Loui 1987 [10]** — "Defeat Among Arguments" — Direct predecessor system. Simari & Loui's formal language (K, Delta, >-) evolved from Loui's system. Important for understanding the lineage of argument-based approaches.

- **Reiter 1980 [21]** — "A Logic for Default Reasoning" — The standard default logic that Simari & Loui's approach is an alternative to. Important for comparing non-argument-based default reasoning.

- **Geffner & Pearl 1989 [5]** — "A Framework for Reasoning with Defaults" — Represents the competing "irrelevance-based" paradigm. Useful for understanding why argument-based approaches diverge from probabilistic/causal approaches.
