## Key Citations for Follow-up

- Brewka and Woltran (2010) because this is the base ADF paper that the current work revises and generalizes.
- Strass (2013) because the paper directly positions its operator-based semantics against that report and several conclusions depend on the comparison.
- Egly et al. (2010) because the DIAMOND implementation builds on these ASP encodings for argumentation frameworks.
- Ellmauthaler and Wallner (2012) because it is the immediate predecessor for ASP-based ADF evaluation mentioned in the implementation section.
- Modgil (2009) because the prioritized ADF construction follows the same broad idea of modeling preferences by ordinary arguments/nodes.
