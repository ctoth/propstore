Formalizes expansion operations on Dung argumentation frameworks, defining normal, strong, and weak expansions based on attack direction constraints between old and new arguments.
Proves that any conflict-free set can be enforced as an extension by adding a single new argument, and establishes monotonicity of extension counts and justification states for weak expansions under directional semantics.
Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer for understanding how incremental additions of arguments preserve or modify extension membership and justified status.
