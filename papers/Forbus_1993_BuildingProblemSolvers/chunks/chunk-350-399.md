## Key Definitions

- **Set construal mechanism**: A mechanism implemented in two files (setcss.lisp and algebra.lisp) that maintains sets with has-member and has-nonmember statements, enforces closed-world assumptions on sets, and handles redundant construal uniqueness constraints. *(p.350)*

- **set-members**: Given the name of a set, returns its current construal if any. The closed-world assumption underlying the construal is returned as a second value. *(p.350)*

- **close-set-if-needed**: Closes a set if it isn't already closed. *(p.350)*

- **close-set**: Closes a set. *(p.350)*

- **with-closed-set**: Takes two arguments, the name of a set and a list of forms. The forms are executed in a logical environment which includes the current environment plus the current construal of the set it was given. *(p.350-351)*

- **Dependency-directed search (DDS)**: An abstract procedure that uses LTMS for general-purpose search with choice-sets, solution recording, and consistency checking. Defined via `(defun DDS (choice-sets) ...)` with `record-solution`, `nogooded?`, `make-assuming`, `consistent?`, and `record-nogood` as key operations. *(p.354)*

- **Quantity (QP theory)**: A continuous property consisting of an amount and a derivative, each of which are functions whose range is the real numbers. The amount $A(q)$ maps a quantity to its value at a specific time; the derivative $D_s(q)$ maps to its temporal derivative. *(p.368)*

- **Quantity space**: The collection of ordinal relationships defining the value of a number. Defined by a quantity, a collection of ordinal information relating to other quantities and parameters. Limit points are special values (e.g., freezing point, boiling point) where the number is compared against. *(p.370)*

- **Landmark values**: Named limit points on a quantity space that track how a quantity changes over time. Distinguished from general limit points: landmarks are the points on a graph denoting a quantity through time. *(p.370)*

- **Qualitative proportionality**: $Q_+ P \propto_{Q_+} Q$ means there is some function which (1) determines $Q$, (2) depends at least on $Q$, and (3) is increasing monotonic in its dependence on $Q$. The idea of "inversely qualitatively proportional" is defined similarly with the implicit function being decreasing monotonic. *(p.371)*

- **Direct influence**: Expresses the direct effects of a kind of process on quantities. Notation: `(I+ (heat kettle) (flow-rate &t1))` or `(I- ...)`. Direct influences can only be imposed by physical processes. *(p.372-374)*

- **Indirect influence (qualitative proportionality)**: Propagates effects of direct changes through the system via qualitative proportionalities. Together with direct influences, they form the causal account of QP theory. *(p.373-374)*

- **Confluence equation**: An abstraction of an entire family of quantitative equations. Expresses the causality which holds between parameters. E.g., Newton's second law: `(qprop (acceleration Block) (applied-force Block))` and `(qprop- (acceleration Block) (mass Block))`. *(p.373)*

- **View (QP theory)**: Defined by four fields: (1) individuals field specifying what kinds of things the view holds between, (2) quantity conditions, (3) relations field, (4) last (and final) field. The semantics of the individuals field is universal instantiation. *(p.376-377)*

- **Contained-Stuff view**: A contained stuff is whenever pieces of material happen to exist in a particular container consisting of a particular substance in a particular phase. Defined with individuals field, preconditions, and quantity conditions. *(p.377-378)*

- **Physical process (QP theory)**: Defined using the same notation as views, with one addition: the `influences` field contains the set of direct influence statements representing the direct effects of a kind of process on quantities. A process must have at least one direct influence. *(p.378-379)*

- **Heat flow process**: A physical process between any two objects that have the quantity heat and are connected via a heat path. The precondition is that a temperature difference must exist. Introduces the quantity `flow-rate` which is positive and depends on both temperatures. *(p.379)*

- **Influence resolution**: Given a process structure, the direct influences on the situation are known. Direct effects propagated through indirect influences (qualitative proportionalities) to ascertain behavior of dependent parameters. Three cases: (1) no influences on a quantity, (2) single influence, (3) conflicting influences requiring sign analysis. *(p.382-383)*

- **Limit analysis**: The operation of detecting potential changes in qualitative structure of continuous parameters. Determines the possibility of such changes and reasons about their effects. Uses quantity space representation to make predictions. *(p.384)*

- **Envisionment**: A collection of all possible qualitative states and the possible transitions between them. Can be generated by expanding limit analysis on some initial situation. While they can be large, envisionments are always finite. *(p.385)*

- **TGIZMO data structure fields**: `title` (string for printing), `measurements` (set of measurements to be explained), `scenario` (name of defining current scenario), `ltre` (the LTRE used), `debugging` (list of symbols), `comparisons` (ordinal comparisons), `comp-cycles` (cycles of comparisons used to denote transitivity), `influence-order` (causal ordering for influences), `update-stamp?` (indicates new ordinal relationship found), `marked` (counter for unique identifiers), `states` (list of states found during search). *(p.395-396)*

- **Additional TGIZMO fields**: `individuals` (statements about individuals), `view-structure` (set of active view instances), `process-structure` (set of active process instances), `comparisons` (set of ordinal relationships other than Ds values), `Ds-values` (comparisons of derivatives with zero). *(p.396)*

## Algorithms and Pseudocode

- **Dependency-directed search (DDS) abstract procedure** *(p.354)*:
  ```lisp
  (defun DDS (choice-sets)
    (if (null choice-sets) (record-solution)
      (dolist (choice (first choice-sets))
        (unless (nogooded? choice)
          (make-assuming choice)
          (if (consistent?)
              (DDS (rest choice-sets))
              (record-nogood choice))))))
  ```

- **DD-Search factory implementation** (Figure 10.7): Full Lisp implementation including contradiction handler setup, choice management with `:TRUE`/`:FALSE` labeling, assumption handling, and backtracking via `close-set`. Handles entering handler for contradictions, iterating through uninstalled classes, and managing legal choices. *(p.355)*

- **justify-cwa-if-needed**: Caches CWA assertions in data-pilot to avoid creating redundant LTMS justifications. Checks whether a CWA for a given set has already been created before building a new one. *(p.353)*

- **close-set procedure**: Begins by gathering current has-member statements using `get-set-informants`, uses that information to build the appropriate members and CWA assertion. Existing closed-world assumptions are retracted by `retract-cwa`. The closed-world assumption is created by the call to `assume-cwa-if-needed` and the justification for the members statement is created in terms of the positive and negative has-member statements and the closed-world assumption via `justify-cwa-if-needed`. *(p.351)*

- **Axiom schemata for view/process instantiation** *(p.390-391)*:
  1. Legal possibility of instance depends on conditions in individuals field: $\bigwedge_i l_i \Rightarrow$ `(ProcessInstance P)`
  2. Physical existence only when all individuals it depends upon exist: `(Exists $l_j$) = (Exists P)`
  3. Active exactly when it exists and preconditions/quantity conditions hold: `(Exists P) ∧ (∧ A_i) → (Active P)`
  4. Active process must physically exist: `(Active P) → (Exists P)` (rules out "ghosts")

- **Inequality reasoning optimization** *(p.392-393)*: Two key observations for efficient inequality reasoning:
  1. Only a finite set of comparisons from QP domain theory are relevant; most results from transitivity compositions are irrelevant.
  2. View the set of mentioned comparisons as a graph where nodes are numbers being compared and edges are ordinal statements. Transitivity inference occurs in the graph's cycles. Cache cycles in a separate data structure, and whenever the belief state of an ordinal relationship changes, check cycles it is part of.

- **Ordinal relationship encoding** *(p.393)*: A single relationship $Q > P$ can encode others:
  ```
  Q > P ⇔ Q > P
  Q > P ⇔ ¬(Q < P)
  Q > P ⇔ Q ≠ P ∧ ¬(Q < P)
  Q > P ⇔ ¬(Q = P) ∧ ¬(Q < P)
  ```

- **translate-relation**: Transforms domain theory descriptions expressed in a form congenial to domain modelers into something overridable by LTRE. For `defView` this is trivial; for `defProcess` and `defEntity` a bit more work. Uses `parse-tp` for basic syntax tests. *(p.397-399)*

- **make-tp-rule**: Uses the individuals specification to create a set of triggers for a pattern-directed rule for detecting instances of that process or view. *(p.397)*

Uses Curry trick -- `Currying` still works in TGIZMO when extra arguments are constants. *(p.387-388)*

## Equations

- **Quantity representation** *(p.368)*:
  - $A(q)$: Amount of quantity $q$ (maps to real number at a time)
  - $D_s(q)$: Derivative of quantity $q$ (temporal derivative)
  - Special value: ZERO

- **Qualitative proportionality** *(p.371)*:
  - $Q_+ \propto_{Q_+} Q$: Increasing monotonic dependence
  - $Q_- \propto_{Q_-} Q$: Decreasing monotonic (inversely proportional)
  - Notation: `(qprop (acceleration Block) (applied-force Block))` meaning if applied force increases, acceleration increases

- **Correspondence** *(p.372)*:
  - `(Correspondence (Acceleration Block) ZERO) (Applied-force Block) ZERO))` -- when applied force is zero, acceleration is zero

- **Direct influence notation** *(p.372)*:
  - $I+(Q_1, Q_2)$: Process directly increases $Q_1$ through $Q_2$
  - $I-(Q_1, Q_2)$: Process directly decreases $Q_1$ through $Q_2$
  - Example: `(I+ (heat kettle) (flow-rate &t1))` and `(I- (heat stove) (flow-rate &t1))`

- **Influence resolution rules** *(p.382-383)*:
  - If $Da[Q_1 > 0]$ and $Da[Q_1]$ is the only direct influence, then $D_s[Q_1] = $ the sign of the qualitative proportionality
  - If quantity has only one indirect influence, $D_s[Q_1]$ will simply be the Ds value of the quantity constraining it, modulated by the sign of the proportionality
  - Conflicting influences require gathering Ds values for all quantities, sorting by sign of functional dependence

- **Axiom schemata for instance existence** *(p.390-391)*:
  - $\bigwedge_i l_i \Rightarrow \text{ProcessInstance}(P)$ (legal possibility)
  - $\text{Exists}(l_j) = \text{Exists}(P)$ (physical existence)
  - $\text{Exists}(P) \wedge \bigwedge A_i \Rightarrow \text{Active}(P)$ (activity)
  - $\text{Active}(P) \Rightarrow \text{Exists}(P)$ (no ghosts)

## Key Claims and Properties

- **LTMS maintains only a single derivation for any supported node.** Unlike the ATMS, this means there is no record of constraints in the LTMS database. The strategy to overcome this is to cache assertions corresponding to the closed-world assumptions in a record in the data-pilot, so justifications that have already been created are not redundantly created. *(p.353)*

- **Decomposition of `make-assuming` and `consistent?` is easy to understand but does not accurately reflect the asynchronous nature of contradiction processing in the LTMS.** These operations could be decomposed by turning off contradiction processing while making assumptions and then looking for contradictions afterward as a distinct operation. Preferred to interleave them because it allows the system to backtrack as quickly as possible, increasing efficiency. *(p.356)*

- **Two kinds of information gleaned from earlier parts of the search are useful**: (1) Choices can be ruled out (labeled `:FALSE`) or forced as a consequence of earlier decisions (labeled `:TRUE`). Exploring the second is as important as the first since in that case choice sets are mutually exclusive and remaining choices are irrelevant. (2) Extra macro debug-data for extracting extra information about the search on demand. *(p.356-357)*

- **A central claim of QP theory is that this view of physical processes is a good portrait of a substantial part of human common-sense knowledge.** This includes both the person on the street and those with professional knowledge such as scientists and engineers. The notion of physical process is directly useful in encoding and organizing domain theories. *(p.367)*

- **Qualitative mathematics cannot be a necessary prerequisite to physical reasoning.** The traditional formalisms of physics and mathematics cannot be necessary because qualitative knowledge is independently applicable to many kinds of expert, professional reasoning about the physical world. *(p.368)*

- **Ordinal relationships are surprisingly powerful.** For instance, flows occur when pressure or other equivalents are unequal, and the comings and goings of pieces of stuff can be tracked by noting how much of them there is. Compositions form the minimum amount of information needed to track changes. *(p.369)*

- **QP theory allows more information to be specified about functions implied by qualitative proportionalities.** We can pin down values at a finite number of points by correspondences, e.g., `(Correspondence (Acceleration Block) ZERO) (Applied-force Block) ZERO))` -- when applied force is zero, acceleration is zero too. *(p.372)*

- **The restrictions imposed on QP theory's causal interpretations are not without cost: they limit the expressive power** to represent any system of ordinary differential equations whose independent parameter is time. Only systems free of simultaneities can be expressed. This restriction is analogous to the state space formulation in engineering modeling: directly influenced parameters play role of state variables, indirectly influenced parameters play role of dependent variables. Loops in the graph of equations must have some delay element. *(p.375)*

- **In considering what we need out of an inequality system, we can design a far more efficient reasoner.** Any model for a specific scenario built from a QP domain theory will mention some (presumably finite) number of ordinal comparisons. It is only these relationships that TGIZMO cares about. Computing anything else is a waste of time. *(p.392)*

- **Envisionments are always finite** because any system that consists of a finite number of physical and conceptual entities has only a finite number of distinct qualitative states. *(p.385)*

- **Good modeling language should have abstracted away as much implementation detail as possible.** Common patterns of usage should be allowed; the language should allow these patterns to be stated concisely. Rule triggers must be insulated from main LTRE details. *(p.388)*

## Design Decisions and Rationale

- **Why interleave assumption-making with consistency checking in DD-Search**: Allows the system to backtrack as quickly as possible rather than making all assumptions first and then checking. This increases efficiency since contradictions are detected early. *(p.356)*

- **Why cache CWA assertions in data-pilot**: The `justify-cwa-if-needed` procedure caches CWA assertions because the LTMS has no record of constraints (unlike the ATMS), and asserting the same construal multiple times can lead to redundant information in the dependency network. Caching avoids creating duplicate justifications. *(p.353)*

- **Why restrict TGIZMO to single period of time**: Simplifies the program considerably. Time can be left implicit in descriptions since no statements need to relate situations involving two distinct times. For some tasks, simpler temporal notations suffice; for extending to multiple timepoints, modal operators or Hayes's notion of slices could be used. *(p.386)*

- **Why restrict direct influences to physical processes only**: This enforces the sole mechanism assumption -- physical processes are the only source of change in physical situations. This provides important advantages for representing and reasoning about physical domains. *(p.367)*

- **Why use Curry trick for modeling language**: The compound predicate `(amount-of-in (substance) (phase))` is treated as an individual (a function of one argument, the container). This works because in TGIZMO extra arguments are constants, not variables. More sophisticated modeling languages would introduce more syntax to simplify domain modeler's work. *(p.387-388)*

- **Why use defPredicate for defining direct consequences**: Provides a method for expressing the occasional pattern-directed rule that doesn't quite fit the other primitives. Syntax: `(metrics (name) (triggers) (consequence))` where triggers become intent triggers for a rule asserting the consequence, justified by the conjunction of triggers using the name as informant. *(p.389)*

- **Why organize inequality reasoning around graph cycles**: Only a finite set of comparisons from the QP domain theory are relevant. Viewing comparisons as a graph, transitivity inference occurs only in cycles. Caching cycles in a separate data structure and checking only when belief state of an ordinal relationship changes is far more efficient than naive transitivity closure. *(p.392-393)*

- **Why use a single relationship to encode all ordinal relations**: A single relationship $Q > P$ can encode $\neg(Q < P)$, $Q \neq P$, etc. Using $>$ internally as the only ordinal relationship greatly simplifies transitivity processing. Convenience should not be sacrificed to simplicity though, so modelers are allowed to freely use standard ordinal relationships; translation is handled automatically via pattern-directed rules. *(p.393)*

## Implementation Notes

- **Set construal is implemented in two files**: `setcss.lisp` (LTRE rule for has-member/has-nonmember semantics) and `algebra.lisp` (redundant construal uniqueness constraints). *(p.350)*

- **DD-Search uses a factory pattern**: The `DD-Search` function creates a closure over a set of choices, contradiction handler, and solution recorder. It returns a function that can be called to generate solutions incrementally. *(p.355)*

- **TGIZMO is implemented across multiple files** *(p.395-399)*:
  - `defs.lisp`: Global organization and LTRE interface, TGIZMO struct definition, state struct definition
  - `mblang.lisp`: Implementing the modeling language (`defView`, `defProcess`, `defEntity`, `defPredicate`). Key procedures: `parse-tp` (syntax checking), `translate-relation` (transforms domain descriptions), `make-tp-rule` (creates pattern-directed rules), `implicit:translate-relation` (handles more complex cases)
  - `gpvs.lisp`: Searching for patterns of activity, creates scenario model and choice sets for views and processes

- **The default contradiction handler (DR-CWA-ContradictionHandler) automatically retracts invalid closed-world assumptions from influence resolution, if any.** An important change is in the debugging system. TGIZMO is complicated enough that it makes sense to provide fine control over how much information about what subsystem gets printed. *(p.396)*

- **Debug-tgizmo takes an extra flag argument** for finer-grained debugging control. The `state` struct provides a medium for taking "snapshots" of the LTRE database, so different states of a search can be cached and later compared. *(p.396)*

- **Modeling language procedures**: `tg-fetch`, `tg-test`, `tg-false?`, `tg-false-form?` (like `tg-false?` but over a list of forms), and `tg-run-rules` provide the usual hooks to refund `*tgizmo*` as needed. *(p.397)*

- **translate-relation comes in two kinds of results**: the simple cases which are to be justified on the basis of the antecedents (stored in `explicit:translate-relation`), and the more complicated cases stored in `implicit:translate-relation` which performs case analysis and returns two kinds of values. *(p.398-399)*

- **Only-During semantics**: The statement `(Only-During (Exists (C-S ?sub ?ct ?stuff)))` in the relations field of the Contained-Stuff view really means: `(iff (AND (Container ?sub) (substance ?sub) (phase ?ct)) (Exists (C-S ?sub ?ct ?stuff)))`. By providing Only-During the modeler's job is simplified since the antecedents are "deduced" from the logical environment. *(p.398)*
