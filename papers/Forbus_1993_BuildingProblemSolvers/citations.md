## Key Citations for Follow-up

These are the most relevant citations for projects focused on argumentation frameworks, truth maintenance systems, and belief revision:

### 1. **Doyle, J., "A truth maintenance system," *Artificial Intelligence*, Vol. 12, pp. 231-272, 1979.**
   - **Relevance:** Foundational work on truth maintenance systems (TMS), which is central to belief revision and non-monotonic reasoning. Directly applicable to argumentation frameworks that model justification and retraction of beliefs.

### 2. **Doyle, J., "Truth maintenance systems," *Artificial Intelligence*, Vol. 12, pp. 231-272, 1979.**
   - **Relevance:** Core reference on TMS architecture, labeled this-and-other-node (JNOD) structures, and propagation mechanisms for managing contradictory beliefs.

### 3. **de Kleer, J., and Brown, J.S., "Theories of naïve physics," *Artificial Intelligence*, Vol. 24, pp. 185-203, 1984.**
   - **Relevance:** Extends truth maintenance with qualitative reasoning and assumption-based systems. Shows how TMS principles apply to causal and physical reasoning domains.

### 4. **Reiter, R., "A logic for default reasoning," *Artificial Intelligence*, Vol. 13, pp. 81-132, 1980.**
   - **Relevance:** Formal framework for defeasible reasoning and default logic, which complements argumentation theory's treatment of non-monotonic inference.

### 5. **Genesereth, M. R., and Nilsson, N. J., "Logical Foundations of Artificial Intelligence," Morgan Kaufmann, 1987.**
   - **Relevance:** Comprehensive treatment of logical foundations including default logic, non-monotonic reasoning, and knowledge representation essential to understanding argumentation frameworks.

### 6. **Forbus, K. D., "Qualitative process theory," *Artificial Intelligence*, Vol. 24, pp. 85-168, 1984.**
   - **Relevance:** Demonstrates application of TMS and qualitative reasoning to model continuous physical processes. Shows how argumentation and justification principles scale to complex domains.

### 7. **McDermott, D., "A temporal logic for reasoning about processes and plans," *Journal of Cognitive Science*, Vol. 6, pp. 101-155, 1982.**
   - **Relevance:** Addresses temporal aspects of reasoning and planning, extending argumentation frameworks to handle time-dependent claims and attacks.

### 8. **Charniak, E., Riesbeck, C., McDermott, D., and Meehan, J., *Artificial Intelligence Programming*, 2nd edition, Erlbaum, 1987.**
   - **Relevance:** Practical implementation guide for AI systems including belief systems, useful for understanding how argumentation frameworks are implemented in practice.

### 9. **Weld, D., and de Kleer, J., *Readings in Qualitative Reasoning about Physical Systems*, Morgan Kaufmann, 1990.**
   - **Relevance:** Comprehensive collection covering truth maintenance, qualitative reasoning, and model-based diagnosis—all domains where argumentation frameworks have been applied.

### 10. **Hobbs, J. R., "An improper treatment of quantification in ordinary English," *Proceedings of the 27th Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics*, pp. 57-63, 1989.**
   - **Relevance:** Addresses representation and reasoning about natural language propositions, relevant for argument mining and extracting structured arguments from text.
