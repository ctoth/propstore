Defines nanopublications as the smallest citable unit of scientific communication, with a layered model (concept -> triple -> statement -> annotation -> nanopublication -> S-Evidence) realized over RDF Named Graphs by reusing existing ontologies (SWAN for provenance/annotation, SWP for attribution and digital signatures, FOAF for agents, TRIG as concrete syntax).
Key design choices: never invent new specifications, defer the "minimum annotation set" to community agreement, use SWP's assertedBy so multiple authorities can independently claim the same statement, and introduce S-Evidence as the aggregation unit over all nanopublications about one statement; three graded adoption tiers (Transformation-, Format-, Concept-Wiki-Compatible) plus the Concept Wiki as a shared identifier repository.
Direct antecedent of propstore's micropublication shape: the statement-plus-annotations-plus-attribution layout and the S-Evidence aggregation concept map onto propstore's claim-plus-provenance substrate and its non-commitment discipline, though the paper supplies no conflict or argumentation semantics (those belong in the argumentation layer).
