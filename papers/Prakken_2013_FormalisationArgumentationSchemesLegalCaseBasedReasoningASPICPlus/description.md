Formalizes CATO-style legal case-based reasoning as ASPIC+ argumentation schemes. The paper models cases as factor sets, defines scheme-based comparisons between current and precedent cases, and proves the resulting theory satisfies standard rationality conditions. It also sketches extensions to HYPO-style dimensions, facts-to-factors reasoning, factor incompatibility, and value-based preference arguments.
