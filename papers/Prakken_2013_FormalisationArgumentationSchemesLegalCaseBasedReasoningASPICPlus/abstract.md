# Abstract

## Paraphrased Abstract
The paper gives a formal ASPIC+ account of legal case-based reasoning as a set of argumentation schemes over factors, factor hierarchies, and undercutting attacks. It starts from CATO-style factor comparisons, extends the analysis toward HYPO-style dimensions and other unresolved issues in legal case reasoning, and uses the formalization to show that the resulting theory satisfies the ASPIC+/Dung rationality conditions under stated assumptions.

## Interpretation
This is a bridge paper: it takes the semi-formal argument schemes from AI and Law and makes them precise enough to reason about their defeat structure, implementation, and metatheory in one framework.
