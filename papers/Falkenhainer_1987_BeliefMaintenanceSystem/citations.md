## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Doyle, 1979** — The foundational justification-based TMS that the BMS directly generalizes. Essential for understanding the baseline architecture being extended.
- **Shafer, 1976** — The mathematical foundation for the belief interval representation used throughout the BMS. Defines Dempster-Shafer theory of evidence.
- **Ginsberg, 1984** — Directly provides the simplified Dempster's rule formulas used in the BMS for combining and subtracting beliefs in the two-valued case.
- **Falkenhainer, Forbus, & Gentner, 1986** — The Structure-Mapping Engine whose pattern matching algorithm the BMS implements in a probabilistic version.
- **Garvey et al, 1981** — Source of the AND and OR belief combination formulas used in the BMS.
