## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **McDermott and Doyle (1978)** --- "Non-monotonic Logic I": The competing modal logic approach to non-monotonic reasoning. Reiter explicitly contrasts his default logic with this approach throughout the paper. Important for understanding the design space of non-monotonic formalisms.

- **Doyle (1978)** --- "Truth Maintenance Systems for Problem Solving": The implementation counterpart to Reiter's formal theory. Doyle's TMS provides the heuristic mechanism for the belief revision that Reiter formalizes. Already in the propstore collection.

- **Clark (1978)** --- "Negation as failure": Establishes the connection between PROLOG's negation-as-failure and the closed world assumption, which Reiter formalizes as a special case of normal default logic.

- **McCarthy (1977)** --- "Epistemological problems of Artificial Intelligence": Introduces circumscription as an alternative formalization of default reasoning. Reiter discusses a potential deep relationship with default logic in Section 8.

- **Sandewall (1972)** --- "An approach to the frame problem": Proposes the UNLESS operator and frame default schema that Reiter formalizes as a default rule, directly motivating the "frame default" example in Section 1.1.4.
