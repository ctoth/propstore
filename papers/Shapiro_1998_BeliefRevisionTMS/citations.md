## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Martins and Shapiro (1988)** - The full SNeBR model paper, which details how assumption sets are computed per inference rule and how belief revision works in the SNePS system. Essential for understanding the ATMS-reasoner integration approach.
- **Gardenfors and Rott (1995)** - The most comprehensive survey of AGM belief revision, and notably the only cross-tradition reference Shapiro identifies as citing both TMS and AGM work.
- **Cravo and Martins (1993)** - SNePSwD extends SNeBR with non-monotonic rules and partial importance ordering among assertions, directly implementing one of the AGM constraints. Relevant for entrenchment-based culprit selection.
- **Friedman and Halpern (1996)** - Critiques AGM belief revision and notes that both AGM postulates (Boutilier 1993) and the TMS tradition (Freund and Lehmann 1994) were independently motivated by the problem of chronological backtracking.
- **Stallman and Sussman (1977)** - The origin of dependency-directed backtracking, the core idea that led to all TMS work. Already foundational context for the Doyle (1979) paper in the collection.
