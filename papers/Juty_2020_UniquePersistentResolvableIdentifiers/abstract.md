# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The FAIR principles describe characteristics intended to support access to and reuse of digital artifacts in the scientific research ecosystem. Persistent, globally unique identifiers, resolvable on the Web, and associated with a set of additional descriptive metadata, are foundational to FAIR data. Here we describe some basic principles and exemplars for their design, use and orchestration with other system elements to achieve FAIRness for digital research objects.

---

## Our Interpretation

This paper addresses the foundational role of persistent digital identifiers in enabling FAIR (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) data practices. The key finding is that identifiers must satisfy three properties -- uniqueness, persistence, and resolvability -- and the authors survey four major systems (DOI, ARK, identifiers.org, PURL) that implement these properties through two distinct persistence patterns. Relevant to propstore as contextual grounding for how the system's own identifier schemes (content hashes, DOIs for papers, concept IDs) relate to established identifier infrastructure standards.
