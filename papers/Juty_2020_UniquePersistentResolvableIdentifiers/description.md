Surveys the design principles and exemplar systems (DOI, ARK, identifiers.org, PURL) for persistent, globally unique, resolvable digital identifiers as the foundation of FAIR data practices. Introduces a two-pattern framework for identifier persistence: individual registration with redirecting services versus namespace-level registration with resolver endpoints. Provides contextual grounding for propstore's identifier infrastructure decisions, though the paper is a conceptual survey rather than a directly implementable formal model.
