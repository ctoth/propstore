Proposes an Identity Ontology for Linked Data that decomposes owl:sameAs-style identity into reflexive, symmetric, and transitive identity relations instead of forcing every link into full isomorphism. Shows that owl:sameAs can corrupt provenance and contextual assertions in biospecimen, FOAF, and knowledge-representation examples, then gives identity properties and OWL 2 property-chain patterns for selective isomorphism. Directly relevant to propstore's concept identity and merge discipline because it treats identity as a typed relation with policy-specific inference rather than a global collapse.
