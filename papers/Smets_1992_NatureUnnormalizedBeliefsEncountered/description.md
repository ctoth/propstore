Smets justifies allowing positive basic belief mass on the empty set, m(empty) > 0, within the Transferable Belief Model, interpreting it as quantified inter-source conflict and as open-world residual mass meaning "the answer may lie outside the frame of discernment".
He proves unnormalized Dempster combination and unnormalized conditioning are the unique rules that commute with refinement of the frame, so the classical Dempster-Shafer normalization step actively destroys information rather than merely hiding it, and shows the credal/pignistic split confines closed-world commitment to decision time via the pignistic transformation BetP.
This is foundational for propstore's principle of deferring resolution until render: m(empty) > 0 is the formal carrier of honest ignorance at the credal level, exactly aligned with the project requirement that vacuous opinions and conflict information must survive into storage rather than being silently absorbed by combination.
