# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

Within the transferable belief model, positive basic belief masses can be allocated to the empty set, leading to unnormalized belief functions. The nature of these unnormalized beliefs is analyzed.

---

## Our Interpretation

Smets answers the question "what does m(empty) > 0 mean?" by showing it is the natural carrier of inter-source conflict and of an open-world residual mass (the actual answer might lie outside the agent's frame Y). He proves that unnormalized Dempster combination and unnormalized conditioning are the *unique* rules that commute with refinement of the frame, so the classical normalization step is not just discardable information — it is actively wrong from a homomorphism standpoint. The credal/pignistic split confines closed-world commitment to decision time, which is directly relevant to propstore's principle of deferring resolution until render.
