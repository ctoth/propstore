## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[5] Antoniou, Maher, Billington — Defeasible Logic versus Logic Programming without Negation as Failure (JLP 2000).** Sister paper showing DL is the most sceptically expressive in the family of LPwNF-style logics. Already in collection as `Antoniou_2000_DefeasibleLogicVersusLogic` — the densest cross-reference partner for this paper.
- **[6] Billington — Defeasible Logic is Stable (JLC 1993).** Establishes that the defeasible part of an acyclic theory cannot derive both +∂p and -∂p. The Auxiliary Lemma in Theorem 17 (no modular defeater eliminator) depends on this stability result. Should be retrieved if propstore wants the foundational stability proof.
- **[13] Maher, Rock, Antoniou, Billington, Miller — Efficient defeasible reasoning systems (CL 2000).** The Delores implementation paper that operationalizes the linear-time consequence algorithm enabled by this paper's transformations. Critical follow-up if propstore wants to wire in the actual evaluator.
- **[10] Grosof — Prioritized Conflict Handling for Logic Programs (ILPS 1997).** Source of the "team A vs team B" metaphor used here. Courteous Logic Programs is the closest sibling system to DL.
- **[16] Nute — Defeasible Logic (Handbook chapter, 1994).** The canonical textbook treatment of DL prior to this paper. Foundational.
- **[12] Kowalski, Toni — Abstract Argumentation (AI & Law 1996).** Independent prior superiority-elimination construction in credulous abstract argumentation; the paper notes this approach does not handle DL defeaters correctly.
