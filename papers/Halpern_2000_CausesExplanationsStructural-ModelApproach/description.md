Proposes a formal definition of actual causation using structural equation models and counterfactual interventions, addressing overdetermination, preemption, and other cases that defeat simpler counterfactual accounts. The three-part definition (AC1-AC3) partitions endogenous variables into frozen and free sets to find contingencies under which the cause makes a difference to the effect, with a minimality condition preventing spurious causal attributions. Relevant to propstore's argumentation layer as a principled framework for reasoning about causal relationships between claims, complementing ATMS assumption-tracking with formal criteria for when one datum can be said to cause another.
