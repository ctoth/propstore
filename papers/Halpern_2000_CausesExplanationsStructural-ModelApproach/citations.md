## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Halpern and Pearl (2005b), "Part II: Explanations"** - The companion paper extending this framework to define explanation, which would be directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer's ability to generate explanations.
- **Pearl (2000), "Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference"** - Comprehensive textbook treatment of the structural equation framework; essential background for implementing any HP causation system.
- **Chockler and Halpern (2004), "Responsibility and Blame"** - Extends HP causation to graded notions of responsibility, which could inform degree-of-support reasoning in argumentation.
- **Hall (2004), "Two concepts of causation"** - Distinguishes counterfactual dependence from production as two fundamentally different causal concepts; relevant for understanding limitations of any single formal account.
- **Eiter and Lukasiewicz (2002), "Complexity results for structure-based causality"** - Computational complexity of checking HP causation; essential for understanding scalability of any implementation.
