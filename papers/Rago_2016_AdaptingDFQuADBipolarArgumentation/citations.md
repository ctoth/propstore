## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[6] Rago et al. 2016 (KR)** — The original DF-QuAD paper defining the algorithm for QuAD frameworks. Already in our collection as `papers/Rago_2016_DiscontinuityFreeQuAD/`. This is the parent paper that the current one adapts.

- **[1] Amgoud et al. 2016 (KR)** — Defines social models with compensation-based ranking semantics for AFs. Provides the gradual valuation that this paper compares against. Relevant for understanding alternative quantitative approaches.

- **[7] Baroni et al. 2015** — Original QuAD framework definition. Provides the structural foundation (arguments, pro/con children, base scores) that this paper adapts away from.

- **[2] Cayrol & Lagasquie-Schiex 2005** — Foundational paper on bipolar argumentation frameworks. Already in our collection. Defines BAFs with attack and support relations.

- **[3] Matt & Toni 2008** — Game-theoretic argument strength measures. Alternative approach to quantitative evaluation of arguments.
