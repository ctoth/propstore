# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

We define a quantitative semantics for evaluating the strength of arguments in Bipolar Argumentation Frameworks (BAFs) adapted from the DF-QuAD algorithm. First QuAD (DF-QuAD) algorithm previously used for evaluating the strength of arguments in Quantitative Argumentation Debate (QuAD) frameworks. We also sketched and compared some properties of the semantics and its relationship to other argumentation frameworks, as well as some properties of the semantics.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper adapts the DF-QuAD (Discontinuity-Free Quantitative Argumentation Debate) algorithm, originally designed for QuAD frameworks requiring base scores, to work on Bipolar Argumentation Frameworks (BAFs) which have no base scores. The key contribution is a three-function decomposition (strength aggregation, mediating function, score function) that computes argument strength using an implicit default of 0.5, with proofs of continuity and correspondence to the original QuAD semantics.
