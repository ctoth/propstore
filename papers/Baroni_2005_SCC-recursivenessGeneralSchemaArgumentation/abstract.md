# Abstract

In the context of Dung's argumentation framework, we propose a general recursive scheme for argumentation semantics, based on decomposition along the strongly connected components. This extends and generalizes our previous work. We introduce the fundamental notion of SCC-recursiveness and show that all of Dung's semantics, based-semantics on SCT varieties, and therefore a special case of our scheme. The space of SCC-recursive semantics offers several advantages from the theoretical standpoint, starting from the analysis of several examples where Dung's preferred semantics gives rise to counterintuitive conclusions. We show that some SCC-recursive semantics are able to overcome the limitations of preferred semantics, while offering in other respects desirable properties. In particular, the approach significantly departs from the traditional notion of admissibility, and leads to the identification of a number of properties which, in particular, is able to correctly deal with the theory of self-defeating arguments.

## Our Interpretation

This paper establishes the SCC-recursive schema as a principled way to define argumentation semantics by decomposing frameworks along strongly connected components and applying a local "base function" within each. The key practical contribution is CF2 semantics, which replaces admissibility with maximal conflict-freeness, producing meaningful (non-empty) extensions for odd-length cycles and frameworks with self-defeating arguments. For propstore, this offers both a computational optimization (SCC decomposition reduces problem size) and an alternative semantics when preferred semantics yields uninformative empty extensions.
