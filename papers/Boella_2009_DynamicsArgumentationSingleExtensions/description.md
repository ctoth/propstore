Extends the principle-based evaluation of Dung argumentation semantics to dynamic abstraction operations that remove attacks or arguments while asking whether the single extension stays unchanged.
Characterizes, in a label-sensitive way, which attack-abstraction and argument-abstraction principles are satisfied by grounded semantics and which fail without extra side conditions.
Directly relevant to propstore's need to understand when deleting claims or attack edges should preserve grounded conclusions and when a local change can legitimately propagate through the extension.
