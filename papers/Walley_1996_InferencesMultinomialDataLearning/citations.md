## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Walley (1991)** - The comprehensive monograph on imprecise probabilities that grounds all the theory in this paper. Essential for understanding the mathematical framework (coherent upper/lower probabilities, natural extension, decision theory).
- **Dempster (1966)** - Foundational paper on reasoning towards posterior distributions. IDM with s=1 agrees with Dempster's method. Important for understanding the connection between imprecise probabilities and Dempster-Shafer theory.
- **Good (1962, 1965)** - Bayesian inferences from multinomial data using continuous mixtures of Dirichlet distributions. Good's approach is the closest Bayesian analogue to the IDM.
- **Smets (1994)** - Belief functions induced by knowledge of probabilities. Agrees with IDM when s=1. Important connection to transferable belief model.
- **Carnap (1950, 1952)** - Inductive logic foundations. Walley suggests the IDM could solve Carnap's problem of finding a principled "continuum of inductive methods." The parameter s plays the role Carnap sought.
