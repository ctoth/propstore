This paper presents a comprehensive overview of the 5th International Competition on Computational Models of Argumentation (ICCMA 2023), covering abstract argumentation frameworks and assumption-based argumentation with multiple reasoning tasks and semantics. Key findings include the dominance of SAT-based solvers (HARPER++, FARGO-LIMITED) for abstract argumentation and ASP-based solvers (ASPFORABA) for ABA, with the introduction of a new compact input format and incremental Dynamic track API. Directly relevant to the project's computational argumentation research as it establishes current performance baselines and identifies the state-of-the-art solving approaches across multiple argumentation formalisms.
