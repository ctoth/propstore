# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The study of computational models of argumentation and the development of practical computational approaches for solving the associated reasoning problems have been central themes in formal argumentation research in recent years. The series of International Competitions on Computational Models of Argumentation (ICCMA) serve as recurring events aimed at fostering the development of practical computational approaches for various reasoning problems in formal models of argumentation, and thereby provide a snapshot of the current state of the art in algorithm implementations for central fundamental reasoning tasks in models of argumentation. The year 2023 saw the 5th instantiation of ICCMA with various new developments, including the adoption of assumption-based argumentation as a formalism alongside abstract argumentation, a more compact input format, and a new no-limits track for solvers. This article provides a comprehensive overview of ICCMA 2023, including details on the various new developments made, discussion on the formalisms, semantics, and computational problems focused on, the organizational details and rules followed, details on the benchmarks and results, as well as lessons learned.

---

## Our Interpretation

This paper documents the 5th ICCMA competition which evaluated solvers for both abstract argumentation frameworks (AFs) and, for the first time, assumption-based argumentation (ABA). SAT-based solvers dominated AF reasoning while ASP-based solvers led ABA tracks. The paper is essential reference material for understanding the current state of the art in computational argumentation, including standard I/O formats, benchmark methodologies, and solver performance baselines.
