## Key Citations for Follow-up

These references are most relevant for propstore's argumentation/contextual layer and warrant tracking the citing paper relationships:

### Direct DeLP foundations
- **García & Simari (2004)** "Defeasible logic programming: An argumentative approach." *TPLP*. — The canonical DeLP paper. Defines (Π, Δ) programs, defeasible derivation, dialectical trees, warrant. Necessary upstream for any DeLP-grounded implementation.
- **Simari & Loui (1992)** "A mathematical treatment of defeasible reasoning and its implementation." *Artificial Intelligence* 53. — Mathematical foundations of the dialectical procedure DeLP inherits.
- **García (2000)** PhD thesis. Operational semantics + parallelism for DeLP. Context for the server execution model.

### DeLP-Server contextual-query lineage
- **García, Rotstein, Tucat, & Simari (2007)** KSEM. Initial argumentative reasoning service for deliberative agents.
- **Tucat, García, & Simari (2009)** AAAI Fall Symposium. Contextual queries for recommender servers — direct predecessor of §5-6 of this paper.

### Explanation lineage
- **García, Rotstein, Chesñevar, & Simari (2009)** ExaCt. Explaining why something is warranted in DeLP.
- **García, Chesñevar, Rotstein, & Simari (2013)** *Expert Systems with Applications*. Dialectical-explanation support for KBSes — the §6 explanation framework.

### Abstract / structured argumentation context
- **Dung (1995)** "On the acceptability of arguments..." *AI* 77. The foundational AF paper that propstore's `propstore.world` and Dung wiring derive from.
- **Prakken (2010)** "An abstract framework for argumentation with structured arguments." *A&C* 1. ASPIC+ — the structured framework propstore's `propstore.aspic_bridge` implements.
- **Bondarenko, Dung, Kowalski, & Toni (1997)** ABA. Abstract assumption-based argumentation, related to ATMS-style assumption labels.

### DeLP family extensions worth tracking
- **Pardo & Godo (2011)** t-DeLP. Temporal extension — relevant to propstore's TIMEPOINT/Allen integration.
- **Alsinet et al. (2008)** Possibilistic-fuzzy DeLP (P-DeLP). Relevant if subjective-logic style certainty becomes a goal.
- **Budín et al. (2012)** ST-DeLP / E-TAF. Time + reliability in structured argumentation.
- **Gómez, Chesñevar, & Simari (2013)** ONTOarg. DeLP-style argumentation over ontology integration — closest to propstore's vocabulary/concept reconciliation use case.
- **Deagustini et al. (2013)** RDBMS as defeasible argumentation source — relevant when wiring storage to argumentation.

### Survey / reference works
- **Chesñevar, Maguitman, & Loui (2000)** "Logical models of argument." *ACM Computing Surveys*. Foundational survey.
- **Bench-Capon & Dunne (2007)** "Argumentation in artificial intelligence." *AI* 171. Argumentation-in-AI survey.
- **Rahwan & Simari (2009)** *Argumentation in artificial intelligence*. Springer handbook.
- **Besnard & Hunter (2008)** *Elements of argumentation*. MIT Press.
- **Prakken & Vreeswijk (2002)** "Logics for defeasible argumentation." *Handbook of Philosophical Logic*.
