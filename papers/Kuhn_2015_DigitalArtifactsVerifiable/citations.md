## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[11] Kuhn & Dumontier 2014, ESWC** — The earlier conference version of this paper; contains the original trusty URI proposal. Relevant as the foundational reference for the extended version.
- **[12] Groth, Gibson & Velterop 2010** — "The anatomy of a nanopublication" — defines the nanopublication structure that trusty URIs are primarily designed to verify. Relevant to understanding the target use case.
- **[13] RFC 6920 — Named Information (ni:) URI scheme** — The closest prior art to trusty URIs; embeds hashes in URIs but in a separate namespace from HTTP. The paper argues trusty URIs improve on this.
- **[17] Carroll 2003 — Signing RDF graphs** — Prior work on cryptographic verification of RDF specifically; trusty URIs generalize beyond RDF-only approaches.
- **[22] Callahan, Cruz-Toledo & Dumontier 2013 — Bio2RDF** — The primary large-scale evaluation dataset; demonstrates trusty URIs at scale with over 1 billion triples.
