# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The current Web has no general mechanisms to make digital artifacts — such as datasets, code, texts, and images — verifiable and permanent. We propose trusty URIs containing cryptographic hash values. We show how trusty URIs can be used for the verification of digital artifacts, in a manner that is independent of the serialization format of the data and of the used encoding. We can make the contents of digital artifacts on the Web immutable, and we can make sure that the referenced artifacts are the same. Our approach adheres to the core principles of the Web, namely openness and decentralization. We present experimental results that demonstrate the feasibility of our approach, and that it remains practical even for very large files.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper addresses the lack of built-in content verification on the Web by proposing URIs that embed cryptographic hashes of the referenced content. This allows anyone to verify that a digital artifact has not been modified, without relying on a central authority. The approach is particularly relevant to propstore's content-hash addressed storage model, where immutability and verifiability of knowledge artifacts (claims, stances, concepts) are foundational requirements.
