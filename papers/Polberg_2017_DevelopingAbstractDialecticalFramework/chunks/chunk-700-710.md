## Theorems / Propositions / Lemmas

### Preceding Theorem (number not visible in this chunk; proof opens at top of p.700) *(p.700-701)*
The proof body shows the theorem to be a correspondence theorem of the form: `S` is a cc-complete extension of $D$ iff its associated `E` is a complete extension of $SF^D_{CC}$, with the relation being **one-to-one** and **monotonic** ($S \subset S'$ iff $E \subset E'$). The proof closes on p.701 by invoking Theorems **2.1.11** and **3.3.21** to transfer the result to **preferred** and **grounded** extensions of $D$ vs $SF^D_{CC}$. *(p.700-701)*

The theorem itself was stated in an earlier chunk (likely Theorem 4.10.7, 4.10.8, or 4.10.9 — chunk 690-700 will pin the number). Within this chunk it can be referred to as "the cc-correspondence theorem".

### Theorem 4.10.10 *(p.701, statement; proof spans p.702-703)*
> Let $D = (A, C)$ be a weakly valid ADF and $SF^D_{AA} = (A, R')$ its associated SETAF created through Translation 88. If $E \subseteq A$ is pd-acyclic conflict-free (aa-admissible) in $D$, then it is conflict-free (admissible) in $SF^D_{AA}$. Not every conflict-free (admissible) extension of $SF^D_{AA}$ is pd-acyclic conflict-free (aa-admissible) in $D$. $E \subseteq A$ is an aa-complete (aa-preferred, acyclic grounded, stable) extension of $D$ iff it is a complete (preferred, grounded, stable) extension of $SF^D_{AA}$.

Proof technique (verbatim from p.702-703):
- Forward direction: every $a \in E$ has an acyclic pd-evaluation $(F,B)$ with $B \cap E = \emptyset$, so no $E' \subseteq E$ blocks all evaluations of $a$, hence no $E' R a$, hence $E$ is conflict-free in $SF^D_{AA}$.
- Counterexample for the failure of the converse direction: the ADF $(\{a,b\}, \{C_a = \top, C_b = a\})$ already cited above. *(p.702)*
- For the aa-complete <-> complete equivalence, the proof analyses each pd-acyclic sequence $F = (a_0, \dots, a_n)$ and shows by induction on the index that the **t** part of $a_i$'s decisively-in interpretation is contained in $\{a_0, \dots, a_{i-1}\}$, so each $a_i$ must lie in $E$ when $E$ is aa-complete. *(p.703)*
- Closes by invoking discarded-set analysis and Theorems **2.1.24, 2.1.25, 3.3.3, 3.3.21** to transfer to preferred / grounded / stable. *(p.704)*

### Proposition 4.10.13 *(p.703)*
> Let $SF = (A, R)$ be a SETAF and $E, E' \subseteq A$ two admissible extensions of $SF$. If there are no $b \in E, B' \subseteq E'$ s.t. $B'Rb$ and no $b' \in E', B \subseteq E$ s.t. $BRb'$, then $E \cup E'$ is admissible in $SF$.

Proof technique (verbatim summary, p.703): assume $X = E \cup E'$ is not conflict-free; case-split on which side the attacked element lies in; admissibility of each side forces an attacker $z \in X$ that conflicts with conflict-freeness of $E$ (or $E'$) — contradiction. Then admissibility (defence) of the union follows from admissibility of each side.

### Theorem 4.10.17 *(p.703, statement; proof p.703-707, ends mid-page 707 with $\square$ at top)*
> Let $D = (A, C)$ be a cleansed-form ADF and $FN^D_{AA} = (A', R', N')$ its corresponding AFN obtained through Translation 89. Then, $FN^D_{AA}$ is in minimal and (strongly) consistent normal forms. $FN^D_{AA}$ might not be weakly valid if $D$ is weakly valid. If $D$ is relation valid, then $FN^D_{AA}$ is weakly and relation valid. If $D$ is strongly valid, then so is $FN^D_{AA}$.

Proof outline:
- **Minimal normal form:** every $(a, v_a) \in A'$ has supports built per Translation 89 from disjoint and incomparable sets, hence trivially in minimal normal form. *(p.703)*
- **Strongly consistent normal form:** $v_a^{\mathbf{t}} \cap v_a^{\mathbf{f}} = \emptyset$, so an argument $(c, v_c)$ created from $c \in v_a^{\mathbf{f}}$ cannot appear in a supporting set of $(a, v_a)$. *(p.703)*
- **Weak validity preservation under weak validity of $D$:** for each acyclic pd-evaluation $((a_0, \dots, a_n), B)$ on $D$, build the corresponding sequence $((a_0, pd^D_E(a_0)), \dots, (a_n, pd^D_E(a_n)))$ in $FN^D_{AA}$; it is a powerful sequence by the proof technique of Theorem 4.10.18. Then handle arguments paired with **unused** interpretations by inductively extending the powerful sequence using `$v_a^{\mathbf{t}} \subseteq A$` and the existence of $(b, v_b) \in E$ for each such $b$. *(p.704)*
- **Relation validity preservation:** define $coh(X)$ = collection of all coherent sets on $X \subseteq A'$ and $Arg(a) = \{(a, v_a) \mid v_a \in min\_dec(in, a)\}$. Use these to show that if $D$ is relation valid (and hence weakly valid by `Lemma 4.2.77`), then so is $FN^D_{AA}$. The argument is by contradiction: assume $FN^D_{AA}$ not relation valid, derive a chain of dependence between $(a, v_a)$ and $(b, v_b)$ that propagates back to $D$ and breaches relation/weak validity there. Two sub-cases: (i) $(b, v_b)$ is the only argument representing $b$, (ii) $b$ has alternative representations. *(p.704-705)*
- **Strong validity preservation:** by Theorem 4.2.44 obtain a sequence $(a_0, \dots, a_n)$ that is a pd-sequence under any pd-function. For each $a_i$ enumerate all `$seq_i = ((a_i, v^1_{a_i}), \dots, (a_i, v^{n_i}_{a_i}))$`. Concatenate the $seq_i$ in order; show by induction on $i$ that the merged sequence is powerful w.r.t. any chosen support function $f$ (Definition 4.2.30). Conclude via Theorem 4.2.33 that $FN^D_{AA}$ is strongly valid. *(p.705)*

### Theorem 4.10.18 *(p.705, statement spans p.705-706; proof p.706-710 ending mid-page 710)*
> Let $D = (A, C)$ be a cleansed-form ADF and $FN^D_{AA} = (A', R', N')$ its corresponding AFN obtained through Translation 89. If $S = \{(a_1, v_{a_1}), \dots, (a_n, v_{a_n})\} \subseteq A'$ is a coherent (strongly coherent, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable) extension of $FN^D_{AA}$, then $S' = \bigcup_{i=1}^n \{a_i\}$ is a pd-acyclic (pd-acyclic conflict-free, aa-admissible, aa-preferred, aa-complete, acyclic grounded, stable) extension of $D$. If $S' \subseteq A$ is a pd-acyclic (pd-acyclic conflict-free, aa-admissible, aa-preferred, aa-complete, acyclic grounded, stable) extension of $D$, then there exists a coherent (strongly coherent, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable) extension $S = \{(a_1, v_{a_1}), \dots, (a_n, v_{a_n})\} \subseteq A'$ of $FN^D_{AA}$ s.t. $S' = \bigcup_{i=1}^n \{a_i\}$.

Proof outline (a long induction-style proof spanning five pages):

1. **Acyclic pd-evaluation on $D$ <-> powerful sequence on $FN^D_{AA}$** via Translation 89. By the pd-sequence requirement $pd^D_E(a_0)^{\mathbf{t}} = \emptyset$, so by Translation 89 there is no set $V \subseteq A'$ with $VN(a_0, pd^D_E(a_0))$, so $(a_0, pd^D_E(a_0))$ satisfies the starting-argument condition of a powerful sequence. Inductively, every $V \subseteq A'$ s.t. $VN(a_i, pd^D_E(a_i))$ is generated by some $c \in pd^D_E(a_i)^{\mathbf{t}} \subseteq \{a_0, \dots, a_{i-1}\}$, so $V$ intersects the prefix sequence. *(p.706)*
2. **Purging.** Given a powerful sequence $seq = ((b_0, v_{b_0}), \dots, (b_m, v_{b_m}))$, construct a subsequence $seq'$ by greedily adding the first occurrence of each *underlying* argument $b_i$. Show $seq'$ is still a powerful sequence; the last argument matches the last argument of $seq$. The construction uses Example 158 on p.443 to motivate why duplication of decisively-in interpretations occurs. *(p.706-707)*
3. **pd-acyclic conflict-free <-> strongly coherent.** Forward: any $S \subseteq A$ pd-acyclic conflict-free in $D$ induces $S' \subseteq A'$ via the pairs $(b, pd^D_S)$; the **f** part of any used interpretation lies in some blocking set disjoint from $S$, so no $c, d \in S'$ have $cR'd$; therefore $S'$ is strongly coherent in $FN^D_{AA}$. Reverse: $S'$ strongly coherent forces $v_{a_j}(a_i) = \mathbf{f}$ to fail for any $(a_i, v_{a_i}), (a_j, v_{a_j}) \in S'$, so each $a \in S$ admits an acyclic pd-evaluation with blocking set disjoint from $S$. *(p.707)*
4. **aa-admissible <-> admissible.** Use the same pair-construction. If $S'$ not admissible there is $(b, v_b) \in S', (c, v_c) \in A'$ with $(c, v_c)R'(b, v_b)$ and a coherent $V \ni (c, v_c)$ unattacked by $S'$. Show $v_b(c) = \mathbf{f}$, build a powerful sequence for $(c, v_c)$ from $V$, purge, extract an acyclic pd-evaluation for $c$ with blocking set disjoint from $S$, contradict $c \notin S^{a+}$. *(p.707-708)*
5. **aa-complete <-> complete.** Use an extended construction: $E = \{(a, v_a) \mid a \in S, v_a^{\mathbf{t}} \subseteq S, v_a^{\mathbf{f}} \subseteq S^{a+}\}$ (interpretations whose acyclic range completes them). Show $E$ conflict-free (since $S \cap S^{a+} = \emptyset$ by Lemma 3.1.17); show $E$ coherent (each $E \setminus S'$ argument extends the powerful sequence representing $S'$); show $E$ admissible by the previous part; show $E$ complete by contradiction using coherence-of-defence requirements. The reverse direction uses the acyclic-range completion of $S'$ to lift to $S$. *(p.708-709)*
6. **One-to-one between aa-complete extensions of $D$ and complete extensions of $FN^D_{AA}$.** Suppose two distinct complete extensions $E, E' \subseteq A'$ project to the same $S$; without loss take $(a, v_a) \in E \setminus E'$. Coherence-construction shows $E' \cup \{(a, v_a)\}$ would still be coherent, so $E'$ must defend $(a, v_a)$, contradicting completeness of $E'$. *(p.709)*
7. **$\subset$-monotonicity:** $S \subset S'$ iff $E \subset E'$, where $E, E'$ are the associated complete extensions. Forward via Lemma 3.1.17 (acyclic range of $S'$ completes acyclic range of $S$). Reverse direct. *(p.709)*
8. **Closure:** invoke Theorems **2.2.31** and **3.3.21** plus the established one-to-one + monotonicity to lift to **preferred** and **grounded** extensions. The proof closes with $\square$ at the top of PDF p.710. *(p.710)*

## Algorithms / Procedures

### Procedure — Purge a powerful sequence *(p.706-707)*
Given $seq = ((b_0, v_{b_0}), \dots, (b_m, v_{b_m}))$:
1. Initialise $seq' := ((b_0, v_{b_0}))$.
2. Find the first $i$ with $b_i \neq b_0$. Add $(b_i, v_{b_i})$ to $seq'$. (Argue powerful: $v_{b_i}^{\mathbf{t}} \subseteq \{b_0, \dots, b_{i-1}\} = \{b_0\}$, so $\{(b_0, v_{b_0})\}$ intersects every supporting set of $(b_i, v_{b_i})$.)
3. Find the first argument in $seq$ representing an underlying argument different from $b_0$ and $b_i$ — call it $(b_j, v_{b_j})$, with $j > i$. Add it to $seq'$.
4. Repeat step 3 until no further argument can be added.
5. Output $seq'$. The last element of $seq'$ corresponds to the same underlying argument as the last element of $seq$. For every $(b_k, v_{b_k}) \in seq$ there is some $(b_p, v_{b_p}) \in seq'$ with $b_p = b_k$ (interpretations need not match).

This is the only fully-spelled-out algorithmic content of the chunk; everything else is mathematical-prose proof.

### Procedure — Lift aa-complete extension of $D$ to complete extension of $FN^D_{AA}$ *(p.708)*
Given an aa-complete $S \subseteq A$:
1. Form the candidate $E := \{(a, v_a) \mid a \in S,\; v_a^{\mathbf{t}} \subseteq S,\; v_a^{\mathbf{f}} \subseteq S^{a+}\}$.
2. Verify conflict-freeness using $S \cap S^{a+} = \emptyset$ (Lemma 3.1.17).
3. Verify coherence: for each $(a, v_a) \in E \setminus S'$, append $(a, v_a)$ to the powerful sequence representing $S'$.
4. Verify admissibility using the aa-admissible <-> admissible part of the proof.
5. Verify completeness by contradiction: assume some $(a, v_a) \notin E$ is defended by $E$; derive that $a$ is decisively-in w.r.t. the acyclic range of $S$ in $D$, hence $a \in S$ by aa-completeness, contradicting $(a, v_a) \notin E$.

## Design Rationale
- **Why an extended construction $E$ for the aa-complete <-> complete direction?** Because Example 158 on p.443 shows the natural set $S' = \{(a, v_a) \mid a \in S \text{ and there exists pd-function } pd \text{ with } pd(a) = v_a\}$ is admissible but not complete in $FN^D_{AA}$. The fix is to extend with all pairs whose **f** part is contained in $S^{a+}$, not just those whose interpretation was actually used. *(p.708)*
- **Why purge powerful sequences?** Because powerful sequences in $FN^D_{AA}$ admit duplicate underlying arguments paired with different interpretations, but acyclic pd-evaluations in $D$ cannot. Purging is a one-pass canonicalisation that removes duplicates while preserving the powerful-sequence property, enabling the AFN-to-ADF lift. *(p.706-707)*
- **Why factor the validity preservation through 4.10.18 first, then reuse in 4.10.17?** The "powerful sequence on $A' \setminus E$ extends a powerful sequence on $E$" lemma is needed in both proofs; Polberg structures the chapter so 4.10.18's proof establishes the powerful-sequence machinery (steps 1-2 above) and 4.10.17 cites that machinery rather than re-deriving it. *(p.704)*
- **Cleansed-form precondition on $D$ for Theorem 4.10.17 / 4.10.18:** ensures the AFN argument set $A' = \{(a, v_a) \mid v_a \in \min\_dec(in, a)\}$ is non-empty for every $a$, which is needed for both the strong-validity concatenation and the purging construction. *(p.703, p.705)*

## Implementation Notes
- **Translation 88 (ADF -> SETAF, aa-treatment):** the implementation surface needed is (a) for each $a \in A$, enumerate every minimal decisively-in interpretation $v_a$; (b) for each such $v_a$, produce the pd-acyclic sequences over $A$ that yield acyclic pd-evaluations; (c) the SETAF attack relation $R' \subseteq 2^A \times A$ is induced by the blocking sets of these evaluations. The asymmetry (admissible-in-SETAF does not imply aa-admissible-in-ADF) means the translation cannot be used as a back-channel: queries about the ADF must be answered against the ADF, not against the SETAF projection.
- **Translation 89 (cleansed ADF -> AFN):** $A' = \{(a, v_a) \mid v_a \in \min\_dec(in, a)\}$. Attacks $R'$ between $(c, v_c)$ and $(b, v_b)$ when $v_b(c) = \mathbf{f}$. Supports $N'$ between $V \subseteq A'$ and $(a, v_a)$ when $V$ "represents" $v_a^{\mathbf{t}}$ in the sense made precise by the powerful-sequence requirement (Definition 4.2.30 referenced).
- **Coherence vs strong coherence (AFN side):** strong coherence corresponds to pd-acyclic conflict-freeness on the ADF side. An implementation should expose **both** as separate predicates because the aa-admissibility correspondence requires the strong variant; using plain coherence would silently admit non-faithful extensions.
- **Extended-construction completion:** when lifting an admissible AFN extension to the ADF, do not just project $S' = \bigcup \{a_i\}$ — also enrich the AFN side with pairs $(a, v_a)$ whose **f** part lies in $S^{a+}$, then verify completeness on the enriched set. This is the $E = \{(a, v_a) \mid a \in S, v_a^{\mathbf{t}} \subseteq S, v_a^{\mathbf{f}} \subseteq S^{a+}\}$ recipe.
- **Purging as a callable subroutine:** anywhere code consumes an AFN powerful sequence to produce an ADF acyclic pd-evaluation, run purging first. Without it, the back-translation can produce ill-formed pd-sequences with repeated underlying arguments.
- **Validity preservation gate:** when feeding a weakly-valid ADF through Translation 89, do **not** assume the resulting AFN is weakly valid. Validity preservation kicks in at relation validity. Code should expose `is_relation_valid(D)` as the precondition for "the AFN translation preserves validity end-to-end."
- **Theorem chains used at the boundary:** the chunk shows the canonical pattern is: prove iff for **complete** extensions, then use the project's monotonicity + one-to-one + Theorems 2.2.31 / 3.3.21 to get **preferred / grounded / stable** for free. An implementation of correspondence proofs in propstore's argumentation layer can mirror this — implement complete-correspondence as the primitive and derive the others.
