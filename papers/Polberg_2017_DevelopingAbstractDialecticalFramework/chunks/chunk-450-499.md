## Key Findings

- Five hard impossibility theorems (4.10.12, 4.10.14, 4.10.15, 4.10.16) rule out exact (full or source-subclass) ADF→SETAF translations under cf, pd-acyclic-cf, ca1/ca2-admissible, ca1/ca2-complete, and (model) stable / (labeling) preferred semantics — even when restricted to BADFs or AADF+ *(p.450-451)*.
- Translation 89 (ADF→AFN) is the **only** translation in the chapter that escapes the four-pattern classification (basic, coalition, attack-propagation, defender) — Polberg explicitly leaves it unclassified and flags this as a known gap *(p.453)*.
- Translation 89 produces an AFN where each ADF argument `a` is duplicated into multiple `(a, v_a)` pairs — one per minimal decisively-in interpretation — and group support in AFN is used to encode the disjunctive nature of ADF acceptance conditions *(p.452)*.
- The translation requires a **cleansed-form** ADF as input — falsum-equivalent conditions cannot just be cleansed; they could equivalently be turned into self-supporters, but cleansing is preferred to keep ca/ac/cc semantics aligned *(p.452)*.
- The translation is strong (not exact) under most semantics, semantics-bijective under (aa-)complete/preferred/(acyclic) grounded/stable, and semi-structural rather than structural due to the minimality assumption on chosen interpretations *(p.453)*.
- Translation is neither ⊕- nor ⊗-modular; the counter-example uses two single-argument ADFs whose merge introduces a redundant link *(p.453)*.
- Translation 90 (sketch only): an "improvement" to Translation 89 that groups together those minimal decisively-in interpretations sharing the **same f-part**, yielding fewer but larger AFN arguments *(p.457)*.
- §4.10.4 explicitly **declines** to translate ADFs to AFRA, EAF/EAFC, BAF, or EAS, with reasoning per framework: AFRA recursive attack is not seen as superseding binary attack for ADFs; BAF lacks the support ingredient in defense; EAS would essentially be ADF-AFN merged with AFN-EAS. The author flags BAFs as "in a sense more problematic than AFNs" because of missing support in defense definition *(p.457-458)*.
- Table 4.11 summarizes all ADF→other translations with strength × functional × syntactic × semantic × computational dimensions; ADF→AFN is the unique semi-structural row *(p.459)*.
- Conclusions assert ADFs emerged "as one of the most general tools for abstract argumentation, capable of handling even the extended argumentation framework" *(p.467)*.
- Future work flags: (1) categorize ADF-AFN within DW11's classification, (2) complexity analysis (least attention paid), (3) more advanced ADF modularity, (4) BAF "missing" grounded/complete semantics, (5) BAF subclasses where semantic classification collapses analogously to AADF+ for ADFs *(p.467-468)*.
- The proof appendix begins (A1 Background, A2 ADFs); proofs for Theorems 2.1.47, 2.1.55, 2.1.60, Lemma 2.1.61, BAF Fundamental Lemma 2.2.8, Lemmas 2.2.12, 2.2.28, 2.2.30, 3.1.14, 3.1.16, 3.1.17, 3.1.19, 3.1.21, 3.1.22, 3.1.23, 3.1.24, 3.1.25, Theorems 3.2.6, 3.2.7, 3.3.2, 3.3.3, Lemmas 3.3.4, 3.3.5, Proposition 3.3.13, Lemma 3.3.14, Lemma 3.3.15 *(p.484-499)*.

## Theorems / Propositions / Lemmas

### Proposition 4.10.11 *(p.450)*
Let `SF = (A, R)` be a SETAF. The set of conflict-free extensions `cf(SF)` of `SF` is downward-closed.
*Used as a contrast: ADFs do NOT have downward-closed cf, exhibited by `D_1 = ({a, b, c, d}, {C_a = ¬b ∨ ¬c ∨ d, C_b = ¬a ∨ ¬c ∨ d, C_c = ¬a ∨ ¬b ∨ d, C_d = ⊤})` whose cf-extensions include `{a, b}, {b, c}, {a, c}, {a, b, c, d}` but not `{a, b, c}`.*

### Theorem 4.10.12 *(p.450)*
Let `Fr^ADF` be the collection of all ADFs, `BADF` of all BADFs, `AADF+` of all AADF+s on `U^ADF`, and `Fr^SETAF` the SETAFs on `U^AF`. There exists no full (resp. source-subclass) translation from `Fr^ADF` (resp. `BADF`, `AADF+`) to `Fr^SETAF` exact under conflict-free and pd-acyclic conflict-free semantics with identity casting functions.

### Proposition 4.10.13 *(p.450)*
Let `SF = (A, R)` be a SETAF and `E, E' ⊆ A` two admissible extensions of `SF`. If there are no `b ∈ E, B' ⊆ E'` s.t. `B' R b` and no `b' ∈ E', B ⊆ E` s.t. `B R b'`, then `E ∪ E'` is admissible in `SF`.
*Quasi-closure under non-conflicting union — the SETAF analog of admissibility closure.*

### Theorem 4.10.14 *(p.450)*
No full / source-subclass translation from `Fr^ADF` (resp. BADF) to `Fr^SETAF` exact under (ca1, ca2)-admissible semantics. Extends to labelings via Theorem 3.4.4.

### Theorem 4.10.15 *(p.451)*
Same impossibility under (ca1, ca2)-complete semantics.

### Theorem 4.10.16 *(p.451)*
Same impossibility under (model) stable and (labeling) preferred semantics. Argued by appeal to SETAF preferred/stable always producing incomparable sets, while ADF labeling-induced preferred/model extensions can be comparable (Examples 38, 39 on pages 82, 86).

### Theorem 4.10.17 *(p.453)*
Let `D = (A, C)` be a cleansed ADF and `FN^D_AA = (A', R', N')` its corresponding AFN via Translation 89. Then `FN^D_AA` is in minimal and (strongly) consistent normal forms. May not be weakly valid even if `D` is. If `D` is relation-valid, so is `FN^D_AA` (weakly+relation valid). If `D` is strongly valid, so is `FN^D_AA`.

### Theorem 4.10.18 *(p.453)*
The semantic-bijection result for Translation 89. For `S = {(a_1, v_{a_1}), ..., (a_n, v_{a_n})} ⊆ A'`, `S` is a coherent (strongly coherent, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable) extension of `FN^D_AA` iff `S' = ∪ {a_i}` is a pd-acyclic (pd-acyclic cf, aa-admissible, aa-preferred, aa-complete, acyclic-grounded, stable) extension of `D`. Both directions stated.

### Theorem 2.1.47 *(p.484, proof appendix A1)*
Reinstatement-set characterization for EAF defeats: in a finite EAF `EF = (A, R, D)` and conflict-free `E ⊆ A`, if `a ∈ E` defeats_E `b ∈ A`, there is no reinstatement set on `E` iff there exists a defense-attack sequence `((z_1, (x_1, y_1)), ..., (z_n, (x_n, y_n)))` of distinct attacks satisfying four bullet conditions (terminal `g`, distinctness, layered protection by lower defeats, no `E`-defeater on `z_1`). Proof spans pages 484-485.

### Theorem 2.1.55 *(p.485)*
For a finitary EAF: every preferred extension is complete (not vice versa); every stable extension is complete (not vice versa); the grounded extension is a minimal complete extension (not necessarily the least). Proof: standard argument by contradiction with admissibility, plus operator-iteration argument for grounded minimality. *(p.485-486)*

### Theorem 2.1.60 *(p.487)*
Let `bh-EF = (A, R, D)` be a bounded hierarchical EAF. Every stable extension is preferred, but not vice versa. Proof uses the level structure of the partition: defeats not defense-attacked at the highest level become absolute defeats, contradicting non-stability.

### Lemma 2.1.61 *(p.487-488)*
Let `EF = (A, R, D)` be bounded hierarchical or have no symmetric attacks `(a,b),(b,a) ∈ R`. `E ⊆ A` is conflict-free iff there are no defeats_E in `E`. Proof considers the bounded hierarchical case (induction on partition levels, contradiction at the top) and the no-symmetric case (defense reduces to required defender existence).

### Lemma 2.2.8 — BAF Fundamental Lemma *(p.488)*
Let `BF = (A, R, S)` be a BAF, `R', R'' ⊆ R^ind` two collections of indirect attacks, `E ⊆ A` a d-admissible extension w.r.t. `(R', R'')` and `a, b ∈ A` arguments defended by `E` w.r.t. `R''`. If `R' = R''`, then `E' = E ∪ {a}` is d-admissible w.r.t. `(R', R'')` and `b` is defended by `E` w.r.t. `R''`. Proof: case-split on `cR'a`, `aR'c`, `aR'a`.

### Lemma 2.2.12 *(p.488)*
Let `BF = (A, R, S)` be a BAF and `R' ⊆ R^ind`. If `E ⊆ A` is +conflict-free w.r.t. `R'`, then so is `d-F^{R'}_{BF}(E)`. Proof: contradiction yields a `c ∈ E` with `(c, a) ∈ R ∪ ⋃R'`, breaking +cf.

### Theorem 2.2.16 *(p.488-489)*
For `BF = (A, R, S)` and `R' ⊆ R^ind`: every d-preferred extension w.r.t. `(R', R')` is d-complete (not v.v.); the d-grounded is the least set-inclusion d-complete; every stable is d-preferred (not v.v.). Proof: appeals to Lemma 2.2.8, Lemma 2.2.13, Proposition 2.2.15, plus Example 1 (page 11) for non-vv.

### Lemma 2.2.28 *(p.489)*
Let `FN = (A, R, N)` be an AFN and `E ⊆ A` strongly coherent. Then `E^att ⊆ E^+`.

### Lemma 2.2.30 *(p.489-490)*
For an AFN, `E ⊆ A` is stable iff strongly coherent and `E^att = A \ E`. Proof: forward direction via Lemma 2.2.28 plus completeness; reverse uses powerful sequence reasoning to show every `E^+` element is in `E^att`.

### Lemma 3.1.14 *(p.491-492)*
Let `D = (A, C)` be an ADF, `E ⊆ A`, and `X = {a ∈ A | for every standard dependency evaluation (F, B) for a in D, B ∩ E ≠ ∅}`. If `E` is conflict-free, then `X = E^+`. Proof: trivial decisively-in interpretations show `E ∩ X = ∅`; each direction by argument on minimal decisively-in interpretation construction.

### Lemma 3.1.16 *(p.492)*
For ADF `D`, conflict-free `E`, partially acyclic range `v^a_E`, and `a ∈ A \ E` with at least one acyclic pd-evaluation `((a_0, ..., a_n), B)`: the interpretation `v^a_E` blocks the evaluation iff `E ∩ B ≠ ∅`. Proof: induction on first falsified element of pd-sequence.

### Lemma 3.1.17 *(p.492-493)*
For ADF `D`, set `E ⊆ A`, `X = {a ∈ A | for every acyclic pd-evaluation (F, B) for a, B ∩ E ≠ ∅}`. If `E` is pd-acyclic conflict-free, `X ∩ E = ∅`. If `E` is conflict-free, `X \ E = E^{a+}`. (Stated with proof referencing 3.1.16 reasoning.)

### Lemma 3.1.19 *(p.493)*
For ADF `D`, set `E ⊆ A`, `E^{p+}` its partially acyclic discarded set: if `E` is conflict-free, then `E ∩ E^{p+} = ∅`. Proof: every `a ∈ E` admits a partially acyclic standard-evaluation derivative.

### Lemma 3.1.21 *(p.493)*
For ADF `D`, conflict-free `E`: `E^+ ⊆ E^{p+} ⊆ E^{a+}`. If `E` is pd-acyclic conflict-free, `E^{p+} = E^{a+}`. Proof: chains reasoning through 3.1.14, 3.1.17, 3.1.19 plus a recombination argument.

### Lemma 3.1.22 *(p.493-494)*
For ADF `D`, conflict-free `E`, two-valued `v` with `v^t = E`: if every argument in `v^f` is decisively-out w.r.t. `v`, then the partially acyclic range `v^a_E` of `E` is a completion of `v`. Plus existence-of-such-`v` claim with `v^f ⊆ E^{p+}`. Proof: trace blocking-set logic across pd-sequence.

### Lemma 3.1.23 *(p.494)*
Characterizes when a two-valued `v` with `v^t = E`, `v^f = X` is the partially acyclic range interpretation of `E`: iff every `x ∈ X` is decisively-out w.r.t. `v` and every `x ∈ A \ (E ∪ X)` has a partially acyclic evaluation `(F^x, G^x, B^x)` s.t. `B^x ∩ E = ∅, F^x ⊆ E`.

### Lemma 3.1.24 *(p.494-495)*
Analog of 3.1.22 for the acyclic range `v^a_E`. Plus: for pd-acyclic conflict-free `E`, there is an interpretation `v` with `v^t = E, v^f ⊆ E^{a+}` s.t. all in `v^f` are decisively-out.

### Lemma 3.1.25 *(p.495)*
Analog of 3.1.23 for the acyclic range under pd-acyclic conflict-free `E`.

### Theorem 3.2.6 *(p.495)*
For ADF `D`, three-valued `v` on `A` and `v'` its (maximal) two-valued sub-interpretation: `v` is admissible iff all arguments mapped to t are decisively-in w.r.t. `v'` and all mapped to f are decisively-out w.r.t. `v'`. Proof: standard characteristic-operator argument both ways.

### Theorem 3.2.7 *(p.495-496)*
For ADF `D`, admissible labeling `v` with maximal two-valued sub-interpretation `v'`: `v` is complete iff all arguments decisively-out w.r.t. `v'` are mapped to f by `v` and all decisively-in to t. Proof: characteristic-operator fixpoint argument.

### Theorem 3.3.2 *(p.496-497)*
For ADF `D`, `E ⊆ A`: `E` is pd-acyclic conflict-free in `D` iff `E` is the grounded extension of the reduct `D^E = (E, C^E)` of `D` w.r.t. `E`. Proof spans both pages: forward shows pd-sequence yields grounded-iteration; reverse rebuilds blocking-set-clean evaluation.

### Theorem 3.3.3 *(p.497)*
A model `E ⊆ A` of `D` is a stable extension iff it is pd-acyclic conflict-free in `D`. Proof: short — follows from Definition 3.2.4 and Theorem 3.3.2.

### Lemma 3.3.4 *(p.497)*
`E ⊆ A` is stable in `D` iff it is a pd-acyclic conflict-free extension with `E^{a+} = A \ E`. Proof uses Lemma 3.3.5 plus Proposition 3.3.13.

### Lemma 3.3.5 *(p.497-498)*
For ADF `D`, `E ⊆ A`: if `E` is a model, `E^{a+} = A \ E` and `E^{p+} = A \ E`. Plus reverse direction. Proof spans both pages with pd-sequence induction in both acyclic and partially acyclic cases.

### Proposition 3.3.13 *(p.498-499)*
Let `D = (A, C)` be ADF, `E ⊆ A` standard, `S ⊆ A` pd-acyclic conflict-free, with corresponding standard / partially acyclic / acyclic ranges `v_E, v^p_E, v^a_E, v_S, v^p_S, v^a_S`. Four bullet results relating `v(s) = f` to decisive-out and acceptance-condition outcomes, with explicit asymmetry: the f → C_s(E ∩ par(s)) = out result holds for `v_E` and `v^p_E`, **but not** for `v^a_E`. Proof bullets (1)-(4): (1) recombination of partially acyclic evaluations using the proof of Theorem 3.3.2; (2) acyclic-range case via similar recombination; (3),(4) follow from (1),(2) plus definition of decisiveness.

### Example 157 *(p.451-452)*
Sample ADF `D = ({a, b, c, d, e}, {C_a = e, C_b = d ∨ (c ∧ e), C_c = ¬e, C_d = ⊤, C_e = a ∧ b})`. Demonstrates Translation 89 producing AFN `FN^D` where `b` has two representations `(b, v_b^1)` and `(b, v_b^2)` (one per minimal decisively-in interpretation). Identifies aa-admissible sets `∅, {c}, {d}, {b, d}, {c, d}, {b, c, d}` with `{b, c, d}` the unique aa-complete/aa-preferred/acyclic-grounded/stable. Walks through minimal decisively-in interpretations: `v_a = {e:t}`, `v_b^1 = {d:t}`, `v_b^2 = {c:t, e:t}`, `v_c = {e:f}`, `v_d = ∅`, `v_e = {a:t, b:t}`, then traces support and attack edges, minimal powerful sequences, and recovery of all and only ADF extensions in the AFN.

### Example 158 *(p.453)*
Two-argument ADF `D = ({a, b}, {C_a = ¬b, C_b = ¬a ∨ b})` showing `b` overrides `a`'s attack via support cycle. Minimal decisively-in interpretations `v_a = {b:f}, v_b^1 = {a:f}, v_b^2 = {b:t}`. The `v_b^2` interpretation is "undesirable" — not used by any acyclic pd-evaluation in the source extension — yet `(b, v_b^2)` is still accepted in the target AFN because another representation of `b` is "correct." Despite this, all and only desired ADF extensions are recovered. Establishes that target-AFN acceptance of an interpretation-pair does not entail the source ADF used that pd-evaluation.

### Example 159 *(p.456)*
Larger seven-argument ADF `({a, b, c, d, e, f, g}, {C_a = ¬c, C_b = f ∨ ¬c, C_c = ¬a, C_d = ⊤, C_e = g ∩ (a ∨ b) ∧ (¬c ∨ ¬d), C_f = f, C_g = ⊤})` (Figure 4.95). Walks through minimal decisively-in interpretations including `v_e^1, v_e^2, v_e^3, v_e^4` which differ only in t-parts but share f-parts `{c:f}` for `v_e^1, v_e^2`, `{d:f}` for `v_e^3, v_e^4`. Motivates Translation 90's grouping by shared f-part: the four `e`-representations collapse to two grouped representations `(e, {v_e^1, v_e^2})` and `(e, {v_e^3, v_e^4})` with shared attacker `(c, v_c)` and unioned support sets.

## Algorithms / Procedures

### Translation 89 — ADF → AFN (cleansed-form) *(p.452)*

Input: cleansed-form ADF `D = (A, C)`.
Output: AFN `FN^D_{AA} = (A', R', N')`.

1. For each `a ∈ A`, compute `min_dec(in, a)` (set of minimal decisively-in interpretations).
2. For every `v_a ∈ min_dec(in, a)`, add the pair `(a, v_a)` to `A'`.
3. For each `(a, v_a) ∈ A'` and each `b ∈ A` with `v_a(b) = t`: let `B = {(b, v_1), ..., (b, v_m)}` be all `b`-pairs in `A'`. Add `(B, (a, v_a))` to `N'` (group support edge).
4. For each `(a, v_a) ∈ A'` and each `b ∈ A` with `v_a(b) = f`: for every `(b, v_i) ∈ B`, add `((b, v_i), (a, v_a))` to `R'` (binary attack).

Properties: source-subclass, target-subclass, overlapping (functional); argument-domain altering, argument introducing, relation introducing, relation removing (syntactical); generic, semantics-domain altering (semantical); semi-structural (computational); strong (not exact except where Theorem 4.10.18 grants bijection); not modular.

### Translation 90 — ADF → grouped AFN (sketch) *(p.457)*

Input: cleansed-form ADF `D = (A, C)`.
Output: grouped AFN `g-FN^D_{AA} = (A', R', N')`.

1. For each `a ∈ A`, compute `min_dec(in, a)`. Partition `min_dec(in, a)` by f-part: collect maximal `v' ⊆ min_dec(in, a)` s.t. for every `v_1, v_2 ∈ v'`, `v_1^f = v_2^f`. For every such `v'`, add `(a, v')` to `A'`.
2. For each `(a, v') ∈ A'`, let `E ⊆ ⋃_{v ∈ v'} v^t` be a minimal set of ADF arguments s.t. for every `v ∈ v'`, `E ∩ v^t ≠ ∅`. Let `B ⊆ A'` be all arguments `(b, v_b) ∈ A'` with `b ∈ E`. Add `(B, (a, v'))` to `N'`.
3. For each `(a, v') ∈ A'` and `b ∈ A` with `∃v ∈ v', v(b) = f`: for every `(b, v_i) ∈ A'` corresponding to `b`, add `((b, v_i), (a, v'))` to `R'`.

Stated as "Sketch"; correctness proof similar to Translation 89.

## Design Rationale

- **Why Translation 89 maps each ADF argument to multiple AFN arguments:** because a single ADF argument's acceptance condition may correspond to multiple disjoint minimal decisively-in interpretations; collapsing them into one AFN argument would lose support structure *(p.451)*.
- **Why cleansed normal form is required as input:** the construction needs every argument to admit at least one decisively-in interpretation; falsum-equivalent acceptance conditions break this. Cleansing is preferred to the self-supporter alternative because it preserves ca/ac/cc-family equivalence *(p.451)*.
- **Why the translation is classified as both source-subclass and target-subclass:** source because cleansed; target because not every AFN over the new argument domain is the image of an ADF (severe domain modifications) *(p.453)*.
- **Why semi-structural rather than structural:** the minimality assumption on chosen interpretations introduces a non-purely-syntactic criterion *(p.453)*.
- **Why the four-pattern classification (basic/coalition/attack-propagation/defender) does not capture Translation 89:** the construction is a hybrid that introduces multiple representations of a single argument with associated minimal decisively-in interpretations — neither of the four patterns covers this. Polberg explicitly chooses not to classify it and treats it as evidence the system can be improved *(p.453)*.
- **Why ADF→BAF is "more problematic" than ADF→AFN:** BAFs lack the support ingredient in defense definition — two arguments mutually supporting in BAF would both be defended by `∅`, but not decisively-in w.r.t. cc-semantics standard range. Forces restriction to strongly valid ADFs even when not focusing on acyclic semantics *(p.457-458)*.
- **Why the stable semantics impossibility differs from cf/cf-acy/admissible:** because (model) stable in ADFs has signature beyond AF stable (Theorem 4.8.7), so a separate impossibility argument applies *(p.456)*.
- **Why BAFs (vs ADFs) need special handling under Translation 90's grouping idea:** BAF cc-semantics decisive-in / standard-range structure does not lift through support cycles in the way ADF acceptance conditions do *(p.458)*.
- **Why AFRA, EAF/EAFC, BAF, EAS were omitted from full ADF translations:** AFRA recursive attack adds no expressive power for ADFs; EAFs interesting due to non-monotonicity and group-attack handling but require further analysis; BAF lacks support in defense; EAS would essentially merge ADF-AFN with AFN-EAS *(p.457)*.

## Implementation Notes

For propstore implementation of ADF→AFN translation:

- **Input precondition gate:** require cleansed-form ADF (no falsum-equivalent acceptance conditions) — either run the cleansing pass first or reject. Polberg's alternative (turn falsum-conditions into self-supporters) preserves cf/aa-comp/aa-pref but breaks ca1/ca2 alignment.
- **Argument explosion:** worst-case `|A'| = Σ_a |min_dec(in, a)|`. Each ADF argument can fan out exponentially when the acceptance condition is a large DNF. Storing `min_dec(in, a)` requires enumerating prime implicants of `C_a` — natural target for a Z3-backed pass that yields prime implicants of the boolean formula constraining `par(a)`.
- **Group-support edges:** every edge in `N'` is `(B, (a, v_a))` where `B` is the *full set* of `A'`-pairs corresponding to a single source argument `b`. This is intrinsically group-typed; cannot be flattened to binary support edges without losing the "any representation suffices" semantics.
- **Translation 90 grouping:** partition `min_dec(in, a)` by f-part, then per-group choose a minimal hitting set across the t-parts. Smaller `|A'|` at cost of less obvious structural alignment with Translation 89's correctness proof.
- **Bijection-in-acceptance:** the implementation can faithfully recover all and only ADF extensions for (aa-)complete/preferred/(acyclic) grounded/stable. For aa-admissible and pd-acyclic-cf, only ⊆-weak — multiple AFN representations of the same ADF extension exist. Render layer must canonicalize.
- **Modularity is broken:** do NOT compose two ADF→AFN translations and expect `Tr(D_1 ⊕ D_2) = Tr(D_1) ∪ Tr(D_2)`. The counter-example on p.454 is small (single-argument ADFs) and immediately reproducible as a regression test.
- **Negative results to encode as Hypothesis property tests:**
    - `forall ADFs D in BADF, no exact translation to SETAF under cf` (4.10.12) — as a property: searching across BADFs and SETAFs, an exact-translation predicate must always return False.
    - same for ca1/ca2-admissible, ca1/ca2-complete, model-stable, labeling-preferred (4.10.14, 4.10.15, 4.10.16).
- **Decisive-in / decisive-out as primitives:** Lemma 3.1.14 and Proposition 3.3.13 give clean characterizations of `E^+` (standard discarded set) in terms of decisive-out under range interpretations. The implementation should carry `(v_E, v^p_E, v^a_E)` as a triple of range interpretations and use Proposition 3.3.13 bullet asymmetries (the C_s(...) = out result holds for v_E and v^p_E but **not** for v^a_E) to gate which checks are valid in which mode.
- **Reduct construction:** Theorem 3.3.2 says pd-acyclic conflict-free in `D` ≡ grounded in `D^E`. This gives a clean "is this set pd-acyclic conflict-free?" implementation via grounded-extension computation on the reduct, which is well-supported in propstore's ATMS layer.
- **Stable extension as model + pd-acyclic-cf:** Theorem 3.3.3 reduces stable-extension check to (a) is `E` a model? (b) is `E` pd-acyclic conflict-free? — both monotone over `E`, both implementable as Z3 queries.
- **EAF reinstatement-set characterization (Thm 2.1.47):** the four-bullet defense-attack sequence condition could ground a propstore utility `is_reinstatable(EF, E, defeat) -> bool` returning True iff no such sequence exists. Important for any EAF-based view layer.
