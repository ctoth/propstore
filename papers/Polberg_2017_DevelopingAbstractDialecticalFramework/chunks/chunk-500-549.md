### Appendix A3 — Framework Normal Forms & Subclasses

#### Theorem 4.2.5 *(p.536)*
For SETAF SF = (A, R), SF^min = (A, R') its minimal form: a set E ⊆ A is a σ-extension of SF (σ ∈ {conflict-free, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable}) iff it is a σ-extension of SF^min. (Proof: minimal form preserves attack relation up to redundancy.)

#### Theorem 4.2.13 *(p.536)*
ADF redundancy-free form preserves all extensions (conflict-free, pd-acyclic conflict-free, model, stable, grounded, acyclic-grounded, xy-admissible/complete/preferred for x,y ∈ {a,c}) and all labelings (3-valued model, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded). Proof sketch: redundant link addition does not alter acceptance condition outcome.

#### Theorem 4.2.15 *(p.536-540)*
ADF cleansed form D^c preserves all extensions and labelings. Long proof developing relations between standard evaluations, blocking sets, and discarded sets across D and D^c. Key relations:
- E^+ = E_c^+ ∪ (A \ A')
- E^{a+} = E_c^{a+} ∪ (A \ A')
- E^{p+} = E_c^{p+} ∪ (A \ A')
Establishes coincidence of cc/ac/aa/ca1/ca2-admissible, complete, preferred, model, stable, grounded, acyclic-grounded extensions between D and D^c.

#### Theorem 4.2.7 *(p.540)*
EAFC = (A, R, D) minimal form EFC^min = (A, R, D) preserves σ-extensions (conflict-free, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable). Proof: defeats and reinstatement sets coincide between EFC and EFC^min.

#### Theorem 4.2.16 *(p.540-543)*
For ADF D and cleansed form D^c: σ-labeling restriction v|_{A'} is a σ-labeling of D^c (σ ∈ {three-valued model, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded}); f-completion of σ-labeling of D^c is σ-labeling of D. Hence bijective correspondence.

#### Theorem 4.2.50 *(p.543-545)*
For AFN FN = (A, R, N) and FN^sc = (A', R, N') its strongly-consistent form via Translation 13: for set E ⊆ A and E^b set of bypass arguments, E σ-extension of FN iff E ∪ E^b σ-extension of FN^sc (σ ∈ {conflict-free, coherent, admissible, complete, grounded, stable}). Key technique: bypass-argument insertion preserves powerful-sequence structure.

#### Theorem 4.2.51 *(p.546)*
Let FN = (A, R, N) be an AFN and FN^sc = (A', R, N') its bypass consistency form (Translation 13). Then FN is weakly / relation-/ strongly-valid iff FN^sc is. FN is in minimal form iff FN^sc is. (Proof: minimality and validity properties carry across translation; bypass arguments supported by single sets only.)

#### Theorem 4.2.54 *(p.546-548)*
Let ES = (A, R, E) be an EAS (evidential argumentation system) and ES^sc = (A', R, E') its strongly consistent form via Translation 14. For S ⊆ A, S' ⊆ A' and bypass set S^b: S is a σ-extension of ES iff S ∪ S^b is a σ-extension of ES^sc, for σ ∈ {conflict-free, self-supporting, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable}. (Long proof: proves correspondence for self-supporting sets, acceptability, then admissibility/complete/preferred/grounded/stable.)

#### Theorem 4.2.56 *(p.548-549)*
For AFN FN = (A, R, N), FN^sc = (A', R', N') its strongly consistent framework via Translation 15: E coherent in FN => E ∪ E^b is pd-acyclic in FN^sc; E' pd-acyclic in FN^sc => E' ∩ A is coherent in FN. E σ-extension of FN iff σ-extension of FN^sc (σ ∈ {conflict-free, strongly coherent, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded}). Every stable extension of FN^sc is stable in FN but not vice versa. Footnote: pure bypass strong consistency form holds direction A => FN^sc; reverse needs subset-of-A condition.

## Design Rationale
- **u-completion** as canonical lifting of admissible extensions to labelings.
- **Weak CA1 fundamental lemma** isolated due to cycles forcing b into acyclic discarded set.
- **ca1/ca2 not forming complete meet-semilattices**: counterexample with self-attacker that grounded but not ca1/ca2 picks up.
- **Preferred labelings only correspond to ca2** because labeling's expressive power matches ca2 range/discarded notions.
- **AADF+ subclass** isolates the well-behaved case where all subsemantics collapse.
- **Cleansed form** justified for normalization without changing semantics — important because heuristic preprocessing is safe.
- **Bypass-argument translation** preserves AFN semantics but yields strongly-consistent framework — making FN^sc the operational form.

## Implementation Notes
- Five-flavored semantics (cc/ac/aa/ca1/ca2) imply propstore should expose these as enumerable variants of admissible/complete/preferred/grounded.
- AADF+ detection is high-leverage: when applicable, all five subsemantics collapse to one engine.
- u-completion is a small operation: extend partial decisive-in v on E by mapping every other argument to u.
- Complexity table is a price list for ADF reasoning service tiers (P / NP / coNP / D^P / Σ_2^P / Π_2^P / Δ_2^P / Δ_3^P / Σ_3^P / Π_3^P / Π_4^P).
- Lemmas 3.7.39 and 3.7.59 are useful test fixtures: they convert propositional formulas / QBFs into AADF+ instances over disjoint variable sets, so QBF-based property tests can drive the implementation.
- Cleansed form (Theorem 4.2.15) is a safe normalization step — every reasoner can run on D^c instead of D without changing answers.
- Redundancy-free form (Theorem 4.2.13) likewise safe.
- AFN to FN^sc translation (Theorem 4.2.50) is the operational pre-processing for AFN solvers.
