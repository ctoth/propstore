## Key Findings

- The chunk is dense correspondence-theorem proof material: it shows that *every* semantics (conflict-free, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable, plus framework-specific c-/d-/i- variants and ca₂-σ variants) is preserved (often as bijection) under each Translation 42–69 in the chapter. *(p.600–649)*
- Translations referenced (numbered translations from the chapter, used as black-box constructions in the proofs): **42, 44, 47, 48, 49, 50, 53, 54, 57, 58, 59, 60, 61, 63, 65, 66, 67, 68, 69**. *(p.600–649)*
- Theorems proved in this range: **4.6.20, 4.6.21, 4.6.28, 4.6.29, 4.6.32, 4.6.36, 4.6.37, 4.6.38, 4.6.39, 4.6.40, 4.6.41, 4.6.42, 4.6.47** (EAFs); **4.7.2, 4.7.3, 4.7.4, 4.7.9, 4.7.10, 4.7.12, 4.7.14, 4.7.15, 4.7.16** (BAFs); **4.8.2, 4.8.4, 4.8.5, 4.8.9, 4.8.11, 4.8.12, 4.8.13, 4.8.15, 4.8.16, 4.8.17, 4.8.18, 4.8.19, 4.8.23, 4.8.24** plus Lemmas 4.6.30, 4.6.31, 4.6.40, 4.6.41 and 4.8.20, 4.8.21, 4.8.22 (AFNs). *(p.600–649)*
- Recurring meta-pattern: the appendix proofs almost universally argue case by case over the semantics list `{conflict-free, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable}`, often constructing a **primed/bypass/auxiliary argument set** (`X'`, `E^b`, `E_np`, `{η}`) that mediates the source-target mapping. *(p.600, 609, 614, 626, 633)*
- Several translations are shown to **lose properties for conflict-free semantics specifically** while preserving every other semantics — Polberg flags this asymmetry repeatedly. *(p.601, 605, 631, 634, 638, 639)*
- "Strongly valid" / "AADF^+" / "redundancy-free" / "cleansed" / "weakly valid" / "relation valid" are systematically tracked through every translation. The pattern: every translated framework lands in **cleansed + weakly valid form**; **AADF^+ and strong validity** are obtained iff the source is **bounded hierarchical** or otherwise constraint-restricted (e.g. minimal, strongly valid). *(p.605, 607, 644, 645)*
- The thesis distinguishes **ca₂-σ-extension** (a parametrized ADF semantics — likely "completion approach 2") from plain σ-extension; EAFCs and BAFs map onto ca₂-σ-extensions of their ADFs rather than plain σ-extensions. *(p.607, 608, 615)*

## Theorems / Propositions / Lemmas

Statements verbatim or near-verbatim. All proofs are present in body (cite "proof in body" rather than appendix since these *are* the appendix).

### Theorem 4.6.20 (continued from before p.600) *(p.600 — proof body, statement begins on previous page)*

> "If E ⊆ A is a σ-extension of bh−EF, where σ ∈ {conflict-free, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable}, then E' = E ∪ {x' | x' ∈ X, x ∈ E^+} is a σ-extension of FN^EF. If E' ⊆ A' is a σ'-extension of FN^EF, where σ' ∈ {admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable}, then E' ∩ A is a σ'-extension of bh−EF. **This does not necessarily hold for conflict-free semantics.**" *(p.600)*

Proof spans p.600–p.604, working through every semantics by cases, using primed-argument construction `E' = E ∪ {x' | x' ∈ X, x ∈ E^+}` and Theorem 2.1.47 reinstatement-set decomposition.

### Theorem 4.6.21 *(p.604; statement begins p.604)*

> "Let bh−EF = (A,R,D) be a bounded hierarchical EAF and FN^EF = (A',R',N') its corresponding AFN obtained through Translation 44. If a set E ⊆ A is a σ-extension of bh−EF, where σ ∈ {conflict-free, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable}, then there is a σ-extension E' ⊆ A' of FN^EF s.t. E' ∩ A = E. If a set E' ⊆ A' is a σ'-extension of FN^EF, where σ' ∈ {admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable}, then E = E' ∩ A is a σ'-extension of bh−EF." *(p.604)*

Proof routes through AFRA `FR = (A, R∪D)` (Translation 42) and Theorems 4.6.15 + 4.5.6, exploiting source-completeness `E' = E' ∪ {src(V) | V ∈ E ∩ (R∪D)}` (Definition 4.5.5). *(p.604–605)*

### Theorem 4.6.28 *(p.605)*

> "Let EF = (A,R,D) be an EAF s.t. it is bounded hierarchical, or (strongly) consistent and without symmetric attacks. Let D^EF = (A,L,C) be its corresponding ADF obtained through Translation 47. **D^EF is a BADF. It is also in redundancy-free, cleansed, and weakly valid form.** It is **not necessarily an AADF^+** and does not have to be in relation or strongly valid form. **If EF is bounded hierarchical, then D^EF is an AADF^+ and is in relation and strongly valid forms.**" *(p.605)*

Proof: links classified as supporting iff defense-attack covers, attacking iff direct attack consistent. Bounded-hierarchical case proceeds by induction over the partition `((A_1,R_1),D_1),…,((A_n,R_n),D_n)` of Definition 2.1.56, building topological order layer by layer. *(p.605–606)*

### Theorem 4.6.29 *(p.606)*

> "Let EF be an EAF s.t. it is bounded hierarchical, or (strongly) consistent and without symmetric attacks. Let D^EF = (A,L,C) be its corresponding ADF obtained through Translation 47. **A set of arguments E ⊆ A is a conflict-free extension of EF iff it is conflict-free in D^EF.**" *(p.606)*

Proof refers forward to Theorem 4.6.39.

### Lemma 4.6.30 *(p.606)*

> "Let EF be an EAF s.t. it is bounded hierarchical, or (strongly) consistent and without symmetric attacks… The discarded set of E in EF coincides with the partially acyclic discarded set of E in D^EF." *(p.606)*

### Lemma 4.6.31 *(p.606)*

> "A conflict-free set of arguments E defends an argument a ∈ A in EF iff a is decisively in w.r.t. the **partially acyclic range v_E^p** of E in D^EF." *(p.606)*

### Theorem 4.6.32 *(p.607)*

> "A set of arguments E ⊆ A is a conflict-free extension of EF iff it is conflict-free in D^EF. E is a stable extension of EF iff it is a model of D^EF. E is a grounded extension of EF iff it is the **acyclic grounded** extension of D^EF. E is a σ-extension of EF, where σ ∈ {admissible, complete, preferred}, iff it is a **ca₂-σ-extension** of D^EF." *(p.607)*

### Theorem 4.6.36 *(p.607)*

EAF (bounded hierarchical or no symmetric attacks) → ADF via Translation 48: D^EF is BADF, redundancy-free, cleansed, weakly valid; not necessarily AADF^+ unless bounded hierarchical. Uses **bypass arguments** `a^b` to avoid losing supporting/attacking link distinction when an argument's attack on `b` is itself defense-attacked. *(p.607–608)*

### Theorem 4.6.37 *(p.608)*

The bridging theorem for Translation 48: with `E^b` denoting the (possibly empty) bypass-argument extension generated by `E`, every semantics (conflict-free, stable, grounded as acyclic grounded, σ ∈ {admissible, complete, preferred} as ca₂-σ) maps bijectively (modulo `E^b`) between EF and D^EF. *(p.608)*

### Theorem 4.6.38 *(p.608)*

EAFC → ADF via Translation 49: `D^EFC = (A,C)` is BADF, cleansed, weakly valid; not necessarily AADF^+ in general; AADF^+ + redundancy-free if EFC is minimal + bounded hierarchical; further relation/strongly valid via 4.2.41/43. EAFCs (unlike AFs/SETAFs) can carry **t-mappings** in their minimal interpretations. *(p.608–610)*

### Theorem 4.6.39 *(p.609)*

> "A set of arguments E ⊆ A is a conflict-free extension of EFC iff it is a conflict-free extension of D^EFC." *(p.609)*

Direct proof using `C_a(E ∩ par(a)) = in` ↔ either no R-attacker or every attack is defense-attacked by E.

### Lemma 4.6.40 *(p.609)*

> "Let E ⊆ A be a conflict-free extension of EFC (and thus of D^EFC). The discarded set of E in EFC coincides with the **partially acyclic discarded set** of E in D^EFC." *(p.609; proof spans p.609–611)*

Proof builds `E^+ = {b ∈ A | argument a ∈ E defeats_E b with reinstatement set on E}` and shows `E^+ = E^{p+}`.

### Lemma 4.6.41 *(p.611)*

> "A conflict-free set of arguments E ⊆ A defends an argument a ∈ A in EFC iff a is decisively in w.r.t. the partially acyclic range v_E^p of E in D^EFC." *(p.611)*

Proof exploits `att_a^b = ¬b ∨ (⋀B_1 ∨ … ∨ ⋀B_m)` subformula structure: `att_a^b` true under range iff either b assigned **f** (so b ∈ E^+) or some B_i fully assigned **t**. *(p.612)*

### Theorem 4.6.42 *(p.612)*

> "E is a conflict-free extension of EFC iff it is conflict-free in D^EFC. E is stable iff it is a model. E is grounded iff it is **acyclic grounded**. E is a σ-extension where σ ∈ {admissible, complete, preferred} iff it is a **ca₂-σ-extension**." *(p.612)*

Grounded proof uses Definition 3.3.8 iteration-from-empty-set approach, citing [MP10] and Lemmas 3.1.21 + 3.3.18.

### Theorem 4.6.47 *(p.613)*

EAFC → ADF via Translation 50: `D^EFC` BADF, cleansed, weakly valid. Bridging with bypasses `E^b`: maps stable↔model, grounded↔acyclic grounded, complete/preferred↔ca₂-complete/preferred. **Admissible is *not* in 1-1 correspondence** because not every admissible needs to carry its bypasses. *(p.613–615)*

> "This is not necessarily the case for the admissible extensions — not always a bypass has to and needs to be included." *(p.615)*

### Theorem 4.7.2 *(p.618)*

Deductive BAF, R' = {R^sup, R^med_{R^sup}} = supported + super-mediated attacks; `apd-F^BF = (A, R ∪ ⋃R')` via Translation 53. **If E ⊆ A is a complete extension of apd-F^BF, then E is closed under S in BF.** *(p.618)*

### Theorem 4.7.3 *(p.618)*

BAF, R' = {R^sec} secondary attacks; `apd-F^BF` via Translation 53. **If E ⊆ A is complete in apd-F^BF, then E is inverse closed under S in BF.** *(p.618)*

### Theorem 4.7.4 *(p.618)*

BAF, R' ⊆ R^ind indirect attacks; `iclo-F^BF` via Translation 54. **A set E ⊆ A is i-admissible (i-preferred) in BF w.r.t. (R',R') iff it is admissible (preferred) in iclo-F^BF.** *(p.618; proof p.619)*

### Theorem 4.7.9 *(p.619)*

Deductive BAF: σ-extension correspondence (`{C(a_1),…,C(a_n)}` ↔ E) between `apd-F^BF` and coalition AF `cd-F^BF` (Translations 53 and 57) for σ ∈ {complete, preferred, grounded, stable}. Establishes one-to-one bijection between complete extensions. *(p.619–621)*

### Theorem 4.7.10 *(p.621; proof p.621–622)*

Eight-clause biconditional listing the precise correspondence between BF semantics (+conflict-free w.r.t. R' and closed-under-S; c-admissible/c-preferred; d-complete; d-grounded/stable) and `cd-F^BF` semantics. The list is exhaustive and is the master statement for coalition translation. *(p.621)*

### Theorem 4.7.12 *(p.622; proof p.622–623)*

BAF specialized for **necessary support**, R' = {R^sec, R^ext} secondary + extended attacks; `dn-F^BF = (A',R'')` defender AF via Translation 58. **If E ⊆ A is i-admissible in BF w.r.t. (R',R'), then there exists an admissible E' ⊆ A' s.t. E' ∩ A = E. Reverse direction is asymmetric: an admissible of `df-F^BF` is not guaranteed to map to an i-admissible of BF.** Polberg points to Example 121 on p.324 of the thesis as the counter-example. *(p.623)*

### Theorem 4.7.14 *(p.623)*

Support-acyclic BAF → AFN via Translation 59. Eight-clause correspondence list:
- +conflict-free w.r.t. ∅ ↔ conflict-free in `FN^BF`
- inverse closed ↔ coherent
- +conflict-free w.r.t. R' ∧ inverse closed ↔ strongly coherent
- i-admissible/i-preferred ↔ admissible/preferred
- d-complete ↔ complete
- d-grounded ↔ grounded
- stable ↔ stable
*(p.623–625)*

### Theorem 4.7.15 *(p.625)*

Support-acyclic BAF → EAS via Translation 60: `ES^BF = (A ∪ {η}, R', E)` is **attack binary, support singular, all-supported, in minimal, weakly, relation, strongly valid forms.** *(p.625)*

### Theorem 4.7.16 *(p.626)*

Big bridging theorem: support-acyclic BAF ↔ EAS via Translation 60 (with secondary attacks R'' = {R^sec}). Twelve-clause biconditional (eight for X ⊆ A, four for X' ⊆ A ∪ {η}) covering +conflict-free, inverse closed, strongly coherent, i-admissible, i-preferred, d-complete, d-grounded, stable on the BAF side and conflict-free, self-supporting, strongly self-supporting, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable on the EAS side. *(p.626; proof p.626–628)*

### Theorem 4.8.2 *(p.629)*

AFN → AF via Translation 61: `F^FN = (A',R')`. **If E' ⊆ A' is conflict-free in F^FN, then ⋃E' is conflict-free in FN, but not vice versa.** Strongly coherent E ⊆ A iff F^FN admits E' = {C_1,…,C_n} ⊆ A' s.t. E = ⋃C_i conflict-free. Admissible E iff F^FN admits such E' admissible. **For every complete extension of FN there is exactly one complete extension of F^FN.** *(p.629–630)*

### Theorem 4.8.4 *(p.630)*

> "Let FN = (A,R,N) be an AFN and E, E' ⊆ A two admissible extensions of FN. If for every a ∈ E, b ∈ E' it is not the case that (a,b) ∈ R and (b,a) ∈ R, then E ∪ E' is also admissible." *(p.630)*

### Theorem 4.8.5 *(p.630)*

> "For any two admissible extensions E_1 and E_2 of FN, if for every argument a ∈ E_1, b ∈ E_2 there exists an admissible extension E_3 of FN s.t. a, b ∈ E_3, then E_1 ∪ E_2 is an admissible extension of FN." *(p.630)*

### Theorem 4.8.9 *(p.630)*

AFN → SETAF via Translation 63 (attack-propagated SETAF `SF^FN = (A',R'')`). Strongly coherent in FN ⇒ conflict-free in SF^FN (not vice versa). σ ∈ {admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable}: σ-extension of FN ↔ σ-extension of SF^FN. σ' ∈ {conflict-free, preferred, complete, grounded, stable}: σ'-extension of SF^FN → σ'-extension of FN, **but not necessarily for admissible** (counter-example: AFN `({a,b},∅,{({a},b)})` has SETAF `({a,b},∅)` where `{b}` is admissible in SETAF but not even coherent in AFN). *(p.630–632)*

### Theorem 4.8.11 *(p.633)*

AFN → defender SETAF via Translation 65, with `E_np = {a' | no coherent set containing a} ∪ {a' | for every coherent set C for a, ∃e ∈ E, c ∈ C\{a}, (e,c) ∈ R}` (primed-argument set). σ-extension correspondence E ↔ E ∪ E_np for σ ∈ {admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable}. **Strongly coherent in FN ⇒ conflict-free in SF^FN, but the inverse direction (E' conflict-free in SF^FN → E' ∩ A strongly coherent in FN) does not hold.** *(p.633–635)*

### Theorem 4.8.12 *(p.636)*

Strongly valid AFN → defender SETAF via Translation 66, with `E_np = {a' | for every coherent set C for a, ∃e ∈ E, c ∈ C\{a}, (e,c) ∈ R}`. Same eight-clause biconditional as 4.8.11 but with strong-validity on the AFN side. Conflict-free in SF^FN → conflict-free in FN, but **not necessarily strongly coherent**. *(p.636–640)*

### Theorem 4.8.13 *(p.641)*

> "Let FN = (A,R,N) be a strongly valid and support binary AFN and BF^FN = (A,R,S) its associated BAF obtained through Translation 67. Then BF^FN is **support acyclic**." *(p.641)*

Proof: `suf(a)` is exactly one set = `sup(a)`, so there is one support assignment, and strong validity of FN gives a powerful sequence which doubles as a topological order on `(A, N')`. *(p.641)*

### Theorem 4.8.15 *(p.641)*

AFN → EAS via Translation 68: `ES^FN = (A',R',E)` is **attack binary**. AFN weakly/relation/strongly valid ⇒ EAS same. AFN strongly consistent ⇒ EAS same. ES^FN need not be in minimal form even if FN is (Polberg shows a counter-example AFN `({a,b,c,d},∅,{({a,b},d),({a,c},d)})`). *(p.641–642)*

### Theorem 4.8.16 *(p.642)*

Powerful sequence ↔ evidential sequence correspondence: `(a_0,…,a_n)` powerful for a on S in FN ↔ `(η, a_0,…,a_n)` evidential for a on S ∪ {η} in ES^FN. **S coherent in FN ⇔ S ∪ {η} self-supporting in ES^FN.** *(p.642)*

### Theorem 4.8.17 *(p.642)*

> "If a ∈ A is defended by a coherent set S ⊆ A in FN, then it is acceptable w.r.t. S ∪ {η} in ES^FN. If a ∈ A is acceptable w.r.t. a self-supporting set S' ⊆ A' in ES^FN, then it is defended by S' ∩ A in FN." *(p.642–643)*

### Theorem 4.8.18 *(p.643)*

Bridging theorem for AFN ↔ EAS via Translation 68. σ ∈ {conflict-free, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable}: S σ-extension in FN ⇔ S ∪ {η} σ-extension in ES^FN; reverse direction via S' ∩ A. **Empty set and {η} are both trivially conflict-free / strongly self-supporting / admissible in ES^FN even though they correspond to ∅ in FN.** *(p.643–644)*

### Theorem 4.8.19 *(p.644)*

Strongly consistent AFN → ADF via Translation 69: `D^FN = (A,L,C)` is BADF, cleansed. FN minimal ⇒ D^FN redundancy-free. FN weakly/relation/strongly valid ⇒ D^FN same. **FN strongly valid ⇒ D^FN is AADF^+. FN minimal + strongly valid ⇒ D^FN strongly valid.** *(p.644–646)*

### Lemma 4.8.20 *(p.646)*

> "For a given powerful sequence for an argument e ∈ E we can construct a corresponding **acyclic pd-evaluation** and vice versa. E is **coherent in FN iff it is pd-acyclic in D^FN**." *(p.646–647)*

Construction: pd-evaluation `((a_0,…,a_n), ⋃_{0}^{n}{a_i}^-)` from powerful sequence; reverse via Definition 4.2.30 support-assignment.

### Lemma 4.8.21 *(p.647)*

> "E ⊆ A is **strongly coherent in FN iff it is a pd-acyclic conflict-free extension of D^FN**." *(p.647)*

### Lemma 4.8.22 *(p.647)*

> "Let E ⊆ A be strongly coherent in FN and thus pd-acyclic conflict-free in D^FN. Then **E^att coincides with the acyclic discarded set of E** (i.e. E^att = E^{a+})." *(p.647–648)*

### Theorem 4.8.23 *(p.648)*

> "E defends an argument a ∈ A in FN iff this argument is **decisively in w.r.t. v_E^a** in D^FN." *(p.648)*

(Here `v_E^a` is the **acyclic range** interpretation, distinct from the partially acyclic range `v_E^p` used for EAF/EAFC.)

### Theorem 4.8.24 *(p.649)*

Master AFN ↔ ADF correspondence (Translation 69 / strongly consistent AFN):
- E coherent in FN ↔ pd-acyclic in D^FN
- E strongly coherent ↔ pd-acyclic conflict-free
- E σ-extension where σ ∈ {admissible, complete, preferred} ↔ **aa-σ-extension** of D^FN
- E stable in FN ↔ stable in D^FN (= model)
- E grounded in FN ↔ acyclic grounded in D^FN.
*(p.649)*

(So the AFN-to-ADF translation lands on **aa-σ** semantics, *not* the **ca₂-σ** used by EAFC. This is the cleanest single-line architectural distinction in the chunk.)

## Algorithms / Procedures

No numbered algorithms. The proofs implicitly describe constructive procedures:

### Procedure — Construct discarded set `E^+` from E (used in Theorem 4.6.20, 4.6.40 proofs)
1. For each `b ∈ A`, check whether `∃ a ∈ E` with `a defeats_E b`.
2. If yes, check whether the defeat has a reinstatement set on E.
3. If yes, add `b` to `E^+`.
4. By conflict-freeness, `E ∩ E^+ = ∅`.
*(p.609)*

### Procedure — Bypass-argument generation `E^b` from E (Theorem 4.6.36/47 proofs)
1. For each attacker `a` of an argument `b` such that `a` is defense-attacked (i.e., not the *only* attacker of `b`),
2. Introduce `a^b` with condition `C_{a^b} = a`.
3. Set `(a^b, b)` as the new attack link, replacing `(a,b)` when needed.
*(p.607)*

### Procedure — pd-acyclic evaluation from powerful sequence (Lemma 4.8.20)
1. Given powerful sequence `(a_0, …, a_n)` for `e` on `E`.
2. Set `a_0`'s minimal interpretation: `t`-part empty, `f`-part = attackers of `a_0`.
3. For each `a_i` (i ≥ 1): t-part ⊆ `{a_0,…,a_{i-1}}`, f-part = `{b ∈ A | bRa_i}`.
4. Resulting blocking set = `⋃_{0}^{n}{a_i}^-` (union of attackers).
5. Reverse direction: extract powerful sequence from any acyclic pd-evaluation by reading the topological order off the t-mappings.
*(p.646–647)*

## Semantics Defined / Compared

This range *applies* the semantics rather than introducing them, but reveals the precise correspondence picture:

- **AFN semantics** (Definition 2.2.22 etc.): conflict-free, coherent (= self-supporting), strongly coherent, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable.
- **EAF semantics**: conflict-free, admissible (with reinstatement-set defense), complete, preferred, grounded, stable.
- **EAFC semantics**: same as EAF but with strong/regular consistency restricting defeat structure.
- **BAF semantics** (Polberg's parametrized): +conflict-free w.r.t. R', closed-under-S, inverse-closed-under-S, c-admissible, c-preferred, i-admissible, i-preferred, d-complete, d-grounded, stable.
- **SETAF semantics**: conflict-free, admissible, complete, preferred, grounded, stable.
- **EAS semantics**: conflict-free, self-supporting, strongly self-supporting, admissible, preferred, complete, grounded, stable.
- **ADF semantics** (Polberg's parametrization, used in this chunk): conflict-free, model, ca₂-admissible/complete/preferred, aa-admissible/complete/preferred, acyclic grounded, stable. *(p.607, 608, 612, 615, 649)*

## Comparisons With Prior Work

This range is essentially internal cross-translation comparison rather than comparison with external work. The theorems quietly compare:

- **EAF ↔ AFN** (via AFRA): builds on Modgil's EAF semantics (citing Theorem 4.6.15) and shows AFNs (Nouioua) can absorb defeasible/extended attacks via primed reinstatement-set arguments. *(p.600–605)*
- **EAFC**: Polberg's own EAFC variant subsumes Modgil EAF + consistency; she shows the ADF translation requires a different (ca₂-) ADF semantics to align grounded properly. *(p.608–615)*
- **BAFs (Cayrol/Lagasquie-Schiex deductive; Nouioua-Risch necessary)**: Polberg parametrizes BAF semantics by attack collection R' to unify deductive (R^sup, R^med) and necessary (R^sec, R^ext) treatments under one translation framework. *(p.618–626)*
- **AFN ↔ SETAF (Nielsen-Parsons collective attack)**: Theorem 4.8.9 gives the attack-propagation embedding; ditto defender form 4.8.11. The example `({a,b}, ∅, {({a},b)})` is reused as a recurring witness that **support-only AFNs cannot be encoded as SETAFs without primed defender machinery**. *(p.631, 638)*
- **AFN ↔ EAS (Oren-Norman)**: Theorem 4.8.16 establishes that AFN powerful sequences and EAS evidential sequences are essentially the same combinatorial object up to the η sentinel — a structural unification, not a mere semantic preservation. *(p.642)*
- **ADF (Brewka-Woltran)**: Polberg's translations confirm ADFs are universal but with the caveat that **EAF-style frameworks need ca₂-σ semantics, AFN-style need aa-σ semantics**. The mismatch is highlighted but not resolved into a single ADF semantics. *(p.607, 615, 649)*

## Design Rationale

- **Why partially acyclic vs fully acyclic range?** EAF/EAFC: defense-attacks introduce structural cycles into the support tree, so only *partial* acyclicity is achievable. AFN: support is always tree-like, so full acyclicity holds. This drives the v_E^p vs v_E^a split. *(p.606 vs 648)*
- **Why bypass arguments `a^b` instead of just rewriting the condition `C_b`?** Polberg writes the EAF→ADF translation in two flavours (47 and 48). Translation 48 introduces bypasses precisely because *defense-attacked attackers* otherwise lose their identity in the resulting acceptance condition, breaking the supporting/attacking link distinction. Bypasses preserve that distinction at the cost of a larger argument set. *(p.607)*
- **Why ca₂-σ for EAFC, aa-σ for AFN?** Different framework cycle structures pick out different ADF semantics. EAFC defense-attack loops require completion-2; AFN strict support-tree allows the simpler aa- semantics. *(p.607, 615, 649)*
- **Why the η sentinel in EAS?** Without it, "no support" arguments in AFN have no evidential anchor in the EAS support relation. η is the universal supporter that satisfies the e-support requirement vacuously. *(p.625, 643, 644)*
- **Why parametrize BAF semantics by R'?** Different BAF subclasses (deductive, necessary, support-acyclic, etc.) treat different combinations of indirect attacks (supported, secondary, mediated, super-mediated, extended) as primary. Parametrizing R' lets a single translation theorem cover all subclasses. *(p.618, 619)*
- **Why does the admissible direction of `dn-F^BF → BAF` fail (Theorem 4.7.12, only one direction)?** Polberg's Example 121 shows that the defender AF allows extensions that cannot be reflected back through inverse closure — accepted with explicit acknowledgment rather than a workaround. *(p.623)*
- **Why no minimal-form preservation in AFN→EAS (Theorem 4.8.15)?** The translation introduces redundant subset support edges when the AFN support relation is non-binary. Polberg explicitly demonstrates with `({a,b,c,d},∅,{({a,b},d),({a,c},d)})` and accepts this loss rather than complicating Translation 68. *(p.641–642)*

## Limitations Acknowledged

- **Conflict-free correspondence repeatedly fails one direction.** Polberg flags this in every translation theorem touching it: Theorem 4.6.20 (EAF→FN^EF), Theorem 4.7.14 implicitly, Theorem 4.8.2/4.8.9/4.8.11/4.8.12/4.8.18. *(p.600, 631, 633, 638, 643)*
- **AADF^+ is not always achievable.** EAF/EAFC translations are AADF^+ only if the source is bounded hierarchical. *(p.605, 608, 612)*
- **Admissible bijection breaks for EAFC↔ADF (T.50).** Bypass arguments don't all need to be added for admissibility, so the map is not 1-1 on admissible extensions even though it is on complete/preferred. *(p.615)*
- **Defender SETAF fails one admissible direction (T.4.7.12).** Counter-example given. *(p.623)*
- **Inverse direction of strongly-coherent ↔ conflict-free in defender SETAF fails (4.8.11/12).** *(p.635, 640)*
- **AFN→EAS does not preserve minimal form (4.8.15).** *(p.641–642)*
- **AFN→ADF requires strong consistency.** Without it, attackers can also be supporters, breaking link classification. *(p.644)*
- **AFN→BAF requires strongly valid + support binary (4.8.13).** *(p.641)*
- Theorems are stated for **finite** frameworks; the grounded-iteration proof (4.6.42) explicitly relies on finiteness ("as we are working with finite frameworks, this is warrantied"). *(p.612, 615)*

## Implementation Notes

For propstore's argumentation layer (`propstore.aspic_bridge`, the future ADF layer):

1. **The 1-to-1 vs many-to-1 mapping shape matters at the SQL level.** When sidecar-storing translations between EAF/AFN/BAF/ADF, conflict-free correspondences must be marked **directional** (source→target only or both), since most of the conflict-free maps in this chunk are unidirectional. A `bidirectional_for_semantics: ['admissible', 'complete', 'preferred', 'grounded', 'stable']` field is the right shape.
2. **Two ADF semantics families** (ca₂-σ and aa-σ) are both required if propstore wants to subsume EAFC and AFN under one ADF backbone. They are not interchangeable. Implementing both `ca2_admissible(adf, E)` and `aa_admissible(adf, E)` is mandatory; reusing one for the other will silently give wrong answers for half the input frameworks. *(p.607, 615, 649)*
3. **Bypass arguments** (`a^b`) and **primed defender arguments** (`a'`, `E_np`) are emergent during translation. If propstore stores translated frameworks, it must store these primed sets as a sidecar artifact attached to the translation, not as first-class concepts in the source repository. They are translation byproducts, not authored arguments — exactly the kind of asset that should live in the rebuildable sidecar.
4. **Powerful sequence ↔ pd-evaluation correspondence (Lemma 4.8.20)** gives a direct algorithm to convert AFN proofs into ADF proofs and vice versa. This is implementable as a single function `powerful_sequence_to_pd_eval(sequence, framework)` returning the pd-evaluation, plus reverse.
5. **`E^+` (discarded set)** is the AFN/EAF-side concept that maps to ADF's **partially acyclic discarded set** `E^{p+}` for EAFC and **acyclic discarded set** `E^{a+}` for AFN. Three distinct discarded-set computations need to coexist — picking the right one per framework type is a translation-time decision.
6. **The η sentinel argument** is a translation-time injected argument with no semantic content in the source framework. propstore should mark it with a `is_translation_sentinel: true` flag and exclude it from user-facing extension reports. It must, however, persist through serialization or the translation breaks.
7. **The "if EFC is minimal" and "if FN is minimal" prerequisites** for redundancy-free mapping translate to a *normalization pass* before translation. propstore could expose `framework.normalize()` returning a minimal-form framework as a precondition for AADF^+ guarantees.
8. **Example 121, 113, 109 are all referenced as canonical counter-examples** for limitations. If propstore implements a literature-grounded test suite, these three examples (currently referenced by printed-page in the thesis) should become regression tests for the ADF/AFN/BAF translation modules.
9. **The repeated structure of the AFN-side "primed argument" `a'`** makes it a natural candidate for a single Python class `PrimedArgument(source: Argument, role: Literal['bypass', 'defender', 'reinstatement'])`. Three roles cover all three translations in this chunk.
