## Key Citations for Follow-up

These five citations are the most directly relevant to propstore's argumentation/reasoning layer (Dung AF, ASPIC+, ATMS, context system, AGM revision). They are not yet processed in the collection (cross-check via reconcile after this is committed):

- **[BW10]** Gerhard Brewka and Stefan Woltran. *Abstract dialectical frameworks*. KR 2010. The seminal ADF paper. propstore's Dung AF construction layer should be aware of ADFs as a strict generalization — ADFs subsume Dung AFs and offer richer acceptance conditions per node, which directly informs how the ATMS bundle nodes can carry per-context acceptance functions rather than fixed attack/support edges.

- **[BES+13]** Gerhard Brewka, Stefan Ellmauthaler, Hannes Strass, Johannes Peter Wallner, and Stefan Woltran. *Abstract dialectical frameworks revisited*. IJCAI 2013. The cleaned-up ADF semantics that this thesis builds on. Required reading for any propstore work that wants to lift the AF construction layer to ADF-style acceptance conditions.

- **[MP13]** Sanjay Modgil and Henry Prakken. *A general account of argumentation with preferences*. Artificial Intelligence, 195:361–397, 2013. The canonical ASPIC+ with preferences paper. propstore's `aspic_bridge` translates rule priorities and stances; this is the reference for whether the bridge's preference handling is faithful to ASPIC+.

- **[Pra14]** Henry Prakken. *On support relations in abstract argumentation as abstractions of inferential relations*. ECAI 2014. propstore models support and attack distinctly through stances; Prakken's analysis of when bipolar/support frameworks abstract genuine inferential relations is directly relevant to whether stance-typed edges in the source layer can be cleanly mapped to ASPIC+ inference rules.

- **[Dun95]** Phan Minh Dung. *On the acceptability of arguments and its fundamental role in nonmonotonic reasoning, logic programming and n-person games*. Artificial Intelligence, 77:321–357, 1995. The foundational Dung AF paper. Already known to propstore conceptually, but the exact 1995 statements of admissibility, completeness, preferred, grounded, and stable extensions are the spec the AF construction layer must satisfy.
