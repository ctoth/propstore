## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[6] Dung 1995** — Foundational AF semantics paper; already in propstore's literature. Attack relations and extension computation.
- **[17, 19, 25] Modgil & Prakken 2013/2014/2021** — The ASPIC+ framework papers; the specific solver used in this work. Critical for understanding the symbolic backend.
- **[15] Kazemi et al. 2023 (BoardGameQA)** — The primary benchmark dataset; understanding its structure helps understand the evaluation context and limitations.
- **[12] Khatun & Hossain 2025 (ArgLLMs)** — Alternative approach integrating abstract argumentation directly into LLM architecture rather than as external solver. Worth comparing architectural patterns.
- **[21] Pan et al. 2023 (Logic-LM)** — Prior art on LLM + symbolic solver combination for classical logic. Shows what works for monotonic reasoning but not defeasible.
