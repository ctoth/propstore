## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[9] Charwat et al. 2015** — Comprehensive survey of methods for solving reasoning problems in abstract argumentation. Essential context for understanding the landscape of solvers (SAT, ASP, CSP-based).
- **[24] Dvořák, Pichler, Woltran 2012** — Fixed-parameter tractable algorithms for abstract argumentation. Predecessor work on structural decomposition for argumentation.
- **[26] Fichte, Hecher, Mahmood, Meier 2021** — Disposition-guided reductions. Direct predecessor to this paper, introduces the DDG reduction concept.
- **[38] Niskanen & Järvisalo 2020** — µ-toksia: Efficient abstract argumentation reasoner. ICCMA competition winner, practical SAT-based solver.
- **[34] Järvisalo et al. 2025** — ICCMA 2023 competition results. Latest snapshot of state-of-the-art argumentation solvers.
