Proposes rich preference-based argumentation frameworks (rich PAFs) that correctly integrate preference orderings into Dung-style argumentation by inverting defeated attacks rather than removing them, proving that existing PAF approaches violate conflict-freeness when attacks are asymmetric. Establishes one-to-one correspondence between stable extensions of the rich PAF and preferred sub-theories of stratified knowledge bases, with the democratic preference lifting shown to be strictly finer than the elitist lifting. Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer as the formal bridge between IC merging preference outputs and ASPIC+ preference-based defeat computation.
