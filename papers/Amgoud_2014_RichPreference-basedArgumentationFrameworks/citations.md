## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Modgil & Prakken 2013 [19]:** The main ASPIC+ paper with preferences. Critical to compare their preference handling (last-link/weakest-link at rule level) against this paper's attack-inversion approach. Already in propstore collection.
- **Benferhat, Dubois & Prade 1993 [4]:** Preferred sub-theories -- the belief revision framework that corresponds one-to-one with rich PAF stable extensions. Essential for understanding the IC merging connection.
- **Bench-Capon 2003 [6]:** Value-based argumentation -- an alternative preference framework criticized here. Worth understanding the differences for propstore's preference architecture.
- **Brewka 1989 [38]:** Original preferred subtheories paper. Foundational for the correspondence theorems.
- **Amgoud & Vesic 2011 [25]:** The earlier Ann. Math. Artif. Intell. version with additional material on the two roles of preferences.
