Defines the FAIR Guiding Principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) as 15 measurable sub-principles for scientific data management, emphasizing machine-actionability as equally important as human usability. The principles are technology-agnostic and domain-independent, applicable to data, metadata, algorithms, tools, and workflows, with exemplar implementations across major scientific repositories. Relevant to propstore's provenance tracking, persistent identifier design, qualified cross-references between claims, and the core principle of preserving data richness rather than collapsing it prematurely.
