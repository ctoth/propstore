# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

Argumentation is inherently pervaded by uncertainty, which can arise as a result of the context in which argumentation is used, the kinds of arguments that are involved in a given situation, the types of arguments that are used, and more. One of the prominent approaches for handling uncertainty in argumentation is probabilistic argumentation, which offers means of quantifying the level of uncertainty we are dealing with. This chapter offers an overview of the state-of-the-art research in this area.

---

## Our Interpretation

This survey chapter taxonomizes probabilistic argumentation into two main families: the epistemic approach (fixed graph topology, uncertain beliefs about arguments) and the constellation approach (uncertain graph topology via subgraph distributions). It provides formal definitions for acceptance probability, extension probability, and labelling probability under each approach, along with rationality postulates (COH, RAT, FOU, TRU, OPT) that constrain epistemic probability functions. Directly relevant to propstore's PrAF implementation and the credulous/skeptical/extension probability taxonomy needed for the argumentation layer.
