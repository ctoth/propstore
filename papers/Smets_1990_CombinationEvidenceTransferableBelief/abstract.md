# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

Description of the transferable belief model to quantify degrees of belief, based on belief functions. The impact of open- and closed-world assumption on conditioning. Presentation of a set of axioms justifying Dempster's rule for the combination of belief functions induced by 2 distinct evidences.

KEYWORDS: Belief function, plausibility function, degree of belief, combination of evidences, Dempster's rule of combination, open-world assumption, transferable belief model.

---

## Our Interpretation

Smets builds the transferable belief model from masses-first, allows positive mass on the contradiction 0_Ω to represent open-world frame inadequacy, and derives Dempster's rule of combination axiomatically (eight postulates A1–A8) rather than postulating it. The key result is that q_12(A)=q_1(A)·q_2(A) is uniquely determined by the axioms, with autofunctionality (A6) — not the conditioning axiom — carrying the proof; Shafer's renormalization is then a derived consequence of a single closed-world conditioning axiom (A9), and Zadeh's counter-example is reinterpreted as a closed-world artifact that the open-world TBM correctly diagnoses via large m_12(0_Ω). For propstore this paper is foundational: vacuous belief is the formal target of the project's "honest ignorance over fabricated confidence" rule, and m(0_Ω) is the right primitive for surfacing frame conflict at the render layer instead of collapsing it in storage.
