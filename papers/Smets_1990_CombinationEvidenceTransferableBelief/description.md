Smets axiomatizes Dempster's rule of combination from eight postulates over an open-world transferable belief model where mass on the contradiction 0_Ω signals frame inadequacy rather than being normalized away.
The paper proves q_12(A)=q_1(A)·q_2(A) is uniquely determined by the axioms, identifies autofunctionality (A6) as the load-bearing postulate rather than the conditioning axiom (A4), and shows Shafer's renormalization is equivalent to a single closed-world conditioning axiom (A9), recasting Zadeh's counter-example as evidence about frame inadequacy rather than a defect of the rule.
This grounds propstore's "honest ignorance over fabricated confidence" principle in formal vacuous belief, gives the open-world m(0_Ω) primitive needed for ATMS-level conflict signaling, and clarifies how closed-world normalization should sit at the render layer instead of the storage layer.
