## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[25] Shafer, *A Mathematical Theory of Evidence* (1976)** — already in propstore collection. Foundational; this paper builds on and corrects Shafer's closed-world default. Required for any work on TBM.
- **[41] Smets, "Constructing the pignistic probability function" (UAI 1989)** — the bridge from TBM to decision theory. Needed to connect propstore's belief-level storage to probabilistic render-time decisions.
- **[37] Smets, "Combining non-distinct pieces of evidence" (NAFIPS 1986)** — operational definition of distinctness behind axiom A1; needed before applying Dempster's rule in propstore's stance machinery.
- **[24] Schweizer & Sklar, *Probabilistic Metric Spaces* (1983)** — T-norm theory underpinning Theorem 3 and the consonant-belief-function characterization.
- **[47] Zadeh, "A mathematical theory of evidence" book review (1984)** — source of the counter-example resolved in §6; useful background on the historical critique of Dempster-Shafer.
