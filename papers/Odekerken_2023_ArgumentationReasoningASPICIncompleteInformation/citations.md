## Key Citations for Follow-up

### Already in Collection
- **Dung 1995** (ref 11): Foundational abstract argumentation framework -- this paper builds directly on Dung's grounded semantics
- **Modgil and Prakken 2013** (ref 20): General account of argumentation with preferences -- the preference-based defeat mechanism used here
- **Modgil and Prakken 2014** (ref 21): ASPIC+ tutorial -- the base framework this paper extends

### High Priority for Collection
- **Odekerken, Borg, and Bex 2022** (ref 24): "Stability and relevance in incomplete argumentation frameworks" -- companion paper doing similar analysis at the abstract (Dung) level rather than structured (ASPIC+) level. Essential for understanding the full picture.
- **Lehtonen, Wallner, and Jarvisalo 2020** (ref 15): "An ASP approach to argumentative reasoning in ASPIC+" -- the base ASP encoding that this paper extends. Predecessor work.
- **Rapberger and Ulbricht 2022** (ref 28): "On dynamics in structured argumentation" -- addresses how structured argumentation frameworks change over time, complementary to stability/relevance.
- **Baroni, Caminada, and Giacomin 2011** (ref 4): Handbook chapter on argumentation semantics -- comprehensive reference for the semantics taxonomy used here.
- **Niskanen and Jarvisalo 2020** (ref 22): Incremental SAT-based algorithms for dynamic argumentation -- relevant for incremental computation as the propstore grows.

### Lower Priority but Relevant
- **Odekerken, Borg, and Bex 2020** (ref 23): Stability estimation for argument-based inquiry -- predecessor with approximation methods
- **Testerink, Odekerken, and Bex 2019** (ref 30): Efficient argument-based inquiry -- methods for guiding evidence gathering
- **Clarke, Gupta, and Strichman 2004** (ref 8): CEGAR for SAT -- the abstraction refinement technique adapted for the relevance algorithm
