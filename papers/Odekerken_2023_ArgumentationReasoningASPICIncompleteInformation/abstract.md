## Our Interpretation

This paper tackles a fundamental gap in structured argumentation: what happens when you don't have all the facts yet? In ASPIC+, arguments are built from knowledge bases using strict and defeasible rules, but standard treatments assume the knowledge base is complete. The authors define four justification statuses for literals under incomplete information (unsatisfiable, defended, out, blocked) and formalize two key decision problems: *stability* (will a literal's status survive any future evidence?) and *relevance* (which specific unresolved questions could change a literal's status?). They prove tight complexity bounds (coNP-complete and Sigma_2^P-complete respectively) and deliver working ASP-based algorithms that handle real-world criminal investigation scenarios. For the propstore, this provides the missing computational layer for determining which claims are robustly justified and which gaps in the literature would actually matter if filled.
