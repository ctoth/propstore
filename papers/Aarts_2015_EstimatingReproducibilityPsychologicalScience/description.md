Presents the first large-scale systematic replication of 100 psychological studies from three major journals, finding that only 36% of replications achieved statistical significance and replication effect sizes averaged roughly half the originals. Original evidence strength (P-value and effect size) was the best predictor of replication success, with cognitive psychology replicating at twice the rate of social psychology. Directly relevant to calibrating uncertainty in extracted scientific claims: provides empirical base rates for how much to trust published findings when no independent replication exists.
