## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[4] de Kleer 1986, "An assumption-based TMS"** — The companion paper defining the ATMS itself; essential for understanding the data structures and label-update algorithms referenced throughout this paper.
- **[5] de Kleer 1986, "Extending the ATMS"** — Extends the ATMS with default assumptions, disjunctions, and nonmonotonic justifications; directly relevant to propstore's hypothetical world handling.
- **[9] Doyle 1979, "A truth maintenance system"** — The original TMS paper; provides the baseline that the ATMS improves upon. Understanding Doyle's single-context approach clarifies why the ATMS multi-context approach is superior for assumption-based reasoning.
- **[6] de Kleer & Brown 1984, "A qualitative physics based on confluences"** — Shows the ATMS applied to qualitative reasoning about physical systems; the constraint language in this paper derives from this work.
- **[18] McDermott 1983, "Contexts and data dependencies"** — The most detailed comparison target in Section 6.4; McDermott's single-data-pool approach is the primary alternative to the ATMS's multi-context approach.
