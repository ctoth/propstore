## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Dung 1995 [10]:** The foundational paper defining argumentation frameworks and grounded/preferred/stable/complete semantics. Essential background for everything in propstore's argumentation layer.
- **Baroni, Giacomin, Guida 2005 [4/19]:** The original SCC-recursiveness paper with the full technical development. Important for understanding the decomposition approach used in propstore's ATMS integration.
- **Caminada 2006 [7]:** Introduces semi-stable semantics. Relevant as semi-stable satisfies skepticism adequacy (unlike grounded) while guaranteeing extension existence (unlike stable).
- **Dung, Mancarella, Toni 2007 [15]:** Defines ideal semantics — the unique maximal admissible set contained in all preferred extensions. Relevant to propstore's conservative rendering mode.
- **Coste-Marquis, Devred, Marquis 2005 [6]:** Prudent semantics considering indirect attacks. Relevant as a cautionary example of how considering indirect defeats changes the formal properties.
