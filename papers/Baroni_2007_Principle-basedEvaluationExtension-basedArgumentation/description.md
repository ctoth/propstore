Introduces a systematic principle-based methodology for evaluating extension-based argumentation semantics in Dung's abstract framework, defining formal criteria including conflict-freeness, reinstatement, directionality, skepticism adequacy, and resolution adequacy. Evaluates eight semantics (grounded, complete, preferred, stable, semi-stable, ideal, CF2, prudent) against these criteria, finding that only preferred and stable satisfy all of them. Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer for selecting appropriate semantics per render policy and understanding the formal trade-offs between conservative and liberal extension-selection strategies.
