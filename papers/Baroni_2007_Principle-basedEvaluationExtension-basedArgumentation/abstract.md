# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

The increasing variety of semantics proposed in the context of Dung's theory of argumentation makes more and more inadequate the example-based approach commonly adopted for evaluating and comparing different semantics. To fill this gap, this paper provides two main contributions. First, a set of general criteria for semantics evaluation is introduced by proposing a formal treatment of some intuitive properties which can be considered as desirable for any argumentation semantics. Second, the proposed criteria are applied in a systematic way to a representative set of argumentation semantics available in the literature, namely grounded, complete, preferred, stable, semi-stable, ideal, prudent, and CF2 semantics.

---

## Our Interpretation

This paper establishes the first systematic, principle-based methodology for comparing argumentation semantics within Dung's abstract framework. It defines formal criteria (conflict-free, reinstatement, directionality, skepticism adequacy, resolution adequacy) and evaluates eight semantics against them, finding that preferred and stable are the only two satisfying all criteria. The results are directly relevant to selecting appropriate semantics for propstore's argumentation and render layers.
