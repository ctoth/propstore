Introduces QuAD (Quantitative Argumentation Debate) frameworks and the DF-QuAD algorithm, a discontinuity-free strength aggregation function for bipolar argumentation with base scores. Proves key properties including balance, monotonicity, reinforcement, and continuity, and demonstrates that the original QuAD algorithm suffers from discontinuities at the attacker-supporter equality boundary. Directly grounds the propstore DF-QuAD implementation and clarifies the distinction between intrinsic base scores and probabilistic acceptance.
