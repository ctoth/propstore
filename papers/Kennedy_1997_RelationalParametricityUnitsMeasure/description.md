Formalizes units of measure as a System-F-style polymorphic type discipline and proves a relational-parametricity theorem for unit scaling. Shows that unit-polymorphic types imply concrete scaling laws, type inhabitation criteria, and a typed analogue of the Pi theorem from dimensional analysis. Directly informs bridgman's next layer beyond boolean dimension checks: proof-carrying scale invariance, Pi-basis witnesses, and propstore equation diagnostics.
