This paper analyzes large-scale owl:sameAs deployment in Linked Data by constructing ESameNet from BTC 2010 and measuring SameAs, pay-level-domain, and class-level similarity networks.
It finds that SameAs networks are mostly small, sparse, and star-like, while PLD and CLS abstractions expose publisher communities, topic overlap, ontology-alignment candidates, and possible schema-level errors.
It is relevant to this project because it treats identity links as source-sensitive graph evidence whose topology, provenance, and schema consequences should be modeled explicitly rather than collapsed into unconditional global equality.
