McCarthy introduces contexts as first-class formal objects in logic via the relation ist(c, p), defines lifting rules for cross-context reasoning, and proposes transcendence as the mechanism for expanding limited axiomatizations. The paper formalizes nonmonotonic inheritance between contexts via abnormality predicates and develops the concept of relative decontextualization for finding common ground between multiple contexts. Directly relevant to propstore's context-qualified claim model and ATMS-based assumption management.
