Demonstrates how Josang's subjective logic can serve as a complementary tool to meta-analysis by encoding individual empirical findings as binomial subjective opinions and fusing them via Weighted Belief Fusion, explicitly preserving second-order uncertainty from factors like sample size, replication status, and peer review. Applied to 67 findings from an infant prosociality meta-analysis, the approach reveals heterogeneity and residual uncertainty masked by traditional pooled effect sizes. Directly relevant to propstore's opinion algebra and calibration pipeline as a worked example of multi-source uncertainty decomposition and transparent fusion.
