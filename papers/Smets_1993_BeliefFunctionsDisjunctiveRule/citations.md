## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Pearl 1990 [34]** ("Reasoning with belief functions: an analysis of compatibility", IJAR 4:363-390) — Pearl's compatibility critique of DS theory; Smets explicitly says GBT/DRC dissolve these counterexamples. Reading both Pearl's attack and Smets' response is essential before any propstore implementation that mixes Bayes-style and belief-function reasoning.
- **Smets 1992 [35]** ("Resolving misunderstandings about belief functions: A response to the many criticisms raised by J. Pearl", IJAR 6:321-344) — direct extended reply; useful diagnostic of common DS misuses.
- **Halpern & Fagin 1990 [28]** ("Two views of belief: Belief as generalized probability and belief as evidence", AAAI'90) — alternative belief-function semantics; clarifies why GBT is the right primitive vs Halpern-Fagin formulation.
- **Kennes & Smets 1990 [33]** ("Computational aspects of the Mobius transform", UAI'90) — Fast Mobius Transform; mandatory if propstore needs efficient bel <-> m <-> q conversions.
- **Smets 1978 thesis [6]** — deepest reference; full proofs of DRC/GBT via minimal commitment. Hard-to-obtain doctoral dissertation but the canonical source.
