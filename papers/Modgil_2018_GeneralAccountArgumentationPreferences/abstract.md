# Abstract

## Original Text (Verbatim)

This paper builds on the recent ASPIC+ formalism to develop a general framework for argumentation with preferences. We motivate a revised definition of conflict free sets of arguments, shall ASPIC+ to accommodate a broader range of instantiating logics, and show that under some assumptions, the resulting framework satisfies key properties and rationality postulates. We then show that the framework subsumes Brewka's approach to argumentation based on classical logic with preferences, and then study instantiations of the framework by classical and other logic approaches to argumentation. We conclude by arguing that ASPIC+'s modelling of defeasible inference rules further testifies to the generality of the framework, and then examine and counter recent critiques of Dung's framework and its extensions to accommodate preferences.

---

## Our Interpretation

The paper extends ASPIC+ into a comprehensive framework for structured argumentation with preferences, addressing formal gaps and criticisms of prior work. Its central technical contribution is replacing defeat-based conflict-free with the stricter attack-based conflict-free definition, which enables rationality postulates to hold more broadly. By proving correspondences with classical logic (Brewka's preferred subtheories) and abstract logic instantiations, the paper establishes ASPIC+ as a unifying framework for preference-based argumentation.
