This paper builds on the ASPIC+ framework to develop a general account of structured argumentation with preferences, revising the conflict-free definition from defeat-based to attack-based and proving that four key rationality postulates (sub-argument closure, consistency, closure under strict rules) hold under this revision with reasonable argument orderings. The main formal results show that ASPIC+ generalizes to classical logic instantiations (where stable extensions correspond to Brewka's preferred subtheories) and to abstract logics, while systematically rebutting critiques that the framework conflates preference-dependent and preference-independent attacks. Directly foundational for the propstore's conflict resolution and belief revision architecture, providing formal preference orderings (last-link, weakest-link, Elitist, Democratic) for resolving conflicts between claims of varying strength and provenance.
