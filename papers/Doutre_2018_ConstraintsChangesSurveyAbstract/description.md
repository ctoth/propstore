Surveys approaches for dynamically enforcing constraints on abstract argumentation frameworks, classifying them by constraint type (structural, semantic, acceptability), change type (structural, semantic), and quality criteria (minimality, postulates). The paper provides a unified typology across 20+ existing approaches and identifies underexplored areas including semantic change quality and dynamics in ranking-based semantics. Directly relevant to propstore's argumentation layer for understanding how to modify AFs at render time to enforce policy constraints while minimizing disruption.
