A formal modification of the Halpern-Pearl actual-causality definition that restricts AC2 contingencies to variables held at their actual values.
It simplifies the definition, handles standard preemption and overdetermination counterexamples more cleanly, and lowers the general causality decision problem to D_1^P-complete.
Relevant to propstore's causal semantics because it gives a typed witness discipline for structural-equation actual-cause queries and distinguishes causes from parts of causes.
