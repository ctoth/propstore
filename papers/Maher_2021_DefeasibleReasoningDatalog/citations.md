## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Maher & Governatori 1999** — "A semantic decomposition of defeasible logics" — The foundational metaprogram approach that this paper extends. Essential for understanding the base construction.

2. **Antoniou et al. 2001** — "Representation results for defeasible logic" — Establishes the formal framework for the D(1,1) logic variant used throughout. Key for understanding the inference rules.

3. **Swift & Warren 2012** — "XSB: extending Prolog with tabled logic programming" — The primary implementation target for executing compiled defeasible theories under well-founded semantics.

4. **Caminada & Amgoud 2007** — "On the evaluation of argumentation formalisms" — Bridge between defeasible logic and ASPIC+ argumentation, directly relevant to propstore's architecture.

5. **Maher 2012** — "Relative expressiveness of defeasible logics" — Defines the parameterized family D(d,a) from which D(1,1) is drawn. Essential for understanding what other variants the metaprogram approach could cover.
