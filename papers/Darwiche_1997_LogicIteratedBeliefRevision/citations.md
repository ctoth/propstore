## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Spohn [23]** — Ordinal conditional functions: the mathematical foundation for the bullet operator and (mu,m)-conditionalization. Essential for implementing ranking-based revision in propstore.
- **Boutilier [4]** — Iterated revision and minimal revision of conditional beliefs. The "natural revision" approach that Darwiche-Pearl improves upon; important for understanding what absolute minimization (CB) does wrong.
- **Katsuno and Mendelzon [17]** — The KM representation theorem that this paper extends to epistemic states. Already foundational for understanding AGM in propositional terms.
- **Gardenfors [8]** — Knowledge in Flux. The canonical AGM reference; already partially processed in propstore as Gardenfors_1988.
- **Goldszmidt and Pearl [13]** — Qualitative probabilities for default reasoning, belief revision, and causal modeling. Connects ranking functions to probabilistic reasoning and causal models.
