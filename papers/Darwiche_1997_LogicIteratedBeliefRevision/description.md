Proposes four postulates (C1-C4) beyond AGM to regulate iterated belief revision, showing that AGM's one-step postulates fail to constrain how conditional beliefs and revision strategies transform across sequences of observations. Provides representation theorems linking the postulates to pre-order preservation conditions (CR1-CR4) and constructs a concrete satisfying operator based on Spohn's ordinal conditional functions. Directly relevant to propstore's treatment of sequential claim updates as iterated revisions on epistemic states rather than belief sets.
