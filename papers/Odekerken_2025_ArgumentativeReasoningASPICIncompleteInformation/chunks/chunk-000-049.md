## Key Findings
- The JAIR paper turns stability and relevance into first-class structured-argumentation tasks for ASPIC+ under incomplete information. *(p.1-3)*
- It uses four grounded statuses: `unsatisfiable`, `defended`, `out`, and `blocked`. *(p.8, p.16)*
- Stability is coNP-complete and relevance is `Sigma_2^P`-complete for all four statuses. *(p.17-20)*
- Exact ASP-based algorithms are practical on the inquiry benchmark and competitive with a prior approximate stability method. *(p.29-34)*
- Structured notions do not reduce cleanly to the corresponding incomplete abstract-AF notions. *(p.35-40)*

## Equations Found (LaTeX)
- `l` is `j`-stable iff every future theory gives `l` status `j`. *(p.8-10)*
- `q` is `j`-relevant iff it occurs in some minimal stable future theory for `l`. *(p.9-10)*
- Grounded rule defense is characterized by the least fixed point of the defense operator. *(p.14-16)*
