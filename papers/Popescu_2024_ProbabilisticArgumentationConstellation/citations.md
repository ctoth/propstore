## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fazzinga, Flesca, Furfaro 2019, 2020** — Foundational complexity results for probabilistic AFs under the constellation approach. These papers establish the FP^{#P}-completeness results that Popescu & Wallner refine. Essential for understanding the baseline complexity landscape.

2. **Fichte, Hecher, Meier, Woltran 2018, 2022** — Weighted model counting and tree-decomposition DP for abstract argumentation. These papers provide the broader algorithmic framework that Popescu & Wallner adapt for probabilistic AFs. Key for understanding how tree-decomposition DP works for argumentation generally.

3. **Li, Oren, Norman 2011** — Introduces probabilistic argumentation frameworks (PAFs) under the constellation approach. The semantic foundation for everything in this paper.

4. **Dvorák, Pichler, Woltran 2012** — Fixed-parameter tractability results for abstract argumentation. Provides the theoretical grounding for why treewidth-based approaches work for AF problems.

5. **Hunter, Thimm 2017** — Probabilistic reasoning with abstract argumentation frameworks. Alternative approach to probabilistic argumentation that may offer complementary techniques.
