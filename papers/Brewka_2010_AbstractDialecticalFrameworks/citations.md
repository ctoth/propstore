## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Brewka, Ellmauthaler, Strass, Wallner, Woltran (2013)** — The full journal-length treatment of ADFs with complete technical details. Essential follow-up for implementation.
- **Cayrol and Lagasquie-Schiex (2005)** — Bipolar argumentation frameworks, the most direct predecessor to ADFs. Already relevant to propstore's support modeling.
- **Prakken (2010)** — ASPIC+ structured argumentation framework. Understanding how ADFs and ASPIC+ relate is critical for propstore's argumentation layer.
- **Gordon, Prakken, and Walton (2007)** — Carneades model with burden of proof. Directly relevant to the weighted ADF / legal proof standards contribution.
- **Egly, Gaggl, and Woltran (2010)** — ASP encodings for argumentation. Potential implementation pathway for ADF computation.
