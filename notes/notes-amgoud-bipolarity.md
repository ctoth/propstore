## OBSERVATIONS SO FAR (pages 1-12 read)
- Title page: Amgoud, Cayrol, Lagasquie-Schiex (IRIT Toulouse), P. Livet (CEPERC Aix-en-Provence)
- Abstract: Survey of bipolarity in argumentation frameworks. Uses Dubois & Prade nomenclature. Shows bipolarity appears in all 5 steps of argumentation process.
- Intro (p.3): Three types of bipolarity per Dubois & Prade: (1) uni-variate (single scale), (2) bi-variate (two independent scales for positive/negative), (3) heterogeneous (both features, minimal consistency between them)
- Section 2 (p.4): 5-step argumentation process: building arguments, building interactions, valuating, selecting, concluding. Dung's framework recalled.
- Section 3 (p.6-8): Bipolarity at argument level. Two kinds of arguments: for/against a conclusion. Explanatory arguments defined. Arguments for/against a decision defined. Bipolarity = type 2 (bi-variate) with exclusivity property.
- Section 4 (p.8-12): Bipolarity at interaction level. Key contribution: bipolar argumentation framework = (A, R_def, R_sup) where R_def is defeat, R_sup is support. Introduces direct/indirect defenders and supporters. Defines defeat and support relations formally. Bipolar graph notation.
- Definition 4 (p.11): Bipolar argumentation framework formal definition with paths, direct/indirect defenders/supporters
- Definition 5 (p.11): Direct/Indirect Defenders/Defeaters of an argument
- Definition 6 (p.12): Direct/Indirect Supporters of an argument
- Definition 7 (p.12): Defeat relations (trivial relations)
- Definition 8 (p.12): Support relations (explanation support)
