## Tier 1 — we are implementing math against the wrong reference

### Jøsang, A. (2016). *Subjective Logic: A Formalism for Reasoning Under Uncertainty*. Springer.
- Why: cluster F flagged `wbf()` is algebraically aCBF, `decision_criterion="pignistic"` uses Jøsang's projected-probability formula. We have Jøsang_2001 and Jøsang_2010 (early papers); the 2016 book is the canonical, exhaustive operator catalog with consistent naming.
- Unblocks: half of cluster F's HIGH math fixes; subjective-logic operator-name audit.
- Type: book.
- Format priority: PDF if available; otherwise purchase.

### Smets, P. & Kennes, R. (1994). *The transferable belief model*. Artificial Intelligence 66(2):191–234.
- Why: real source of "pignistic transformation" (BetP). propstore currently mis-credits to Denoeux.
- Unblocks: cluster F / U pignistic naming dispute in one shot.
- Type: paper.

### Cuzzolin, F. (2021). *The Geometry of Uncertainty: The Geometry of Imprecise Probabilities*. Springer.
- Why: belief-function geometry — operator algebra for combining/discounting opinions; lets cluster F's missing operators (Brier, ECE, Sobol-on-opinions) be implemented from one consistent formalism.
- Unblocks: cluster F missing-features.
- Type: book. Earlier Cuzzolin survey papers acceptable substitute.

## Tier 2 — load-bearing for active gaps

### Lehmann, D. & Magidor, M. (1992). *What does a conditional knowledge base entail?*. AI 55.

- Unblocks: cluster B DeLP completion.
- Type: paper.

- Unblocks: if cluster J implements actual cause, target HP-modified.
- Type: paper.
